# The Lord God Space

**The Lord God Space**

*(The page opens with a short scene from the novel: Zheng Zha wakes dazed on a vast, empty platform lit by an enormous sphere of light like a sun, surrounded on all sides by a darkness so total that just looking at it makes his head swim.)*

The Lord God Space is where samsara travelers rest and recuperate — their only home. It is a huge white platform ringed by 20 doors. When a samsara traveler opens an unused door, that door and the room behind it belong to them until they die. Evidently, one Lord God Space holds at most 20 samsara travelers.

**Personal room rules**

Within the Lord God Space, a character has absolute authority over their personal room. They decide its materials, style, layout, and furnishings — though still within certain limits set by the Lord God.

Each character's total space allowance is 27 cubic kilometers, in any number of rooms. But unless the character designs it themselves and registers it with the Lord God, the Lord God provides only an empty venue.

**Entering a personal space**

The door to a personal space has no lock, but only the owner and humanoid beings they created can open it. No one else can enter unless the owner consents entirely of their own free will. If the owner wishes, they may grant humanoid beings they created the authority to invite or admit others; people outside may request a meeting with those inside via the Lord God. Finally, even when invited, the invitee must come purely voluntarily — not under charm effects or similar domination — or they cannot be brought into the room.

**Forms a personal space can take**

The form of a personal space is specified by the character at creation, but must conform to Earthly common sense and geographic and physical reality. It may be an artificial or natural environment, in any form — a basement, an indoor garden, a stretch of open grassland. For an outdoor natural environment, the Lord God can simulate the sky and distant scenery and produce realistic natural phenomena.

Inside a personal space you may build any facility at Earth's technological level and use equipment consistent with Earth's present day. You could build a shooting range stocked with any firearm that exists or has ever existed on Earth, with moving-target control systems, electronic scoring, and clay-pigeon launchers. You could build a deluxe karaoke hall or an arcade. You can build workshops or studios — but if you want to manufacture items that normally require point-exchange, or mass-produce on an assembly line, you must exchange for the equipment and control facilities. Apart from items exchanged with points, or items made from materials exchanged with points, nothing materialized inside your personal space worth more than 1 point can be taken into the Lord God Space proper or into a film world — it vanishes when you leave. (Ordinary clothes, food, backpacks, and suitcases can usually be taken out; designer clothing, rare delicacies, or a bulletproof alloy case — things that would cost reward points — cannot. Check with your ST on specific items.)

You may also construct natural environments matching Earth's real conditions. Unless you specify otherwise, an outdoor environment's sky is 1 kilometer high (it looks indistinguishable from a real sky, with lightning, rain, snow, and rainbows — you can even use weather-affecting abilities or special skills on your personal space's environment). The Lord God automatically populates such an environment with a reasonable biome, but unless you specify, no animal larger than Size 1 appears. (Ask simply for "a grassland with a river" and you get grasshoppers, voles, and small snakes in the grass, songbirds overhead, and little fish and shrimp in the river. Only when you specify "I want to see galloping horses and cheetahs" do those animals appear.) You may specify any animal, plant, mineral, or other visible organism that exists or has existed on Earth and has no supernatural properties — but if the environment doesn't suit it, it may not survive. Put a South China tiger in a tropical rainforest and it may sicken and die; create a dinosaur and it will likely starve, because you cannot create a Jurassic-grade environment for it.

Plants and animals from these natural environments cannot be taken into the Lord God Space or film worlds — not even as corpses.

If you build an enclosed personal space, such as a cave or interior, its boundary is made of whatever reasonable material you specify. If you build an outdoor space, its boundary is a void of warped spatial interface: try to cross it and you pass through the closed, twisted boundary right back to where you were. Either way, the edge of a personal space is in substance the edge of space itself and cannot be broken or crossed by any method.

**Living in a personal space**

Food, water, and simple daily necessities can be freely created by the owner or their created humanoids — just open the corresponding pantry or cabinet and they appear. The owner can also change guests' permissions to take items.

Food and drink are limited to things that really exist on Earth, though not necessarily "food and drink" in the usual sense — generally, anything digestible, nutritious, and detoxifiable can be provided as food by the Lord God (spiders, ants, or bat rectums, for instance). Unless requested otherwise, food and drink are always maximally fresh; a character can even obtain "fresh meat cut from the leg of a living cow" (though not living things this way). For satisfying special physiologies, skills, and energy needs, however, they still count as preserved goods. A vampire can take human blood from the kitchen cabinet that tastes and pleases exactly like blood drawn fresh from a living person — but for restoring blood energy and triggering blood addiction it still counts as bagged blood. Prepared dishes are likewise available. Luxuries, though (say, "the brandy Napoleon personally bought and buried before Waterloo for his victory feast"), require exchange.

Daily items with modest technological content — clothing, furniture, soap, candles — are also available this way. Luxury goods produced on modern industrial lines can be obtained directly (brand-name clothing, say), but luxuries made by ancient or artisanal craft (a bespoke garment of top materials and world-master tailoring) require exchange. New items are always brand-new, well-made, and clean, but wear, dirty, and deplete normally.

Electronic and technological facilities in the room can be created and arranged freely, so long as they are reasonable by common sense and physics, and they always have ample power. But unlike food and daily goods, electronics and technological products cannot be taken out of the room unless exchanged for.

The room can also generate currency of any era and any country (gold included), plus drugs and contraband of all kinds — none of which can leave the room.

Any information-bearing cultural product — books, films, video games — can be generated, but cannot be taken out of the room either.

Note: no matter what, apart from things bought with points, you cannot take anything from your personal space out into the Lord God Space or a film world.

**Language conversion**

In the Lord God Space, as long as you wear your watch and no one is deliberately jamming it, you understand — without study — any language that could be mastered through study. Likewise, anything you say in any real-world language is converted by the watch into a language the story characters understand. Language conversion follows these special rules:

1. Although speech is converted into language you understand, you still "know" which language the speaker used — one you know, one you don't, or which one you know.

2. Only listening and speaking are converted; you gain no reading or writing ability from this.

3. This is optional: a character may switch the ability off toward samsara-team members or toward story characters, losing both the listening and speaking conversion. Switching it on or off is a free action requiring no action at all.

**Samsara teams**

Based on geography on Earth, people from different regions are summoned into one of the following 15 Lord God Spaces.

Anyone summoned by the Lord God is called a "newcomer" during their first film. Survive the first film, and you become a "samsara traveler".

Samsara travelers in the same Lord God Space are members of the same samsara team.

North Ocean Team: the Arctic, near the Arctic Ocean.
North Ice Team: Western Europe, northern North America.
South Ice Team: near Antarctica.
Central Team: China, Mongolia, East Asia.

North Blaze Team: Eastern Europe, North Africa.
South Blaze Team: South Africa.
East America Team: the United States, Canada, North America.
West America Team: Mexico, Central America.
Indus Team: India, Pakistan, Indian Ocean island nations, West Asia.
North Sea Team: Northern Europe, Russia.
Oceania Team: Australia, Oceania.

East Sea Team: Japan, Indonesia, Southeast Asia, Pacific island nations.
West Sea Team: Atlantic island nations.
Forest Team: Brazil, Argentina, South America.
Sand Team: Egypt, the Arab states, Israel, the Middle East.

Beyond these, two special samsara teams stand above the ordinary ones in power:

Deva Team: whenever anyone in any team attains the rank of captain, they may choose to abandon their team and join the Deva Team. It consists entirely of samsara travelers the Lord God has recognized as captain-qualified.
Demon Team: the Lord God privately creates duplicates of outstanding, high-potential samsara travelers. The team formed of all these duplicate travelers is the Demon Team.

**Fratricide**

The Lord God does not wish to see teammates slaughter each other. Killing a member of your own team costs D+1000 as punishment. If the killing happens in the Lord God Space, the penalty is deducted immediately; if in a film world, it is settled together with rewards when the film ends.

If a PC's assets cannot cover the penalty — whether their Tiers or their reward points fall short — the PC is immediately erased by the Lord God.

Only characters wearing your team's samsara watch count as your teammates in the Lord God's eyes.

**Captaincy**

Since they are called "samsara teams", the position of "captain" must exist.

The captain may not be the strongest member of a team, but is certainly its most important.

Only a team with a captain can learn the plot of the next horror film in advance and prepare accordingly.

And only a team with a captain has the right to truly join the endless battlefield of samsara — fighting other samsara teams until the end of time.

A team may find itself temporarily captainless — perhaps the captain died in battle, perhaps they abandoned the team. Either way, someone must inherit the position.

The Lord God places a guide in captainless teams to select a captain; see "Traveling to and from the Lord God Space".

**Captain's privileges**

A captain gains the following privileges:

1. One free rank of Gene Lock advancement with full proficiency, up to Gene Lock rank 3.

2. On gaining captaincy, the captain qualifies to join the Deva Team. (This usually removes the player from the game, turning the character into an NPC.)

3. One free use of Creating Lifeforms. Gaining captaincy again does not grant this benefit again.

4. On the second day after returning to the Lord God Space, the captain can learn about the next horror film from the Lord God — its title, whether it is a team battle, and so on.

5. Only teams with captains may join team battles. (This rule is ST-optional.)

**Traveling to and from the Lord God Space**

After your first summons by the Lord God, you enter the first film directly, without passing through the Lord God Space.

Alongside the other PCs in your first film there is a guide — a special NPC, usually run by the ST. The guide's duty is to explain the Lord God Space's rules and information to newcomers, providing the first game's tutorial. Guides generally lean toward following the film's original script.

If a PC explains the Lord God Space's rules to the newcomers in the guide's place, that PC earns 100 reward points as tutoring pay.

The guide's other duty is choosing the captain. The captain is chosen only from team members who have opened Gene Lock rank 1 and have been through at least three films. If no member qualifies, the ST may appoint one by force. With multiple candidates, choose the player with the best performance that game — normally the one who earned the most Tiers and points. Once the captain is appointed, the guide vanishes.

If the PC holding captaincy ceases to exist (death, team transfer, or otherwise), a new guide appears and searches the remaining PCs for a suitable captain.

Each time a film begins, all PCs appear inside a shroud of light. Characters in the film cannot perceive the shroud or the PCs inside, while the PCs can watch the outside through it.

The shroud usually lasts 1 to 3 minutes, then shatters soundlessly. Until it shatters, PCs cannot leave the area it covers. The shroud generally cannot be destroyed early.

After the shroud breaks, the film's NPCs feel nothing amiss about the PCs' existence or entrance.

Each time you complete a film, you are transported back to the Lord God Space. There you enjoy 10 days of peace before departing for the next film's adventure.

Without a captain, you cannot learn what the next film will be.

A samsara cycle is normally 20 games — run 20 worlds and the story concludes.

Typically:

Game 1 has the guide — an NPC who explains the Lord God Space's rules and information and handles the tutorial. In later games with newcomers, a PC who explains earns 100 reward points.

Game 3: the team's captain is chosen — only from members who have opened Gene Lock rank 1. If none qualify, the ST may appoint one; with multiple candidates, the best performer of that game (normally most Tiers and points earned) is chosen.

Games 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, and 18 are team battles.

Game 20 is the final battle. (The story's ending.)

**Returning to film worlds**

Alternatively, you may spend Tiers and points to return to a world you have visited — to train, to relax, or to hunt for extra Tier missions.

After a mission ends you may return to the world of your previous mission for free. Or pay one D Tier to open any mission world you have been through before; or pay one A Tier to open a mission world you have never experienced. Each day spent in a story world costs 10 reward points, up to at most one year (365 Earth days). Between two missions you may re-enter a mission world only once. You choose the point in time you enter freely, except that it cannot be earlier than the moment you last left that world.

Note that missions you find inside film worlds usually have their difficulty force-locked to the highest grade.

However long you stay in a world outside the Lord God Space, from the Space's perspective you were gone 24 hours.

Note: the ST may refuse your use of this feature for any reason.

**Team battle rewards**

In a team battle, killing an enemy member earns your team one point of score; each of your own members killed deducts one. Scores settle when the film ends: every positive point grants all surviving teammates 2,000 reward points, and every negative point deducts 2,000 from all teammates.

The killer earns a kill reward: C+2000 if the victim had not opened their Gene Lock, or B+6000 if they had. In addition, tallying all Tiers and points the victim ever earned and spent, the killer receives a Tier one rank below the victim's highest equivalent Tier total, plus one third of their points, as extra reward. (That is, AA = A, SAC = A — no matter how many S, it's A.)

**The Lord God's erasure**

The Lord God Space is not a picnic ground. A samsara traveler is erased by the Lord God when:

The main mission fails — e.g. the mission requires protecting an NPC, and that NPC is killed.

They leave the mission map — e.g. the mission requires operating within a set area, and the PC flees it.

Their assets go negative — e.g. a crushing defeat in a team battle costs them massive Tiers and reward points, or they kill many teammates and are docked heavily.

A PC erased by the Lord God can never be revived, by any means, under any circumstances.

**Self-enhancement**

The Infinite Horror TRPG has three main currencies:

Tiers: Tiers come in 5 ranks — D, C, B, A, S. In most cases an ability priced at C is almost certainly stronger than one priced at D, and a B ability stronger again than a C. Owning a full suite of S-Tier abilities and chaining them into one glorious combo is every samsara traveler's dream. Tiers can be split and combined freely: 3 D make a C, 3 C make a B, and so on — and the reverse split also works, so a C can be broken up to buy 3 different D-Tier resources.

Reward points: also just called points, the most common currency in the Infinite Horror setting. Most resources you buy cost some combination of Tiers and reward points: a high-energy particle pulse rifle priced at D+500 means paying one D Tier and 500 reward points for that expensive future-tech weapon.

Experience: also called XP. XP represents strength gained through tempering within the films. Obviously, XP earned by one person cannot be transferred to another. Nor can XP conjure an ability from nothing — it must build on an existing foundation through "study" or "training". See the "Experience System" and "Self-Taught Abilities" pages.

The Lord God Space not only lets samsara travelers rest after near-death adventures — it provides the means to grow stronger. In the world of Infinite Horror you can exchange for a nearly unlimited variety of abilities, but by type they all fall into one or more of the following base categories (feats, in particular, can be classified under skills and abilities).

Spending Tiers and reward points must, barring special cases, be done in the Lord God Space; exchanging for a resource is a Free Action.

Spending experience can be done directly both in the Lord God Space and inside horror films — though inside a film, barring special cases (e.g. Light War, Human Sovereign), it costs double the normal XP. Learning new abilities with XP requires no action.

**Resource transfers**

Members of the same team may give or trade points and Tiers with one another, notarized by the Lord God. XP, no matter what, cannot be transferred.

**Direct enhancement**

Buying Stats and Skills outright is the simplest, crudest way to get stronger.

Buying 1 Stat point costs (current Stat value × 200) points, or (current Stat value × 2) XP.

Buying 1 Skill level costs (current Skill level − 1) × 2 XP. Specifically, raising a Skill from 0 to 1 costs 3 XP; from 1 to 2 costs 1 XP.

**Bloodlines**

A bloodline is power that comes from inheritance: whoever holds a bloodline, regardless of talent or later effort, finds its basic characteristics hard to change. As one of a character's main templates, a character may have only one bloodline. Stat bonuses from a Bloodline are Intrinsic Bonuses (for bonus definitions see the "Essence and Bonuses" page), which stack rank by rank with themselves but not with Intrinsic Bonuses from other sources. Unless stated otherwise, bloodline prices per rank are D+600 / C+1200 / B+3600 / A+7200 / AA+10800 / S+14400; a bloodline never has both AA and S ranks at once.

All units who exchange for a bloodline count as supernatural beings for checks, unless the bloodline itself says otherwise.

**Alternations**

The power of Alternation comes from transforming the body outside or in. Like Bloodlines, once an Alternation is accepted, personal talent and effort have little effect on its basic characteristics. As one of a character's main templates, a character may have only one Alternation. Stat bonuses from Alternations are Intrinsic Bonuses: they do not stack with a Bloodline's — take the higher — but do stack rank by rank with the Alternation itself. Unless stated otherwise, Alternation prices per rank are D+500 / C+1000 / B+2000 / A+4000 / AA+6000 / S+8000; an Alternation never has both AA and S ranks at once.

All units who exchange for an Alternation count as supernatural beings for checks, unless the Alternation itself says otherwise.

**Eye Techniques**

An Eye Technique is a special ability — think of it as an Alternation of the eyeballs. Its peculiar nature rejects the study of other techniques of its kind: a character generally has one and only one Eye Technique, though certain Alternations or abilities may allow several (heterochromatic pupils, or a beholder bloodline, say). An Eye Technique usually confers some advantage whether active or not, and a greater one when activated. Prices are D+600 / C+1200 / B+2400 / A+3600 / AA+5400 / S+7200; an Eye Technique never has both AA and S ranks at once.

All units who exchange for an Eye Technique count as supernatural beings for checks, unless it says otherwise.

**Cultivation Systems**

As the name suggests, a Cultivation System is one of the many world-specific paths of cultivation. To avoid bursting apart and dying, each person may hold only one Cultivation System. It generally grants various passive effects and can amplify the character's abilities to various degrees.

All units who exchange for a Cultivation System count as supernatural beings for checks, unless it says otherwise.

**Titles**

A title is a skill system mastered through systematic training: complete the same training and you should reap the same reward. A title is a unique ability skill-tree with its own proprietary techniques. It is written much like ordinary abilities, but forms its own system and can keep growing. Unless stated otherwise, title prices per rank are D+1000 / C+2000 / B+4000 / A+8000 / AA+12000 / S+16000; a title never has both AA and S ranks at once. Titles are generally supernatural abilities unless the title says otherwise.

**Energy pools**

Energy is a way of wielding and acquiring power. Unless stated otherwise, energies are not mutually exclusive — you can cultivate several kinds.

When you gain an energy type, you gain its operating mechanism along with it (which key Attribute it uses, how it recovers and replenishes, whether it has basic uses, and so on).

Unless stated otherwise, other abilities do not interfere with this mechanism — they supplement it.

For example, generic mana recovers through meditation, and there is also a Meditation feat — but these are two entirely different abilities. A character may recover mana via the meditation built into mana's recovery mechanism, or via the Meditation feat; the two don't interfere, and you may not declare that time spent using the Meditation feat also counts as mana-mechanism meditation.

All energies are supernatural abilities unless stated otherwise.

If an Energy Pool ability grants Stat bonuses, they are Practicing Bonuses unless stated otherwise.

**Arts**

Most technique-based abilities fall here. Unless stated otherwise, all bonuses provided by Arts are Technique Bonuses and do not stack, even when several Arts improve the same capability (one inner method might continuously raise your defense while a protective aura concentrates energy for defense when you're attacked — both give defense, but they may not stack). The defining trait of an Art is that the character must actively use it rather than it passively applying. Arts include Techniques, inner methods, movement arts, protective auras, and skills; these can be activated separately, but you cannot activate multiple Techniques using the same action within that one action. Arts are generally supernatural abilities unless the Art says otherwise.

**Items**

Items are the easiest to understand: from a palm-sized dagger to a Gundam-scale ship-cleaving blade, nearly every object that does or doesn't exist in reality can be created.

**Item modifications**

Enhancement and modification of items, plus all manner of plugins and special-material crafting, belong here. Unless specified, only item modifications that grant the same (or opposing) property exclude each other (you cannot turn a magic weapon into a magitech device, because both grant the **[Magical]** property).

**Vehicles**

A kind of item; beyond having a few more properties there's little special to say. As simple as a bicycle, as complex as an EVANGELION — if you have the money, you can get it.

Of the categories above, Bloodline, Alternation, Eye Technique, and Cultivation are mutually exclusive slots: you may exchange for only one of each, and thereafter may only upgrade it, never buy another exchange of the same category. These are called "template abilities".

For example, a character with a werewolf Bloodline cannot also buy a vampire Bloodline; a character with the Sharingan Eye Technique cannot also have the Byakugan; a character with a cybernetic Alternation cannot also become a mutant through a genetic Alternation.

If a character exchanges for an ability that costs them a template slot, they likewise cannot buy another ability that would cost the same slot. The same goes for abilities that remove your Gene Lock. (But abilities like spellcasting bans, grimoire bans, or counting as non-human can be exchanged for in multiples without conflict, since they don't touch your template slots.)
For example, a character with the King in Yellow Bloodline cannot take the Baisaindu Alternation, because the King in Yellow already costs the Eye Technique slot and Baisaindu would cost it again.

**Healing, revival, and respec**

After a vicious battle — an arm and a leg gone, grievous wounds, dead teammates — or simply when you regret your exchanges, you can heal yourself, revive teammates, and re-allocate your build in the Lord God Space. For prices and rules, see "Special Exchanges".
