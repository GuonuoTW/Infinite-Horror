# Core Setting

"Do you want to understand the meaning of life? Do you want to… truly live?"

Welcome to the Lord God Space. Welcome to the game world of Infinite Horror.

As a TRPG, Infinite Horror's setting differs greatly from similar games. Those who come here learn the truth of the world, and fight hard in order to "truly live".

Those who arrive in the Lord God Space are collectively called "samsara travelers". A samsara traveler might be a hot-blooded youth, a fiery beauty, a disabled person of unbreakable will, or even an elder in their sixties. Their ages and social standings vary, but they always share one thing: a deep confusion — even despair — about their own future.

In this place you can cast off rotten social conventions and a boring past, and become whoever you most want to be. Whether you choose to be the hero who slays the dragon or the demon of purgatory, an enlightened master riding a flying sword or a wild brute who strikes the ox through the mountain — it all turns on a single thought.

The rulebook then illustrates play with an excerpt from an actual session log (*"Storming the Condor Shooting World"*, edited and polished): the ST describes the party waking on a huge martial arts stage in a wuxia town — tattered banners advertising a martial tournament for a bride's hand, a three-storied tavern named "Jianghu" with a suspiciously corporate-sounding branch-name plaque — and a yawning, short middle-aged clerk registering newcomers at a small desk. The players, controlling characters pulled from different worlds (a certain Long Aotian among them, entirely unfazed because dimension-hopping is routine where he comes from), size each other up, hop off the stage, and start interrogating the clerk, who grumbles that he's just the unlucky registrar for "the chosen" — newcomers periodically delivered here from other worlds — and doesn't even get paid.

As in every adventure game, combat is an indispensable part of play.

A second session-log excerpt (*"Land of the Buried-Bone Seal 1"*, edited and polished) demonstrates a fight: the party is surrounded by eleven soldier-thugs; one PC raises his hands while another declares a surprise round, spends a standard action to activate his twin swords' weapon skill "Wolf Soul", and with free actions commands the two summoned netherworld wolves to grapple the nearest thugs — hit checks, grapple contests, initiative rolls, and the action-economy of standard/move/swift actions all playing out in dialogue between players and ST.

The world of Infinite Horror revolves around the Lord God Space — an infinitely wide white platform lit by a single colossal sphere of light. At intervals — usually every 10 days — all samsara travelers depart from the Lord God Space and enter worlds modeled on films, games, and anime, to adventure and survive. Survive, and you grow stronger; die, and… no one will even collect your corpse. You might trade quips with Captain Jack Sparrow in the world of Pirates of the Caribbean, or take it into your head to bathe in Mount Doom in the world of The Lord of the Rings. You could even enter the world of The Legend of the Condor Heroes, kill Yin Zhiping ahead of schedule, and steal Xiaolongnü for yourself. Every action you take can create countless different endings.
