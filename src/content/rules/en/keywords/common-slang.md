# Common Slang

**Common slang:**

**Blank card (白卡):** a character card built under the standard card-building rules, with no resources or enhancements of any kind (except those obtained through special identity feats).

**Roleplay (扮演):** also called RP — the practice of a PC bringing their character vividly to life, including but not limited to keeping speech and behavior consistent with the character card's concept. Barring special backstory or hidden circumstances, a five-year-old girl who swears like Lu Zhishen and brawls her way down the street will generally not be considered good roleplay.

**Burning Willpower (爆意志):** normally means paying 1 or more points of Willpower to use Willpower's basic application or another of its uses.

**Che (车):** a verb with two usages. As in "che a card" (车卡), it means building a character card. As in "che that monster over" (or similar), it means defeating, killing, and so on — steamrolling it.

**House rules (房规):** some STs, unsatisfied with the rules in this book, add new rules or modify existing ones in the campaigns they run — such rules are called house rules. House rules cover a wide range: "everyone starts with D+1000 to buy resources on top of blank-card creation", or "all crafting abilities are banned this campaign", or "only Eastern immortal-hero style abilities and items may appear in this campaign". In matters of priority, house rules > rulebook rules. A set of house rules applies only to one campaign by one ST — even if that ST wants the same house rules in another campaign, they must be declared anew.

**Pink (粉红):** this keyword is generally tied to erotic content. Pink resources, pink campaigns, pink PCs — the pattern extends.

**Pigeon / gu (鸽子/咕):** derived from "releasing pigeons" — standing people up. PCs or STs who habitually no-show get called pigeons.

**Campaign wrap (结团):** the story of this Lord God Space is complete; all participating character cards have finished their stories.

**Unlocking (开锁):** in most cases, making a Gene Lock check. Occasionally the literal meaning: a lockpicking (theft) check.

**Kouhu (口胡):** when the rulebook doesn't cover a situation or its wording is ambiguous, twisting what the rulebook says toward one's own advantage is called *kouhu* — rules-twisting.

**Coasting (摸鱼):** in other games this might be called "fringe observing". Its definition: being present in the game but taking no operations, no actions, and doing no roleplay. Note that coasting and the Sin of Sloth are not, were never, and will never be the same concept.

**save:** exactly as it reads — "save your progress". Online tabletop play is very slow; one film can take 10 to 40 hours, spread over many days, so a "save" is made at the end of each day's portion. After the ST declares a save, subsequent operations and actions are not recorded as part of the session and do not affect the plot — and the ST likewise has no obligation to respond to them.

**ST ruling (心判):** where the rulebook doesn't cover something or describe it in detail, the outcomes of some events and actions require the ST's judgment based on the scene — this is called an ST ruling. It includes but is not limited to calls like "does this move count as fleeing combat?" and "does this act count as just?".
