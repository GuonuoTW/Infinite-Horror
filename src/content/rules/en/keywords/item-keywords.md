# Item Inscriptions

**Item inscription list:**

This list covers the specialized terms for the basic and special properties carried by weapons, items, armor, and protective gear.

These inscriptions appear not only on items — they show up on other resources too.

Example: a character uses no weapon, but their natural weapon attacks carry the **[Magical]** property.

Example: a character wears no armor, but their flesh has the **[Bulletproof]** property.

In such cases, the inscription explanations on this page still apply.

**[High Speed X]**: also called the high-velocity advantage. A target attacked by this property suffers a penalty equal to the property's value on Base Defense + Evade Defense + Guard Defense, reduced to a minimum of 0 — but **[High Speed]** cannot break through the shield-defense portion of Guard Defense. The order of reduction is Guard Defense → Evade Defense → Base Defense. Against an area attack with the **[High Speed]** property, the Reflex check also suffers a penalty of the corresponding value. Generally, most crossbows have **[High Speed 2]**, firearms **[High Speed 5]**, Gauss weapons **[High Speed 6]**, and ranged energy weapons **[High Speed 8]**.

If one attack carries multiple **[High Speed]** properties: those worded "gain X points of High Speed" stack; those worded "obtain X High Speed" do not.

**[Breach X]**: a weapon with an Breach value reduces, on the attack, an equal amount of the defender's armor bonus, Shield Defense bonus, and Born Defense — but does not break other bonus types granted by armor, such as deflection or force-field bonuses. The general order is Shield Defense, armor bonus, Born Defense.

Generally, any Breach value exceeding the sum of the target's shield, armor, and Born Defense is wasted.

If Breach reduces any armor, shield, or flesh defense to 0, then the **[Bulletproof]**, **[Energyproof]**, **[Sealed]**, and "super-protection" properties granted by that armor, shield, or flesh have no effect against this attack.

When an area attack carries Breach: if the target's Reflex save includes natural, armor, or Shield Defense, you may break those defenses as normal.

Additionally, a weapon with Breach, when attacking objects, buildings, and similar targets, reduces their hardness by its Breach value.

Also, when attacking a non-object target (generally a creature), once Breach has fully broken the target's Born Defense, any remainder may reduce the target's hardness by the leftover value.

If one attack carries multiple **[Breach]** properties: those worded "gain X Breach" stack; those worded "obtain X Breach" do not.

Special: the Breach of an attack dealing force damage can break force-field defense bonuses.

**[Dispelling X]**: just as Breach bypasses physical armor, Dispelling ignores a number of points of defense granted by supernatural effects — including but not limited to the armor bonus of Inert Armor, the shelter bonus of Guarding Slime, and the environmental bonus of Nitroflame Shield. Magic piercing cannot reduce armor or Shield Defense from physical armor and shields regardless of their essence — though spell-armor and spell-shields constructed of magic can be broken. Nor can it reduce Born Defense; the character's own base, dodge, parry, and Insight Defense from their actions; or cover and environmental defense granted by natural-essence cover or surroundings.

When an area attack carries Dispelling: if the target's Reflex save includes defense granted by supernatural effects, you may break those defenses as normal.

If one attack carries multiple **[Dispelling]** properties: those worded "gain X" stack; those worded "obtain X" do not.

Note: if the target has several effects that **[Dispelling]** could affect, the attacker chooses the order.

Note: if Breach and Dispelling can both apply to one protective effect, they stack. But when armor/Dispelling could apply to several places including the same protective effect, the attacker decides how to distribute them.

**[Light Throwing Weapon]**: this weapon suits throwing — it grants a 1 DP equipment bonus on throwing attacks.

**[Heavy Throwing Weapon]**: throwing attacks with this weapon use Strength as the key Attribute.

**[Concealed Weapon]**: a concealed weapon is necessarily a light thrown weapon. When attacked with one, the target must make a **Perception + Investigation** check at DC 1; failing it, the target counts as flat-footed — this is No-Tier flat-footedness.

**[Flexible Weapon]**: when making grapple contests or trips while wielding this weapon, you may substitute the Blade skill for Brawl. It can entangle objects, and the weapon's reach equals twice the reach computed from its Size.

**[Barehand Weapon]**: these weapons are usually fist-wraps, or sheathes over natural weapons. Attacks with them use Brawl checks rather than Blade checks, and their effects stack with the natural weapon's — duplicates take the higher, conflicts resolve in the natural weapon's favor. A barehand weapon does not count as a blade weapon: skills, feats, or Techniques that require Blade to activate cannot be used with it.

When a barehand weapon receives any modification or enchantment that increases weapon damage, the damage-increasing effect is ignored.

**[Polearm Weapon]**: attacks with this weapon have reach equal to twice the reach computed from its Size.

**[Explosive]**: explosives generally burst on activation, their contents flying everywhere and affecting creatures in the area.

Unless stated otherwise, all Tech-category explosives — grenades, C4, missiles, rocket launchers, and so on — gain the High Speed 5 property.

**[EMP Field]**: a single-target EMP field affects only its target; one with an area affects everything within.

An EMP field affects all items of the Tech category.

Armor and shields lose all their properties, keeping only base and special statistics.

Weapons and mechanical vehicles are immediately paralyzed and unusable.

Items classified as "devices", "systems", or "plugins" lose all properties.

An EMP field only affects items up to one rank above its own Tier. (A No-Tier EMP field affects at most D-Tier items; if the field's Tier is unstated, use its purchase Tier or the creature's Tier.)

**[Defense A/B]**: the protective gear has different defense systems against melee and ranged attacks. Against melee attacks use the value before the slash (A); against ranged attacks, the value after it (B).

**[Armor Penalty X]**: the armor penalty represents how the armor hampers and restricts movement. It applies to Base Defense and base speed (against abilities like base-speed doubling, the penalty applies before the doubling). It also applies to certain Physical checks.

In any case, an armor penalty will not reduce your Base Defense below 0, nor your base speed to a negative number.

**[Bulletproof]**: damage from solid-projectile firearms is downgraded one grade against this (to a minimum of Blunt). If the bearer has no corresponding armor/shield/Born Defense (bulletproof flesh with no Born Defense, say), an attacker with even 1 point of Breach punches through the **[Bulletproof]** property.

**[Energyproof]**: energy-weapon attacks cannot convert damage to Aggravated against this. If the bearer has no corresponding armor/shield/Born Defense, an attacker with even 1 point of Breach punches through the **[Energyproof]** property.

**[Sealed X]**: armor with this property encases the wearer completely, protecting against area damage. When the wearer takes any area damage, they may negate points equal to the property's value. Moreover, any area damage whose value is below the armor bonus plus the Sealed value is downgraded one grade, as though affected by "Bulletproof" or "Energy-Resistant".

If a creature has this property innately, its cuticle encases it completely — in that case read the armor bonus above as Born Defense.

**[Tri-Protection]**: proof against high pressure, space, and temperature extremes. Armor with this property encases the wearer completely. The wearer is immune to damage from water pressure and effects of altitude, immune to the effects of vacuum and radiation, and immune to the effects of temperature change.

**[Spell Resistance X]**: armor with this property shields the wearer against the impact of spells. When the wearer is subjected to any spell effect (including but not limited to daoist arts and magic), the casting check loses successes equal to the spell-resistance value.

**[Penetrating]**: after successfully damaging one target, you may immediately make another attack check against a further target on the same straight line; that attack's DP is reduced by the damage dealt by the previous attack, continuing until no suitable target remains or one check deals no remaining damage. If the weapon also has **[Tracking]**, that property applies only to the first target.

**[Super Penetrating]**: like **[Penetrating]**, but its attacks ignore obstacles along the trajectory whose hardness is below its Breach value, dealing normal damage to them, and it takes none of the normal **[Penetrating]** penalties when attacking targets. If an object or character on the trajectory has hardness above its Breach value, treat it as an ordinary **[Penetrating]** attack from there on. It also ignores cover bonuses up to its Breach value.

**[Energy Weapon]**: ranged energy weapons have the **[High Speed 8]** property. Attacks with these weapons may convert up to half the weapon's damage value into Aggravated damage. Unless stated otherwise, energy weapons deal pure energy damage.

**[Magical]**: attacks with this property can pierce the corresponding damage reduction, and can to a degree harm spirit and incorporeal creatures. When a weapon with this property attacks a spirit or incorporeal creature, roll a d10: on 6–10, it deals damage to the creature normally. Items and equipment with this property still work in films where technology fails.

**[Spiritual]**: items with this property directly affect and damage spirit and incorporeal creatures, and the final damage is doubled.

**[Holy]**: also called **[Light]**. Damage this weapon deals to creatures of darkness is upgraded one grade, up to an amount not exceeding the weapon's damage.

**[Unholy]**: also called **[Dark]**. Damage this weapon deals to creatures of light is upgraded one grade, up to an amount not exceeding the weapon's damage.

**[Stun]**: through sheer impact, if this attack's damage exceeds the target's Size or Stamina (whichever is higher), the target gains stun points equal to the weapon damage. Fortitude save; does not stack.

**[Knockback]**: these weapons deliver such tremendous impact that they hurl opponents away: each point of damage exceeding the higher of the target's Size and Stamina knocks the target back one meter, direction chosen by the attacker. The knocked-back target must pass a Reflex check with difficulty equal to the knockback distance (in meters) or fall prone.

**[Mighty X]**: just as Breach bypasses physical armor, Mighty ignores a number of points of defense granted by guarding — including but not limited to Guard Defense, Shield Defense, the Field Bonus of Aurora Shield, and the Feat Bonus of Dragonslayer Knight. Mighty cannot reduce Armor Defense or Born Defense; the character's own Base, Evade, or Insight Defense not derived from guarding; or Covered and Environmental Defense from Natural-category cover or surroundings.

When an area attack carries Mighty: if the target's Reflex Save includes bonuses based on guarding or shields, you may reduce those bonuses as normal.

If Mighty reduces all guard-sourced defense to 0, the guard's other effects are also nullified for this attack, as though no guard had been made.

If one attack carries multiple **[Mighty X]** properties: those worded "gain X" stack; those worded "obtain X" do not.

When Mighty carries no value, its value defaults to half the weapon's Size (rounded down).

Note: Mighty stacks with armor/Dispelling when both can apply to one protective effect. When they could apply to several places including the same protective effect, the attacker decides the distribution.

**[Loyalty]**: after a ranged throwing attack, this weapon automatically retrieves itself, appearing in the user's hand as the attack action ends.

**[Guard X]**: when guarding with this weapon, the character gains X points of shield bonus, added along with the guard bonus to the defense from guarding. A character can benefit from only one weapon with the Guard property.

When Parrying carries no value, its value defaults to the weapon's Size.

**[Explosive Projectile]**: if a target attacked by a weapon with this property has Armor Defense + Born Defense (after Breach) lower than the bolter weapon's damage value, an amount of the damage dealt equal to half the weapon's Size converts to Aggravated.

**[Bombardment]**: bombardment is an area attack on a target zone; everyone within makes a Reflex save. The area's diameter is the gun's Size, and the area damage equals the weapon damage. Make a normal cannon attack check with no weapon damage; it strips all the target's defenses except base, dodge, insight, and parry, and your Breach applies as normal to armor, natural, and Shield Defense. Each success on the target's Reflex save prevents 1 damage; the target suffers a penalty equal to your successes and also takes the high-velocity penalty.

Because bombardment strips part of each target's defenses, make a separate check for each target attacked.

**[Mount]**: if a full-round action is used to deploy this weapon's mount on a stable surface, its required Strength halves.

**[Guided]**: this weapon can spend a full-round action on a "target lock": make a ranged touch-attack check (no weapon damage) against the target's Reflex — or Evasion, for vehicles. For each point you win by, the next attack you make with this weapon against the locked target may convert 1 DP into 1 bonus success, up to the weapon's damage value. Guided weapons usually cannot fire without locking first, and gain no benefit from aiming.

**[Homing]**: as **[Guided]**, but no action is needed to lock — the weapon locks its target automatically after firing.

**[Tracking]**: tracking requires **[Guided]** as its basis. After attacking, if this round's attack misses, the weapon keeps attacking the target in subsequent rounds at a decreasing −5 DP per round until the target leaves maximum range or the dice run out.

**[Short Burst]**: you may fire short bursts — generally 3 rounds of ammunition per shot — gaining a 1 DP perfect bonus on the attack.

**[Long Burst]**: you may fire long bursts — generally 5 rounds per shot — gaining a 2 DP perfect bonus on the attack and automatically attacking one target adjacent to your target as well. Each extra target attacked costs you a 1 DP unnamed penalty; per attack, no target is affected by this ability more than once. Some Techniques or skills grant **[Long Burst]** — in that case ignore this entry's action requirements.

**[Full Auto]**: you may fire on full auto — generally 10 rounds per shot — gaining a 3 DP perfect bonus on the attack and automatically attacking all targets adjacent to your target, up to the number of rounds fired. Each extra target costs a 1 DP unnamed penalty; per attack, no target is affected more than once. Some Techniques or skills grant **[Full Auto]** — ignore this entry's action requirements in that case.

**[Suppressive Fire]**: with a standard action and no penalty, weapons with this property can rake a line out to half base range, or a cone out to a quarter of range. This is an area attack, generally consuming twice full-auto's ammunition, with damage equal to weapon damage. Make a normal firearms attack check with no weapon damage; it strips all the target's defenses except base, dodge, insight, and parry, and your Breach applies as normal to armor, natural, and Shield Defense. Targets make Reflex saves — each success prevents 1 damage — and suffer a penalty equal to your successes, plus the high-velocity penalty. You may deliberately spare targets in the area: each spared target costs your firearms attack check 1 success. If your Strength is below the weapon's requirement, you take the corresponding damage penalty while suppressing.

You may spend double ammunition again to sustain the suppression until the start of your next round's action. If you do, any creature or obstacle entering the suppressed area during that time also takes the damage.

Because suppressive fire strips part of each target's defenses, make a separate check for each target attacked.

Suppressive fire targets everything in range within the scene not under total cover; targets under total cover may have their cover attacked instead — break and destroy the cover, and the damage carries through to the target behind it as normal.

Special: some Techniques or skills grant **[Suppressive Fire]** — ignore this entry's action requirements in that case.

**[Unlimited Ammo]**: weapons with this property are generally launch-type ranged weapons whose ammunition or energy cost counts as unlimited, needing no actual bullets or power source.

If such a weapon uses physical ammunition, it cannot use special ammunition or receive bullet modifications.

Its bullets or energy cannot be extracted by any means — only when it attacks normally does the exact needed amount materialize and immediately fire.

When firing, each attack's bullets or energy are capped at the weapon's original magazine capacity (if any).

**[Material]**: this is not the property's actual name. The weapon is made of some material and gains that material's effects, piercing the corresponding damage reduction. Generally one weapon can have only one material type — for example **[Adamantine]** or **[Mithral]**.

**[Two-handed]**: weapons with this property are two-handed weapons; those without are one-handed. These weapons are usually larger and heavier. Wielding a two-handed weapon in one hand raises its Strength requirement by half the weapon's Size; wielding a one-handed weapon in two hands treats its minimum Strength requirement as 1 lower.

**[Heavy]**: this weapon is heavier than it looks. Its required Strength increases by half the weapon's Size, added to the weapon's Strength requirement.

**[Bound]**: a bonded item forms a close tie of fate with the first person to wield it perfectly; thereafter no one else can use it. The item is soul-bound to its owner: if it isn't on the owner when they return to the Lord God Space, it returns with them anyway. If the owner dies and loses the item, it automatically appears in their hand upon revival. The owner always knows its current direction and distance — a mind-affecting effect that cannot be cut off by any means.

**[Spray]**: weapons with this property can make a spray attack as a standard action — an area attack whose area depends on the weapon's other properties. Make a firearms attack check and use the successes as the area damage, gaining the attack's normal High Speed, Breach, and Dispelling properties. All targets in the area make Reflex saves; each success prevents 1 damage.

Unless stated otherwise, for all firearms with this keyword, base range is maximum range.
