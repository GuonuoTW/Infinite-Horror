# Crafting Rules

**Using documents and self-making Tech items**

On self-made items:

You can make items, or repair them. You must know how the making is done and be trained in the relevant skill. If the workshop the making requires exists in reality, or is realistically attainable, you may materialize it in your own room. If the making needs tools and facilities that don't exist in reality, or that real technology cannot reach (a brainwave reader, a nanoscale autonomous bipedal robot), you must exchange for those items in the Lord God Space.

Materials must be exchanged in the Lord God Space; materials with a special price count as noted, and materials with no noted price count as included in the item's basic needs.

PCs may self-make items marked 【Tech Category】 — items unusable in 【tech-fails】 films. (For Categories, see the last chapter's material.)

With certain special abilities, PCs may self-make items marked 【Fantasy Category】.

PCs cannot self-make 【Eldritch Category】 items unless specially stated.

When the item being made has No Tier, its interpretation and crafting DCs compute as though it were a D-Tier item.

The steps of making and repairing:

**1. Obtain the blueprint.**

The player must hold a blueprint before interpreting it. Two routes generally provide one: 1. Won through adventure in a horror film — the T-virus synthesis files in *Resident Evil*, or the dinosaur gene-synthesis files in *Jurassic World*. 2. Exchanged from the Lord God — who sells blueprints only for items the Lord God permits exchanging. The price is 【the item's Tier × its points price / 100】 in reward points, no Tiers needed, rounded up.

**2. Interpret the blueprint.**

Action: a periodic check with a period of one hour — meaning that with patient study, success always comes.

Use: interpreting scientific blueprints, studying a document's contents.

This takes an INT + Science (matching Profession) check. And through the study, each full hour of reading forces a DTR check at DC 2: on a failure, each point of margin costs your subsequent study checks 1 DP, and the DC climbs 1 per consecutive hour read. Clearing the penalties and the DC takes 8 hours of rest.

The interpretation DC is the higher of 【the blueprint's price】 and 【the item's Tier × 5 (rounded up)】.

An item's document need be interpreted only once.

Interpreting also demands prerequisites; some are listed here:

| Blueprint grade | Stat requirement | Skill requirement | Key Professions required |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| No Tier | INT 5 | Science skill 4 | 1 matching key Profession |
| D | INT 7 | Science skill 6 | 2 matching key Professions |
| C | INT 10 | Science skill 8 | 4 matching key Professions |
| B | INT 14 | Science skill 10 | 6 matching key Professions |
| A | INT 19 | Science skill 13 | 8 matching key Professions |
| S | cannot be made | cannot be made | cannot be made |

Note: double-Tier blueprints count as the current grade + 0.5.

Here are some science-related key Professions; the ST may add and adjust as fits:

Classical physics (a.k.a. Physics): required for all blueprint interpretation.

Optics: spectroscopy in particular.

Mechanics: buoyancy, gravity, attraction, and the rest.

Quantum physics: the physics of the microscopic.

Chemistry: the composition, structure, transformation, and uses of matter.

Biology: the kinds, structures, behaviors, functions, and origins of living things.

Mathematics: the study of quantity, structure, and change.

Blueprint-reading: see the "reading blueprints" Profession.

These science Professions, once learned, also help on the matching knowledge checks.

Broadly we suggest keying certain Professions to the item's abilities, for example:

An item dealing Field damage or granting Field defense demands the "Field" Profession for interpretation.

An item dealing energy damage or bearing energy resistance demands "Energetics".

An item built of metal demands "Metallurgy".

Details are the ST's call, and this clause applies only to Tech-Category crafting.

Note: if the target is Fantasy-Category, swap "Science" for "Occult" throughout; the Occult key skills follow the "Occult" skill notes.

Note: a fully interpreted blueprint is also called parsed.

**3. Prepare the machine tools.**

All repair and making need matching auxiliary equipment (tools); items of original Tier C and above need extra fabrication-and-assembly equipment when made. This machining equipment counts as a fully featured auxiliary rig — a nanobot swarm, a high-performance CNC machine — serving all making of same-Tier items, itself of the Tech Category; higher-grade rigs are backward-compatible with lower.

Auxiliary equipment for making and repairing D-Tier items counts as free in the Lord God Space, needing No-Tier tools.

Auxiliary equipment for C-Tier items costs D+500.

For B-Tier items, C+1000.

For A-Tier items, B+2000.

Special: the auxiliary equipment can itself be made.

But Fantasy and Tech Categories demand two separate rigs — one set for making Tech items, another Fantasy set for Fantasy items; the two cannot mix.

**4. Buy parts and materials.**

Exchanging parts from the Lord God Space for making costs 1/3 the original price, with the Tier requirement lowered one grade (a D-Tier item resolves through a notional E Tier that doesn't otherwise exist; E Tiers serve only for D-Tier items' materials). A Gauss pistol's materials run E+33 points, an electromagnetic shield's E+500.

Exchanging parts for repair needs no Tiers: divide the item's points by the vehicle's Structure, rounding up — each point of Structure repaired burns that many points.

**5. Making and repair**

Making:
You must combine the Lord God's principal parts with a share of parts you make yourself into the finished product.
This step takes an INT + relevant Crafts check — a periodic check with a period of one day, at DC (Tier × points price / 100), rounded up.
You must hold the matching Crafts Profession to check at all; the Professions for each item follow the "Crafts" skill notes.

For No-Tier items whose DC falls below 1, several can be made in a day, their total points summed until the DC reaches 1.

Repair:

You must combine the Lord God's principal parts with a share of your own to repair an item.

This step takes an INT + relevant Crafts check — a periodic check with a period of 1 hour; each time the successes reach the item's Hardness, 1 point of Structure returns.

And you must already have interpreted the item's blueprint, with Stats and skills meeting the conditions to make it.

**Dan-alchemy and immortal artifice**

The elixir rules apply only to items classified: dan elixirs. All double-Tier elixirs count as single-Tier except when buying blueprints and materials.
The artifice rules apply only to items classified: treasure artifacts. All double-Tier artifacts count as single-Tier except when buying blueprints and materials.

The prerequisites of dan-alchemy and immortal artifice match "Fantasy crafting", and the Occult Professions must include "Daoist Arts".
The Crafts sub-skills for them must be "Crafts–Dan Alchemy" and "Crafts–Immortal Artifice" —
sub-skills gained only through certain special abilities.

Refining times: No Tier 3 days, D 7, C 18, B 36, A 47, S 81.
Note that dan-alchemy and artifice must reach the required making DC within the set time; if time runs out with successes short, the work fails, materials and time unreturned.
And from condensation onward, the refiner cannot part from the furnace, or the condensation stage fails outright.

Refining also needs machine tools (furnaces, cauldrons and their kin), with requirements as for Tech crafting.
These tools equal the rigs of Fantasy crafting — owning the Fantasy-crafting rig means buying nothing more for refining, and vice versa.

Refining runs in stages: understanding, condensation, quenching, harvest.
Understanding and lighting the furnace take no time; condensation takes all of it; if the refining time is odd, quenching claims the final day alone.

Understanding: as with Tech crafting, refining requires knowing the elixir's or artifact's makeup and materials — buy the matching blueprint, rules as for Tech crafting, Science becoming Occult, and Science's Professions becoming their Occult counterparts.

Condensation: as with Tech crafting, with the Crafts Profession becoming Crafts–Dan Alchemy or Crafts–Immortal Artifice.
Note, unlike Tech crafting, the work must finish within its time limit.
Note also: if refining fails as Tech crafting can, roll 1d10 — on a 1 the furnace explodes, your rig permanently lost, the blast dealing area Lethal damage of (5 + furnace Tier × 5) in a radius of (furnace Tier × 10) meters, Reflex save.

Harvest: as with Tech crafting, you receive the elixir or artifact.
