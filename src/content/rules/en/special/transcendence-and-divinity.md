# Transcendence and Divinity

**Transcendent Divinity:**

Transcendent Divinity marks the gulf between mortals and gods.

To hold it is to step, from that point on, into the realm of the "divine".

For clarity, divinity is graded from 0 to 20.

Divinity

Category: Fantasy

Prerequisite: holding divinity (or Transcendent Divinity)

Price: S+16000

Description: divinity is a god's core of power; nearly all a god's strength runs through it. Legend even tells of many mortals who gained a divinity and were enthroned as gods.

Divinity advancement: your divinity grade rises by one.

The road to godhood: this ability may be bought repeatedly, effects stacking.

Born apart: most roads to godhood demand real trials — mere cultivation cannot earn this, so it cannot be self-taught. But once you hold divinity 0, you may buy this ability through self-teaching, standing for growth through your own cultivation.

Divinity 0

Beings at this grade are often called quasi-gods or demigods; the offspring of mortals and gods belong here, as do the great majority of celestial soldiers and generals. Heracles is the famous example.

Divinity 1–10

These beings are called lesser gods — weaker deities, generally the obscure small gods of some pantheon, usually excelling at one skill or holding one office.

China's Judges of the Dead, and the Duke of Thunder and Mother of Lightning, belong here.

Divinity 11–16

These are the greater gods — generally strong, playing main roles in their pantheons, with many worshippers and broad divine power.

Thor of the thunder and Ares of war are of this kind.

Divinity 17–19

These are the chief gods — the greatest deities of their pantheons, with tremendous strength and divine power.

Zeus and Odin are of this kind.

Divinity 20

At this grade stand only the creator gods. They surpass all gods; they may no longer even exist, yet their place cannot be shaken.

Pangu and Jehovah are of this kind.

**Powers of divine energy:**

Great deities hold supreme divine power, and it strengthens them from every side.

Note that divine-energy powers are bound tightly to divinity grade — no divinity, no divine energy.

Divine checks: boundless energy floods the deity, making their checks mightier: a deity's full checks gain a divine bonus of (divinity grade × 5) plus attack AS equal to their divinity grade.

Divine bonuses stack with every bonus.

Chief-god-grade deities gain a wholly new basic use of Willpower:

when making a check (in or out of turn), the character may pay 3 Willpower with no action; every DP in the check automatically counts as a 10, gaining no + Dice.

Divine defense: sacred energy wreathes the deity, making their defenses firmer than any mortal thing: the deity gains divine defense of (divinity grade × 5) plus defense AS equal to their divinity grade.

Divine defense stacks with every defense.

Divine reduction: divine protection grants the deity DR/− equal to (divinity grade × 2).

Divine resistance: divine protection grants all-energy resistance equal to (divinity grade × 2).

Divine spell resistance: divine protection grants spell resistance equal to (divinity grade × 2).

**Powers of divine right:**

Every god holds the powers of divine right.

Sacred immunities:

Deities are immune to all shapeshifting, petrification, and form-changing effects; their own shapeshifting abilities still work.

Deities live without food, water, sleep, or breath.

Deities have limitless lifespans and are immune to aging effects.

Deities are untouched by energy drain, Stats Drain, and Stats Damage.

Deities are immune to all mind-affecting effects.

Deities take no environmental influence.

Deities hold uncanny keenness of insight, with darkvision.

Divinity grants immunity to disease, toxins, sleep, possession, death, dazing, paralysis, and instant-death effects.

Deities of divinity 11 or higher are immune to all imprisonment and banishment.

Deities of divinity 17 or higher are immune to time, space, and causality effects.

For all graded immunities, your deity grade sets the Resisting Tier:

starting at C, rising one Tier per 3 grades of divinity

(0: C; 1–3: B; 4–6: A; 7–9: S).

**Transcendent abilities:**

Deities of grade 1 or higher understand and govern some piece of the mortal world, and the tie grants them power.

A deity of grade 1 or higher gains at least one extraordinary power per divinity grade — a transcendent ability. Using one is a Standard Action plus 1 Willpower; unless the power says otherwise, transcendent abilities work normally inside antimagic zones and ignore spell resistance.

Every god embodies the powers of their own domains,

choosing one domain ability per grade of divinity.

Nature domains: elements, seasons, the sun, forests, oceans, springs, pools, waterfalls, storms, fire, earthquakes, whirlwinds, rainforests, glaciers, the poles, stars, caves, dungeons, the Underdark, dawn/noon/dusk, night, the moon, shadow, darkness, wilderness, currents, waves, sea winds, physical force, ships, fair winds, cold, forest bounty, budding, tranquil places, crops.

Species domains: flying creatures, dragons, reptiles, intelligent sacred beasts, intelligent non-humans, therianthropes, beasts and monsters, liches, dryads, forest creatures, felines, dinosaurs, polar dwellers, the dead, the young.

Vocation domains: mages, casters, druids, nobles, rangers, guards, protectors, farmers, gardeners, keepers of tombs, hunters, miners, sailors, monks, thieves, bards, wanderers, necromancers, paladins, warriors, mighty overseers, caravans, knights.

Skill domains: sculpture, painting, literature, writers, cartography, technique, craft, architecture, smithing, theft, verse, song, debate, magic, spells, necromancy, weapon skill, stratagem, planning, tactics, prophecy, seafaring, naval war, trade, the arts, dance, portals, exploration, athletics, tracking, agriculture, self-preservation.

Concept domains: supremacy, migration, speed, joy, happiness, victory, adventure, justice, inspiration, freedom, creation, knowledge, beauty, love, passion, oblivion, loss, secrets, hedonism, sensual desire, renewal, fate, truth, kingship, the weave of magic, money, wealth, greed, duty, loyalty, insurrection, disease, venom, travel, exploration, undeath, luck, misfortune, unlucky accidents, endurance, suffering, martyrdom, fortitude, war, destruction, plunder, betrayal, cruelty, birth, creativity, vitality, pain, injury, anguish, torment, misery, torture, bloodlust, righteous war, great festivities, solemn funerals, purification, death, permanence, changelessness, oaths, protection, vengeance, retribution, just deserts, strife, hatred, tyranny, fear, mischief, murder, lies, conspiracy, fraud, illusion, festival revels, political corruption, peace, the cycle of life.

1. Restoration of Life

You can restore vitality to all things.

Effect: draw great powers of life inward, centered on the deity. Make a casting check: creatures of your choosing within (100 meters × success dice) of you gain Rapid Healing of (1 + your divinity grade), stacking with Rapid Healing they already hold; Rapid Healing above 4 heals Aggravated damage at 4:1. Lasts (1 round per 2 success dice).

The ability can also animate unowned objects, which take humanoid or animal shape as best fits their form; the animated thing's Stats are (divinity grade × 2 + Size adjustment + the energy the deity spends).

Deity spends 10%–100%: all Stats +1 to +10; lifespan 12–120 hours.

Size 5–100: bodily Stats +0 to +50; lifespan −0 to 100 hours.

Related domain elements: life.

2. Divine Recall

The god's memory for detail passes all mortal measure.

Effect: the god can recall any event of a chosen type that has ever happened; the type's scope is set in the god's domain description.

Special: this power may be chosen repeatedly, each time for a different type.

Related domain elements: knowledge.

3. Power of Truth

The deity sees through every lie, and can compel creatures to speak truth.

Effect: the deity knows the lies of anyone (excepting deities of equal or higher divinity), applied to any creature the deity can perceive.

The deity can move creatures by divine influence to speak honestly — at most a number of creatures equal to their divinity grade at once, the nearest in sight affected first. The moved cannot utter an untruth for a day. Resisting takes a Will check (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes); a success shields them from this power for a day.

Related domain elements: justice, law, truth.

4. Piercing Secrets

Before the deity, there are no secrets!

Effect: the deity need only look at a creature to know everything it has ever done (trivial or momentous). Only a passed Will check (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes) protects the target.

Related domain elements: knowledge, secrets.

5. Sensing Death

The deity senses when and where mortal things will die.

Effect: at a glance the deity knows precisely when, and in what manner, a mortal's life will end. Some deities offer mortals oblique explanations of it — which seems only to deepen their helpless sorrow.

Related domain elements: death.

6. Divine Skill Focus

Prerequisite: chosen skill at 15.

Effect: the god gains a (10 + divinity grade) bonus when using the chosen skill.

Special: the bonus stacks with the divinity-grade bonus. If the chosen skill relates to the god's domain, the god may add their divinity grade to the DC to attempt the task as a free action — a task of DC 20, for a Rank-6 god with this focus in a domain-related task, can be attempted as a free action at DC 26.

The god may take this power repeatedly, each time for a different skill.

Related domain elements: any.

7. Master of Craft

The deity's handiwork stands alone in all the world!

Prerequisite: Crafts skill 15.

Effect: everything the deity makes counts as masterwork, at ordinary cost and time. Their makings carry twice normal Hardness, and DCs to break them gain a +10 Reinforcement Bonus. This serves for walls, gates, or anything else the deity can build.

Related domain elements: craft, creation.

8. Forging Divine Artifacts

The deity crafts magic items beyond mortal limits.

Prerequisites: Crafts 15, Occult 15, three Professions under Crafts.

Effect: the deity can craft weapons, armor, scepters, staves, wands, rings, and legendary items beyond ordinary items' limits.

Related domain elements: craft, knowledge, magic.

9. Peerless Performance

The god's artistry towers over every mortal bard.

Prerequisite: Socialize skill 15.

Effect: singing to an instrument, telling jokes, reciting verse, playing, or performing otherwise takes the god only a Full-turn Action to produce an entrancing effect, usable (divinity grade) times per day, touching up to (20 × divinity grade) creatures within (100 × divinity grade) meters. Choose among:

— The affected cannot act properly: they dance, stare transfixed, roar with laughter or cheer the god's art. They take −4 to all defense, cannot make Reflex Saves, and cannot use shields. A Will save resists (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes). The effect lingers (10 × divinity grade) turns after the god stops performing.

— The affected feel blessed with happiness: immune to fear and coercion effects; if your divinity exceeds that of whoever laid an effect on a creature, you may counter or dispel it. This can dispel frenzy effects unless the source's divinity tops yours. The immunity lingers (10 × divinity grade) turns after the performance ends.

— The affected grow heavy with sleep: like a sleep spell, but touching any living thing. Resisting this mind effect takes a Will save (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes). The affected sleep (1 × divinity grade) hours; the god may end it at will.

— The affected are moved to their depths: any creature whose constitution falls below the god's divinity grade dies on the spot unless it passes a Fortitude save (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes); a success shields it from performance effects for a day.

Special: not every god may use all these effects; the usable range is set down in the domain description.

Related domain elements: art, poets, beauty, dance, love, music, poetry.

10. Shapechange

The deity can alter appearance and form — an Eldritch ability.

Prerequisites: a form-changing ability or skill; Survival 10.

Effect: with a mere Movement-type action the deity changes form, clothing and equipment included. The new form must be a tangible material body; the deity can bear limited structural change — an arm or two added or removed.

If the chosen form has wings, the deity flies with them; fins and gills likewise grant swimming and water-breathing. The new form holds itself freely, but returning to the true form takes another use of this power.

The power changes no attack bonus, defense grade, or saves, and grants none of the new form's special abilities, natural weapons, Born Defense, or supernatural and Eldritch powers. The deity may adjust the new form's details (hair color and style, skin color, sex, and so on). Used to aid disguise, it grants +10 on "Stealth" skill checks.

Related domain elements: nature, secrets, theft.

11. Speech of All Things

The deity converses freely with every creature they know.

Effect: the deity holds the supernatural power to communicate with all creatures known to them.

Related domain elements: nature.

12. Command from On High

The deity compels creatures to carry out appointed tasks.

Effect: as an enslavement art, but working only on mortal things; deities of equal or higher divinity can lift it.

The deity commands up to (divinity grade) targets at once, and no more than (divinity grade) per day, the nearest in sight affected first.

Related domain elements: justice, law.

13. Curse from Heaven

The deity lays a curse upon a creature!

Effect: mortal things get no save. Used against a deity of equal or lower divinity, the target must pass a Will check (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes). Only deities of equal or higher divinity can lift the curse.

The deity inflicts one of three effects:

Every Stat in one category takes Stats Damage equal to the divinity grade, unrecoverable naturally, to a floor of 1.

A 50% chance each turn of standing vacant, taking no action.

All checks take a penalty equal to the divinity grade.

The deity curses up to (divinity grade) targets at once, and no more than (divinity grade) per day, the nearest in sight affected first.

Related domain elements: evil.

14. Gift of Life

The deity holds the power to raise the dead!

Effect: the deity grants the dead new life, however long dead, corpse or no corpse.

Special: the power needs material components — and again, however long dead.

It can revive against the dead one's will. But the deity must rule the dead's kingdom, or their divine realm must house mortal souls. The power can restore life to elementals, outsiders, or an imprisoned soul.

The revived creature returns with all HP Healthy, Will full, and every Energy Pool full.

It cannot revive those slain by a higher-divinity deity's "Hand of Death" or "Dominion over Life and Death".

Related domain elements: healing, life.

15. Hand of Death

The deity can kill any living mortal thing on the spot!

Effect: the deity need only point lightly at any living mortal within sight — with a clear line between them — to snuff out its flame of life.

Special: a Standard Action. The mortal must pass a Fortitude check (DC = 10 + divinity grade + automatic DMR successes); even passing, it takes Aggravated damage of (divinity grade × 5) — near enough to death itself — ignoring damage absorption, damage reduction, and damage negation. A mortal killed by the failed check cannot be revived, save by a deity of equal or higher divinity through "Gift of Life", "Dominion over Life and Death", or its area version.

Related domain elements: death.

16. Keen Perception

The deity's senses pass all mortal keenness.

Transcendent perception: your sensory range becomes (1,500 meters × PCT) + (divinity grade × 15,000 meters).

Related domain elements: truth, exploration.

17. Divine Radiance

The very person of a deity of grade 1 or higher works deeply on mortals and lesser gods: they find themselves brimming with spirit or scrambling in dread, by the god's mood and their standing with the god.

Effect: divine radiance is a psychic power; mortals and lesser gods resist with Will checks at DC = 10 + divinity grade + the deity's automatic DMR successes. A deity is immune to the radiance of gods at or below their own grade, and any creature passing the check is immune to that deity's radiance for a day. The radiance spreads outward from the god, its maximum radius set by divinity grade; the deity reshapes the region as an immediate action — choosing 0 meters effectively suspends the power. Where two radiances overlap, only the higher divinity's works; equals coexist.

Divinity — maximum radius:

1–5: 10 meters × grade

6–10: 100 meters × grade

11–15: 1 kilometer × grade

16–20: 10 kilometers × grade

The deity may, as an immediate action, exempt an ally of their alignment or one of their own worshippers from the radiance — for a day, or until the deity ends it. Once caught in the radiance, a creature stays affected until it leaves the area. The deity picks one radiance effect, changeable each turn as an immediate action:

Dazzled: creatures bathed in the holy light stand entranced, able to do nothing but defend.

Dread: creatures tremble under the deity's majesty, taking a Morale Penalty of the deity's automatic DMR successes on all checks. The god's unveiled gaze and bearing strike terror; they flee by the quickest road they can find.

Valor: the deity's allies gain a Morale Bonus of the deity's automatic DMR successes on all checks.

Related domain elements: any domain.

18. Remote Sensing

The deity perceives the surroundings of any worshipper, temple, or other holy site or relic of theirs.

Effect: a Standard Action, radius growing 1 light-year per grade. It also works wherever the deity's name or title is spoken, for 1 hour after the speaking — and wherever events touching the deity's domains occur. Remote sensing crosses any plane, cannot be fooled by misdirection or mind-blank-type spells, and raises no detectable magical field (against psychic scans, say).

A deity watches their own surroundings plus two or more remote spots at once (by divinity grade):

1–5: 2; 6–10: 5; 11–15: 10; 16–20: 20.

Once the deity picks a remote spot, information flows from it automatically until they pick anew or the link fails (the hour after a spoken name expiring, say).

Blocking: a deity of grade 1 or higher may spend a Standard Action to block the sensing of gods at or below their grade, radius growing 1 light-year per grade, over any of their temples and holy places or wherever their domain's events occur. A god maintains two blocked regions at once, plus the hundred miles around themselves.

Related domain elements: any domain.

19. Domain Sense

The deity feels every event touching their domains.

Effect: the deity looks back (1 "light-year" per grade) into the past, or ahead the same span, over events tied to their domains. Sensing an event, the deity knows only that it happened and where — no more. A demigod of the seas senses the great wave striking a town and knows where, but not the wave's shape or heading. Sensing something, the deity may turn remote sensing upon it for the details.

Related domain elements: exploration, truth, secrets.

20. Transcendent Might

Deities do not grind through skill checks as mortal things do.

Effect: when the deity attempts a domain-related act whose DC does not exceed the table's value (by divinity grade), it resolves as an immediate action. Immediate actions per turn are also tabled:

Divinity — max automatic DC — immediate actions:

1–5: 15 — 2

6–10: 20 — 5

11–15: 25 — 10

16–20: 30 — 20

E.g.: a demigod of war and creation makes 30 Gauss pistols as an immediate action — given materials and tools at hand.

E.g.: a demigod of healing completes any surgery of DC 15 or below as an immediate action — given materials and tools at hand.

Related domain elements: any domain.

21. Enslaved Servants

This deity holds the essence of servitude: their command is absolute, and every servant obeys or walks the one road to destruction. Several uses:

Command: roll Will + Occult against the target's Will (the target may add Size and Occult level). Success makes the target your fanatic servant for a Scene; winning by more than 10 successes makes them your servant eternal, in life and after it.

Destruction: roll Will + Occult against the target's Will (the target may add Size and Occult level). Any success fills the target's Blunt track; winning by more than 5 fills their Lethal track; by more than 10, the target bursts apart and vanishes, no save.

Inspiration: make a casting check; every 2 successes grant servants 1 Morale Bonus, every 5 grant +1 automatic Morale success, capped by divinity grade.

Related domain elements: evil.

22. Fate's Turning Card

This deity holds the essence of fate: palm up is fortune, palm down is ruin — success and failure turn in the flip of a hand.

Effect: you may treat every 1 rolled in one check as a 10 — or every 10 rolled in one check as a 1.

Related domain elements: fortune, luck.

23. Root of Magic

This deity holds the essence of mystery.

Effect: using any ability or item of the magical essence, the deity gains (10 + 1 × divinity grade) automatic divine-bonus successes.

Related domain elements: magic, knowledge.

24. Omen of Death

This deity holds the essence of death; that touch is fatal.

Effect: the deity makes a casting check against the target's Will (the target may add Size and Occult level — generally, Bloodline rank).

Win, and you have "understood" the target's "death", and so may erase their "life" by the plainest, most direct route. The "death" shows itself in whatever form you "understand" — a scrawl of dots and lines, an unmistakable scar and bloodstain, or simply a reticle.

Whatever the form, that spot is where the creature's "death" resides. The spell holds for less than one "cloudflash", so the strike must bear, beyond the called-shot penalty (as for a target the size of an eye — five times Evade Defense), a further doubling (seven times Evade Defense in all).

Land the blow on the "death" and deal even 1 damage, and the attack counts as Blunt damage exactly filling all three of the target's HP tracks, unmitigable by any means.

Related domain elements: evil, death.

25. Heaven-Wrecking, Earth-Breaking

This deity holds the essence of destruction; their blows unmake sky and ground.

Effect: attacking to destroy large continuous objects (peaks, dams, towers), the deity ignores the strike point's size and position, damaging the whole object, with damage to Structure fifty-fold. Against creatures, final damage counts double.

Related domain elements: war, destruction.

26. Granting Divine Arts

You bestow your supreme power upon your faithful.

Effect: you may grant any creature that prays to you (their faith must be yours) the use of your energy-costing skills — these are called divine arts.

Related domain elements: any.

27. Undying Body

Your body does not perish.

Effect: you may restore your HP to Healthy.

Related domain elements: renewal, self-preservation.

28. The Kingdom Come

The world made by your supreme power arrives at last.

Effect: you gain a divine kingdom, returnable to at any time as a Standard Action — an S-Tier space ability, interceptable only by beings with divinity. Yet any being with Occult 10 or higher may fix your kingdom's coordinates with an Occult check at DC equal to your CTR, and enter it through any world-crossing space ability.

Your kingdom counts as an S-Tier vehicle: with X as the sum of your Stats, kingdom Size = X × 10, Structure = X, Hardness = X/2. Specially, the kingdom's Hardness can be neither ignored nor offset by Breach. It counts as a fully sealed vehicle: nothing outside can directly touch those within, while the kingdom's lord freely attacks or affects targets in range outside.

You may spend 9 Willpower per round to bring the kingdom down upon a mission world temporarily — counting as being within your kingdom.

29. King of War

Your attacks cannot be stopped.

Effect: on your attack checks, every die counts as a 10, gaining no + Dice. And if you hold abilities that deal damage or Negative Statuses, that damage and those points are all unavoidable.

Related domain elements: destruction, war, death, injury.

30. Power of the Warden

Your defense cannot be breached.

Effect: you may convert your defense AS into divine defense at 1:4, and add your defense bonuses, as divine bonuses, to saves and Resisting Checks.

Related domain elements: permanence, protection.
