# Creating Lifeforms

**Creating lifeforms**

In the Lord God Space, everyone may own one lifeform of their own making — one at a time. Only when your created lifeform is lost or dead may the making of the next begin.

When film difficulty is tuned to the samsara team's strength, created lifeforms don't count into team strength — and neither do they draw the per-head rewards. The creator answers for everything their creation does: if it fulfills the conditions for some reward or punishment through any cause, the creator counts as having fulfilled them, and all reward and punishment falls on the creator.

When the creator dies, a still-living creation is erased with them; when the creator is revived, it revives too.

A member's created lifeform must travel the samsara worlds alongside its creator — unless the creator pays 1 D Tier per film to leave it in the Lord God Space.

Creating a human:

If the creator is the team's captain, their first created human is free; anyone else pays 500 points.

The creator freely sets the artificial human's appearance — a winged bird-person, a wolf-headed body — but it must be grounded in human form; base capabilities compute as an ordinary human's, and any extra limbs are decoration only.

The created human's memories, character, and manner form entirely from the creator's own memory data. The creator may name someone they know of as the model — but if the model is too strong, the Lord God may bill extra points. A character can ask for Saeba Ryō, say, but his strength so exceeds a mortal's that the excess must be topped up with points and Tiers.

Stats: set all base Stats to 1, distribute 5/4/3 points freely among the Physical, Mental, and Social categories, adding them to individual Stats. Then add 4 free points wherever desired. Raising a Stat already at 4 up to 5 costs 2 points. An artificial human's Stat cap is 5.

Skills: set all base skills to 0, distribute 11/7/4 points among the Physical, Mental, and Social skill categories, adding each share to its skills. Then add 7 free points wherever needed. The artificial human's skill cap is the creator's own highest skill; if that highest falls below 5, the cap is 5. It also receives 3 Professions, placed under one or more skills of at least level 1.

Feats: assign 5 points to Feats, each buying 1 Feat level; multi-level Feats cost 1 point per level. Artificial humans cannot take the Special Identity Feat. Also, each point of its INT grants 2 language Feat points, spendable only on languages; language Feats aren't capped by the INT Stat.

What artificial humans are for:

Making Tech-Category items: an artificial human may craft Tech-Category items of C-Tier or below under the tech-crafting rules.

Casting BUFF effects: once enhanced, an artificial human can lend bonuses to some of your checks, or reinforce parts of your capability.

Fighting beside you: an artificial human can join your battles.

Contract purposes: an artificial human can stand as the other party to certain contracts or joint undertakings — though it must still meet the matching conditions.

Inheritance (optional): with this optional rule open, an artificial human may take the Lord God Space's enhancements just as its creator does, every price paid by the creator. When the player's character dies, the player's next character card inherits everything invested in the artificial human — Tiers, points, and XP, the base creation price included.

Creating an animal companion:

If the creator is the team's captain, the first animal companion is free; anyone else pays 200 points. If the chosen animal is rare in the real world, or long extinct, pay 500 instead.

The created animal's Stats rest entirely on the ordinary breed. Players cannot create species that never existed — or were never confirmed — in the real world.

The created companion's memories, character, and manner form entirely from the creator's memory data. The creator may name a known animal as the model — but too strong a model draws extra billing. A character can ask for a Tyrannosaurus, say: being far beyond ordinary animals and extinct besides, its price first rises to 500 points, and the strength beyond an ordinary animal's is topped up with points and Tiers.

Stats: set all base Stats to 1; assign 8 free Stat points to Physical, 3 to Mental, and 3 to Social, adding them to individual Stats. Then add 4 free points wherever desired. Raising a Stat already at 4 up to 5 costs 2 points. The companion's Stat cap is 5.

Skills: set all base skills to 0; assign 15 free skill points to Physical skills, 2 to Mental, and 2 to Social, adding each share to its skills. Then add 7 free points wherever needed. The companion's skill cap is the creator's highest skill, or 5 if that falls below 5. It also receives 2 Professions, placed under one or more skills of at least level 1.

Feats: assign 2 points to Feats, each buying 1 level; multi-level Feats cost 1 point per level. Companions cannot take Special Identity. Companions get no language Feat points — not even parrots and other species that produce or mimic human speech.

Size: a companion's starting Size runs 1 to 5 at most. To build one above Size 5, fix the final Size, then pay, from 5 upward, (target Size − 5) × 200 points per point raised. E.g.: player Jia wants a big Size-8 tiger — from Size 5, reaching 6 costs 200, 7 costs 400, 8 costs 600: 1,200 points in all.

A companion's limbs bar it from certain checks — put a dinosaur's Larceny at 5 and it still cannot pick locks. The ST rules such cases.

The creator may exchange for abilities on the companion's behalf, and it can grow through cultivation. But the creator cannot buy it the Lord God's enhancements directly (nearly all being designed for humans): the companion's exchanges — Bloodlines, Alternations, Arts, items, and the rest — must use special variants compatible with its species, and the variants pass through the ST's review.

A created companion's attitude toward its creator counts as Obedient, unchangeable for any reason — mind-affecting abilities included.

Toward its creator, a created companion knows every command; the commands are listed under the Animal Ken skill on the "Social Skills" page.

Since companions span so many species, the creator automatically receives the species' matching traits at creation — the first free, the rest bought from the trait list below.

E.g.: create an eagle, and Flight comes automatic and free.

E.g.: create a bat, and Mammal and Flight come automatic — one free, the other paid per the prices below.

E.g.: create a tiger, and Mammal and Feline come automatic — one free, the other paid. The creator cannot bolt Flight onto a tiger.

Trait list:

Reptile:

Price: 1,000

Effect: a companion with this trait has land speed of half base speed, moves on land without checks, and breathes normally in atmosphere.

Conflicts: Mammal, Insect, Aquatic

Example: lizards

Amphibian:

Prerequisite: Reptile

Price: D+1000

Effect: a companion with this trait has land speed of half base speed and swim speed of full base speed, moves on land and underwater without checks, and breathes normally in air and underwater alike. For trait conflicts it counts as holding an "Aquatic" trait compatible with "Reptile".

Example: toads

Mammal:

Price: 1,000

Effect: a companion with this trait has land speed of full base speed, moves on land without checks, and breathes normally in atmosphere.

Conflicts: Reptile, Insect, Aquatic

Examples: cats, dogs

Feline:

Prerequisite: Mammal

Price: 1,000

Effect: each turn, the companion may spend a Whole-round Action on two melee Brawl attacks at one target, each at a 5 DP Unnamed Penalty; the pair counts as one attack for rulings.

Conflicts: Primate, Canine, Ursine

Examples: lions, tigers, leopards

Primate:

Prerequisite: Mammal

Price: 1,000

Effect: the companion further gains a climb speed of half base speed, climbing without checks.

Conflicts: Feline, Canine, Ursine

Examples: apes, monkeys

Canine:

Prerequisite: Mammal

Price: 1,000

Effect: after a melee Brawl attack hits, the companion may spend a Movement Action on a trip, counting the touch-attack portion as already passed.

Conflicts: Feline, Primate, Ursine

Examples: wolves, foxes, dogs

Ursine:

Prerequisite: Mammal

Price: D+2000

Effect: after a melee Brawl attack hits, the companion may spend a Movement Action on a grapple, counting the touch-attack portion as already passed.

Conflicts: Feline, Primate, Canine

Example: grizzly bears

Insect:

Forbidden: the Lord God cannot — or will not — create insect-kind.

Blind:

Price: 1,000

Effect: companions with this trait live in lightless places and generally lack eyes; choose one of darkvision, blindsense, or tremorsense for the companion.

Examples: moles — blindsense; spiders — tremorsense

Flight:

Price: D+1500

Effect: the companion can fly, at flight speed equal to base speed. The flight's starting Mobility follows the companion's kind.

Special: at creation only, the creator may choose:

each doubling of the companion's flight speed costs a further D+500;

each grade of flight Mobility costs a further D+500, up to perfect.

Examples: colugos — clumsy; sparrows — average; falcons — agile; hummingbirds — perfect

Aquatic:

Price: 1,000

Effect: the companion swims at base speed, moves underwater without checks, and breathes underwater normally. Out of the water it cannot act, and enters suffocation.

Special: whales, dolphins, and other marine mammals may take Aquatic without conflict, but cannot breathe underwater — instead their breath-holding time stretches a hundredfold.

Conflicts: Reptile, Mammal, Insect

Example: fish

What animal companions are for:

Riding: a companion can serve as a mount for mounted combat.

Fighting beside you: a companion can join your battles.
