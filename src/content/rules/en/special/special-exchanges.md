# Special Exchanges

**The Hyperbolic Time Chamber:**

A small training space opened inside the Lord God Space. Each entry costs D+1000. However long you train inside, the moment your training there has earned 11 XP — bonuses included — the Lord God kicks you out. Usable once per rest period in the Lord God Space. (Integrity note: INT powers do not work on this training.)

Optional rule: training in the Time Chamber isn't a matter of stepping in and stepping out — the player should roleplay the training, and the ST may arrange virtual battles to taste: casting the player's old nemesis as an inner demon to fight in the phantasm, and so on, for roleplay's sake.

**Healing and resets:**

When you crawl back to the Lord God Space after a vicious battle — an arm and a leg gone, half a lung blown away — the first order of business is healing; prices and rules below.

The Lord God Space can repair every wound and strip supernatural effects from a target.

Anyone may trigger this by spending their own points — even spending their own points to use it on someone else.

Prices:

Blunt damage: free.

Lethal damage: free.

Aggravated damage: 200 reward points per point repaired.

Removing Negative Statuses: 50 points per point removed.

Removing supernatural effects (including but not limited to magic/curses): No Tier free, D 200 points, C 400, B 600, A 800, S 1,000; above S costs as S.

Repairing an organ/limb: 200 points per organ — eyes, stomach, lungs, and so on.

Whole-body repair: fabricating a new body costs 1,000 points.

Since most Stats Damage carries no special notes, such Stats Damage costs 200 points per point repaired.

Damage specially marked permanent — and Stats Drain — cannot be repaired.

**Revival:**

Every character gets one direct revival through the Lord God in the Lord God Space.

Should the character die again, this route is closed — only story items gathered in the plot (*The Mummy*'s Book of Amun-Ra) or special plot effects (*The Chronicles of Narnia*'s sacrificial Stone Table) can revive them.

Each revival method works only once per character.

Revival's price equals the sum of all Tiers and points on the character plus everything they ever bought with Tiers and points (template abilities like Bloodlines, skill trees, items, all of it).

E.g.: a character holds an unspent D+1000, a D+600 Bloodline, and 3,000 points of Stats — reviving them costs DD+4600.

Unless stated otherwise, every non-permanent BUFF and DEBUFF is removed outright on revival; permanent effects — permanent Stat gains, permanently reduced HP caps — persist through it.

**Respec:**

Sometimes the thought comes: "ah, regret — I never should have bought that ability." In that case, pay C+3000 for a respec reset. It resets every exchange bought with points — Stats, equipment, Bloodlines, Alternations, the lot — returning you to the state in which you first entered the Lord God Space. Abilities bought with XP are not reset — those you earned by your own hard study.

Anything already lost along the way — an item, Bloodline, or Alternation gone for other reasons — is not settled in the respec. Likewise, items no longer at full charge cannot be washed away. (Spend 5 mana from the World Tree Leaf, and the Leaf survives your respec.)

If you bought resources granting extra XP or XP bonuses, each extra XP gained costs you 400 points at respec; short of reward points, deduct at 1,000 points = 1 D Tier; short of that, no respec.

Bonuses from Legendary INT are exempt.

If buying an ability gave you a bundled item or ability, you lose it along with the purchase; if you have already lost that bundled item or ability, its original price is deducted from your Tiers and points — short of that, no respec.

Time Chamber purchases refund nothing at respec — and Time Chamber XP costs you no reward points or Tiers either.

Respec has no usage limit: dissatisfied, wash again, as long as the points hold out.

Team-transfer papers and the samsara watch are never reset.

Note: if you bought Stats with XP, the respec washes those Stats away too — and the XP is not returned.

If the respec costs you an ability's prerequisites (learning prerequisites included), the ability becomes unusable —

learn a Tome's exclusive spells and lose the Tome, and its exclusive spells are beyond you.
