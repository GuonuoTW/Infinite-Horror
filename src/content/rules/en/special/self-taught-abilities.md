# Self-Taught Abilities

**Self-taught ability rules**

Sometimes the Lord God allows you to "grasp certain abilities through your own study" — that is, to learn them not by paying Tiers and reward points, but by spending XP. These are the self-teaching rules.

What self-teaching can buy includes:

Titles and the skills of their skill trees;

Energy Pools and the skills of their skill trees;

skills of Bloodline skill trees;

skills of Alternation skill trees;

skills of Eye Technique skill trees;

Cultivation and the skills in its manuals;

skills of school skill trees;

spells;

Arts;

and more besides. What it cannot buy is a template ability itself (you can't self-buy a higher Bloodline rank, though Bloodline skills remain buyable) or items (Tomes being special, they can't be self-created either).

For a skill you already own, you may self-teach its successors or sub-skills within the same tree (raising D-Tier Inner Force to C, raising a D-Tier energy-reinforcement skill to C, or training a same-Tier technique when you already hold the D-Tier energy-shooting skill).

But for a new non-template ability (a Title, an Art), you must buy its lowest rank or its outline with Tiers and points — you may not self-create the lowest rank directly.

Example 1) A skill spans D, C, B, A: the D rank must be bought with points and Tiers before later ranks can be bought with XP.

If the skill spans only C, B, A: the C rank must be bought with points and Tiers before later ranks can be bought with XP.

If the skill has only a C rank: it must be bought with points and Tiers, and cannot be bought with XP at all.

Example 2) A Bloodline skill requires first holding the Bloodline, with the matching rank of its skill tree opened, before self-teaching can buy it.

Example 3) An Art with no outline, whose D rank onward is a scatter of assorted abilities — such a resource can only be bought with Tiers.

The computation:

XP paid = (number of D Tiers × 8) + (reward points / 125), rounded up.

E.g.: an A+4000 resource gained through self-teaching costs 248 XP (27 × 8 + 4000 / 125).

If the original price already includes XP, add that XP on top after computing the above.
