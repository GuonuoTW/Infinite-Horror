# Mental Disorders

**Mental disorders**

In the course of play, plenty of things can unsettle a character's once-stable mind and tip it into madness —

strange magics, Negative Statuses, skills, disease, toxins, certain regional effects, glimpses of certain mysterious beings: all of them possible triggers.

Madness comes in two states: temporary madness and permanent madness.

===

A character in the grip of a flare-up may lose temporary control of themselves; control generally passes to the ST, who may run the character and act for them, or coach the player on how to play the madness out — telling the player how long the flare-up lasts.

If a character holds several madness symptoms, the ST decides which triggers; generally temporary madness resolves first, permanent madness last.

Special: note that mind-effect immunity may keep your disorders from triggering where possible — but a mental disorder is not itself a mind-affecting effect.

===

Temporary madness:

Special: the 1d10 rounds, 1d10 minutes, and 1d10 symptom rolls in this section are mere dice rolls, not checks — no check-related ability applies.

A character under temporary madness suffers its effect for 1d10 rounds in combat, or 1d10 minutes outside it.

On entering temporary madness, roll 1d10 for the symptom:

1. Panicked flight: the character immediately flees the scene — or the target that drove them mad — by their most powerful means of movement.

2. Phantom disability: the character is instantly convinced they've lost a limb. Choose one at random; as though permanently lost, the character cannot use it until the madness ends. (Eyes, ears, voice, the four limbs, and tail all qualify.)

3. Violent frenzy: the character falls into rage, and each turn must aim their most powerful attack at the nearest target, friend or foe alike; among several qualifying targets, they pick the strongest/weakest possible (by the character's nature).

4. Paranoid delusion: vivid phantasms fill the character's eyes until reality and hallucination blur — true seeing and other abilities do nothing — and the character skips their turn.

5. Blackout: the character falls unconscious.

6. Hysteria: the character bursts into wild laughter, weeping, screaming, or the like — dazed.

7. Lost within: the character's mind is trapped in its own inner world — held.

8. Will-less: the character loses all judgment of their own, acting on whatever command the previous character in Initiative order gives them — though never self-harm or its kin (harming teammates is fine).

9. Lamb to the slaughter: nothing matters anymore — the character is helpless.

10. Deeper madness: reroll 1d10 (ignoring 10s, rerolling them) for the temporary symptom — and the madness upgrades to permanent.

===

Permanent madness:

Special: the 1d10 rounds, 1d10 minutes, and 1d10 symptom rolls in this section are mere dice rolls, not checks — no check-related ability applies.

A permanently mad character seems no different from anyone else day to day — but that only means the madness lies dormant: the next trigger plunges them straight back into temporary madness.

Roll 1d10 (ignoring 10s, rerolling) for your permanent madness's flare-up symptom. If the permanent madness grew out of a temporary one, skip this — your temporary symptom simply becomes the permanent one's flare-up symptom.

While permanently mad, the character's mind is deeply unsteady: any negative "mind-affecting" effect immediately sets off their symptom.

And in the dormant stretches of permanent madness, the character carries one of the following traits — roll 1d10 for which:

1. Compulsion: the character is driven to repeat some act — washing hands, touching a certain thing. While awake they must perform it every hour; failing to, their full checks take a 3 DP Unnamed Penalty, stacking without limit, until the act is done.

2. Delusions: vivid hallucinations visit the character's eyes from time to time; true seeing and its kin do nothing. The character cannot apply skill-level AS on checks.

3. Phobia: the character conceives an absolute terror of whatever broke their mind. Sensing it, they flee if they possibly can; unable to flee and sharing its Scene, their full checks take a 6 DP Unnamed Penalty.

4. Self-harm: the character is forever striking at themselves. Half their HP (rounded down) stays permanently filled with Blunt damage.

5. Amnesia: the character can't recall what they've done — they forget things, and the player must play it out. Each passing day, cast an Opportunity Roll: anything short of success makes the character forget one random active skill, Technique, or spell — not lost, merely unusable while forgotten. A successful Opp. Roll recovers one lost memory (a forgotten ability or event).

6. Withdrawal: the character believes themselves good for nothing and stops striving. They cannot make Gene Lock opening checks — though ranks already opened still open as normal.

7. Mania: the character's temper burns and will not be soothed. They cannot take Short Rests.

8. Schizophrenia: a new personality splits off within. The new personality's traits (temperament, alignment) run nearly opposite the old. You gain one random new Sin, active only while the new personality fronts. Under intense shock, or after any Long Rest, there is an even chance the personalities switch.

9. Dependence: the character clings desperately to another character or a lucky object. Choose one: while that character is out of your perception (or dead) / while that object is out of your perception (or destroyed), your full checks can use no AS.

10. Dysregulation: the character's senses fall out of tune. On taking damage, they make a Will Save at DC equal to the total damage (3 Lethal plus 4 Aggravated makes DC 7); failing, they are dazed.
