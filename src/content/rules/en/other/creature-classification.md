# Creature Classification

**Creatures**

Some resources grant players special creature types.

Characters generally receive a creature type through Lord God exchange or some special ability — abilities the Lord God has adjusted — so gaining the creature type does not grant the creature's "HP adjustment" or "monster-only" traits, unless specially stated.

If a creature comes to hold creature traits that cannot coexist, it bursts apart and dies — no save, unavoidably.

Generally, samsara travelers are human; exchanging for an enhancement of another creature classification changes them automatically to the matching creature type.

Exchanging for resources under the human classification, the player remains human for rulings.

Exchanging for gene-altering resources, classification-changing ones, or resources under non-human classifications changes the player's creature classification; unless stated otherwise, they no longer count as human.

A player no longer counted human who gains further creature classifications holds them all together, provided they can coexist.

For creatures with several classifications, when effects like 【Spiritual】, 【Holy】, 【Divine Arms】, or 【Magical】 apply, the attacker picks one of the creature's classifications to apply against.

Such a creature gains every immunity and every trait of all its classifications — but the HP adjustment applies only once.

Where effects conflict, the trait less favorable to the creature prevails.

**Creatures of darkness:**
A fairly broad notion. In the Infinite world, every creature born of evil magic and supernatural power — or carrying that power in its blood — counts as a creature of darkness: undead, certain yao-kind, and specially annotated creatures among them. Apart from the few Bloodlines, cultivations, and spells that confer the trait on players, its holders are usually story creatures, and it generally requires explicit annotation.

Holy damage taken upgrades one grade.

Damage from 【Holy】-property weapons upgrades the weapon-damage portion one grade.

When a character holds several sources of the darkness trait, each beyond the first adds 2 to the Holy damage — and 【Holy】-property damage — they take.

No creature can be at once a creature of light and of darkness.

**Creatures of light:**
Every creature born of benevolent magic and supernatural power — or carrying that power in its blood — counts as a creature of light: celestial beings, generally, or others of sacred blood. Apart from certain exchanges conferring the trait on players, its holders are usually story creatures.

Unholy damage taken upgrades one grade.

Damage from 【Unholy】-property weapons upgrades the weapon-damage portion one grade.

When a character holds several sources of the light trait, each beyond the first adds 2 to the Unholy damage — and 【Unholy】-property damage — they take.

No creature can be at once a creature of light and of darkness.

**Undead:**
Undead of every flavor fill the Infinite Horror worlds, from D&D settings to mythologies — but unless stated otherwise, the rules give them all these traits:

HP adjustment: undead have no Aggravated damage track, but double HP. Everything that deals Aggravated damage deals them Lethal instead; the moment Lethal damage fills the track, they are destroyed.

Monster-only: undead have no INT and take no effects from INT gains or losses — meaning immunity to abilities resisted or saved through INT, and so immunity to mind effects. Note: an undead that does have INT (a lich) loses this trait.

Undead are immune to toxins, paralysis, dazing, disease, nausea, and instant-death effects.

Undead need no sleep — hence sleep immunity — nor food nor breath.

All undead immunities are C-Tier.

Once dead, undead cannot be revived, save by time-rewinding abilities.

Acts with 【Spiritual】 or 【Holy】 properties deal undead double damage outright; holding both properties does not make it triple.

Undead generally carry the creature-of-darkness trait.

**Incorporeals:**
Incorporeal describes what has no actual body — unaffected by ordinary physicality and unable to exert physical influence on anything, unsmellable, unhearable, yet visible, and genuinely there.

Incorporeals have these effects:

HP adjustment: incorporeal creatures have a single HP track, with HP increased by their Will. Everything that deals Aggravated damage deals them Lethal instead; the moment Lethal fills the track, they are destroyed.

Incorporeals affect incorporeals normally.

Unless stated otherwise, incorporeals choose at will whether to affect objects lacking 【Magical】, 【Spiritual】, or 【Divine Arms】; against objects holding those properties, judge as below.

Incorporeals choose at will whether ordinary physical acts affect or harm them.

Acts with 【Divine Arms】 affect incorporeals normally. Acts with 【Spiritual】 deal them double damage outright and affect them normally. Beyond that, 【energy damage】, 【Field damage】, and 【Mental damage】 harm them normally. Acts with 【Magical】 have only a half chance of harming an incorporeal.

Incorporeals are immune to toxins and disease — a D-Tier immunity.

Permanently incorporeal creatures need no food, water, or sleep; the temporarily incorporeal still do.

Incorporeals feel no gravity and have no weight, flying at (base speed / 10) with perfect Mobility.

Incorporeal creatures pass through ordinary solids and liquids, though inside solids they are blind; they are immune to falling damage.

Incorporeals cannot pass barriers bearing 【Magical】, 【Spiritual】, 【Divine Arms】, or Field natures.

An incorporeal's deliberate sounds can be heard by anyone.

Attacking, incorporeals ignore the Armor Defense and Shield Defense of armor and shields lacking 【Spiritual】 or 【Divine Arms】; against 【Magical】 armor and shields, the defenses work with a half chance.

Incorporeal creatures lose their own Born Defense.

**Spirits:**

In essence, a spirit hardly counts as an actually existing thing — or rather doesn't belong to this world or plane. A spirit can neither exert nor receive physical influence, cannot be smelled or heard — cannot even be seen.

Spirits have these effects:

HP adjustment: spirit creatures have a single HP track, with HP increased by their Will. Everything that deals Aggravated damage deals them Lethal instead; the moment Lethal fills the track, they are destroyed.

Spirits affect spirits normally.

Unless stated otherwise, spirits choose at will whether to affect objects lacking 【Magical】, 【Spiritual】, or 【Divine Arms】; against objects holding those properties, judge as below.

Spirits choose at will whether ordinary physical acts affect or harm them.

Acts with 【Divine Arms】 affect spirits normally. Acts with 【Spiritual】 deal them double damage outright and affect them normally. Beyond that, 【energy damage】, 【Field damage】, and 【Mental damage】 harm them normally. Acts with 【Magical】 have only a half chance of harming a spirit.

Spirits are immune to toxins and disease — a D-Tier immunity.

Permanently spirit creatures need no food, water, or sleep; the temporarily spirit still do.

Spirit creatures pass through ordinary solids and liquids, though inside solids they are blind; they are immune to falling damage.

Spirits feel no gravity and have no weight, flying at (base speed / 10) with perfect Mobility.

Spirits cannot pass barriers bearing 【Magical】, 【Spiritual】, 【Divine Arms】, or Field natures.

Spirits can turn invisible; switching between invisible and visible is a Free Action, counting for detection as a C-Tier anti-detection means of the Fantasy Category. Against spirit sight the invisibility simply fails, with no contest under the detection rules.

Attacking, spirits ignore the Armor Defense and Shield Defense of armor and shields lacking 【Magical】, 【Spiritual】, or 【Divine Arms】.

Spirit creatures lose their own Born Defense.

**Constructs:**
Construct creatures are the robots, golems, and other made machines. Machine-bodies resemble objects, with differences. Unless stated otherwise, constructs generally count as objects, and generally not as living.

Constructs have these effects:

HP adjustment: constructs generally have no EDR and take no effects from EDR gains or losses — meaning immunity to abilities resisted through EDR or Fortitude. A single HP track, but doubled HP.

Constructs have darkvision.

Constructs are immune to toxins and disease.

Constructs are immune to fatigue points, exhaustion, and drowsiness points.

Constructs need no sleep — hence sleep immunity — nor food nor breath, having neither digestive nor respiratory systems.

All construct immunities are C-Tier.

Constructs cannot recover damage or Negative Statuses on their own; only their own Rapid Healing or Regeneration works.

Once dead, constructs cannot be revived, save by time-rewinding abilities.

Under EMP effects a construct is not paralyzed, but held for the duration; and if the EMP can deal damage, the final damage doubles.

**Fantastical magic creatures:**
These are the fantastic beasts of human imagination —

the dragons of Western fantasy, your Smaugs and *Eragon*, not the Eastern divine dragons;

phoenixes, griffons, manticores, and kunpeng all belong here.

Fantastical magic creatures generally have these effects:

They have darkvision.

Unless they can take human form, they can use only their own natural weapons.

They are immune to sleep and paralysis points.

All their immunities are C-Tier.

**Elemental creatures:**
Elementals here mean the elements self-formed between heaven and earth (Eastern) — metal, wood, water, fire, earth — or the creatures of the elemental planes (Western): earth, water, fire, and air.

Elemental creatures generally have these effects:

They have darkvision.

They are immune to toxins, disease, paralysis, and dazing.

They are immune to the multiple-attack penalty.

Unless they can take human form, they can use only their own natural weapons.

They need no sleep — hence sleep immunity — nor food nor breath, having neither digestive nor respiratory systems.

All their immunities are C-Tier.

Once dead, elementals cannot be revived, save by time-rewinding abilities.

**Ooze creatures:**
Oozes are a rare creature type — the slime being the most familiar.

Ooze creatures generally have these effects:

Monster-only: oozes have no DTR and take no effects from DTR gains or losses — meaning immunity to abilities resisted through DTR or Will, and so immunity to mind effects. Note: an ooze that does have DTR loses this trait.

Oozes sit in permanent blindness, counting as eyeless — but gain blindsight out to sensory range.

Oozes are immune to toxins, disease, paralysis, dazing, and shapeshifting.

Oozes need no sleep — hence sleep immunity.

Oozes are immune to 8+ Dice and 9+ Dice.

Oozes are immune to the multiple-attack penalty.

All ooze immunities are C-Tier.

Oozes can use only their own natural weapons, and can neither wear nor use any shield or armor.

Oozes lose the Gene Lock: a being with one who becomes an ooze can neither open nor attempt to open it.

**Plant creatures:**
Plants are a fairly common creature type, films included — the great tree in *Hellboy II: The Golden Army*, Treebeard in *The Lord of the Rings* — both plants.

Plant creatures generally have these effects:

Monster-only: plants have no INT and take no effects from INT gains or losses — meaning immunity to abilities saved or resisted through INT, and so immunity to mind effects. Note: a plant that does have INT loses this trait.

Plants have low-light vision.

Plants are immune to paralysis, dazing, and shapeshifting.

Plants need no sleep — hence sleep immunity.

All plant immunities are C-Tier.

Plants can use only their own natural weapons, and can neither wear nor use any shield or armor.

**Swarm creatures:**
A swarm is a mass of ultra-tiny (fungi, bacteria), tiny (roaches and other insects), or very small creatures (rats, bats) that acts as one creature. A swarm carries the properties of the individuals composing it.

Swarm creatures generally have these effects:

A swarm has unified HP, unified Initiative, unified speed, and unified defense, and makes saves like a single creature.

A swarm has no clear front or back and no definite anatomy, so it is immune to called shots, immune to 8+ Dice and 9+ Dice, and immune to the multiple-attack penalty.

A swarm of Size-1 creatures takes only half damage from single-target attacks dealing piercing or slashing damage (after damage is computed, before mitigation abilities).

A swarm of sub-Size-1 creatures is immune to single-target physical damage.

Facing abilities that attack every unit in an area separately, a swarm counts as one unit, taking one attack.

Swarms are immune to spells, abilities, and effects that target a specific number of creatures. Area-affecting spells, abilities, and effects that deal damage deal a swarm double.

A swarm occupies no space and can share a position with other creatures. A creature casting while sharing a swarm's position needs Focusing checks.

A swarm has no reach, attacking only targets in its own position.

A swarm's attacks ignore cover, Base Defense, Evade Defense, and Guard Defense.

Swarms cannot be tripped, grappled, or slammed — nor can they grapple.

If a swarm routs, 30% of its members count as dead, 30% unconscious. To judge a particular individual, roll a d100: 1–30 dead, 31–60 unconscious of grave wounds, 61–100 escaped unharmed.

Bodies of larger units can also count as swarm-like (armies, say), ST's call.

Swarms lose the Gene Lock: a being with one who becomes a swarm can neither open nor attempt to open it.
