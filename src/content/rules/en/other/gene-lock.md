# Gene Locks

**Gene Lock rules**

The Gene Lock is the most fundamental potential hidden in the human body. Great stretches of the human genome go unused — those genes are a record of the evolutionary road Earth's life once walked. From the most primitive single-celled organisms, through fish and reptiles, up to humankind, fragments of every sort can be found there — even genes whose use and origin humanity does not yet know. To open the Gene Lock is to switch those slumbering fragments back on, breaking the body's potential past its limits. But excess carries its price: when a limit is broken, the punishment for breaking it must follow… and the punishment for opening the Gene Lock is life itself…

On average, more than eight in ten people carry the potential to open a Gene Lock: sensing mortal danger, and letting that danger goad the body's potential awake. Survive the danger, and you stand very near an opening; with better constitution still, you may open the Gene Lock at the brink of death — the "burst of hidden strength in an emergency". Reality offers plenty of examples: the grandfather lifting a truck to save his grandchild, the mother holding up half a pillar through an earthquake for her child. Such things are common enough — but those people commonly die after the burst. Medicine guesses at sudden bodily death; the truth is not far off — the body simply cannot bear a burst beyond its limits, and so death follows…

Note: though called a check, the Gene Lock check is not a check — it is unaffected by anything that raises check results or check DP, unless specially stated. It can, however, be rerolled by abilities that affect non-checks (such as "Legendary CTR").

Note: a Gene Lock can only be opened while the matching rank is not already open, whether the opening is voluntary or not.

Conditions for opening a Gene Lock:

1) Taking, in one instance, Lethal damage equal to or exceeding half your maximum HP.

2) Taking Aggravated damage.

3) A person you treasure, or a teammate, taking fatal damage.

4) Yourself taking fatal damage.

5) Being wholly absorbed in something extremely urgent and important. (ST-optional.)

Supplement 1: no amount of Blunt damage can ever open a Gene Lock.

Supplement 2: while your maximum HP is reduced, compute the conditions from the current maximum.

Supplement 3: no self-harming skill, Technique, or the like can open your Gene Lock — even if it would kill you.

Supplement 4: under no condition may friendly fire — accidental or deliberate — from teammates or allies open your Gene Lock.

Worked examples:

Case 1) A player at 10 HP, all Healthy, takes 10 B damage — no Gene Lock opening.

Case 2) A player at 10 HP, all Healthy, takes 15 B damage; after conversion the final tally is 5L 5B — Lethal damage equal to half their HP in one blow, so the Gene Lock may open.

Case 3) A player at 10 HP already carrying 4L takes another 4L — the single instance falls short of half total HP, so no opening.

Case 4) A player at 7 Healthy 3L takes 5L — the Gene Lock may open.

Case 5) A player at 11 HP, all Healthy, takes 5L — no opening; 6L, though, would open it.

Case 6) A player at 2 Healthy 8L takes 3L; after conversion the result is 9L 1A — Aggravated damage was dealt, so the Gene Lock may open.

Case 7) A player at 4 Healthy 6L takes 6L; after conversion, 8L 2A — over half and Aggravated both, but two conditions still grant only one opening chance.

Case 8) A player at 10 HP, all Healthy, takes 1A — the Gene Lock may open.

**Extraordinary performances:**

A first Gene Lock opening generally carries an extraordinary-performance reward.

An extraordinary performance is the astonishing feat a character pulls off in the grip of unleashed potential, generally chosen from:

Ignoring the gap in power, killing an opponent in one blow. (Depending on circumstances, the ST may downgrade this to "all attack dice count as 10s, no + Dice".)

One action succeeding outright, no check. (Depending on circumstances, the ST may downgrade this to "all check dice count as 10s, no + Dice".)

Negating this instance of damage.

Carrying a gravely wounded teammate out of the enemy's encirclement without a scratch.

One extraordinary performance can only be canceled by another extraordinary performance.

The ST may, per their plot and setting, grant a Gene Lock opener rewards beyond the extraordinary performance — see the "house rules" entry on the "Common Slang" page. The ST may also add to or trim the list of extraordinary performances by plot and setting.

**Gene Lock — Rank One:**

Opening the first rank releases the organism's evolutionary potential. From then on, whenever you need it, you can be a genius in any field. Unlocking the first rank frees the battle instincts of earliest humankind: your INT governs how well you steer those instincts, your PCT how much of them you can call up, and the instincts called up show themselves in your concentration.

**Passive opening:**

Whenever your life — or the life of someone you value even above yourself — stands in utmost danger (see above), you gain one chance to open the Gene Lock. Roll a d10: a result of 10 or higher opens it. Each failed attempt adds +1 to your next Rank One result — Gene Lock proficiency. And while Rank One stays unopened, proficiency rises +1 automatically at the end of each horror film. Without a Gene Lock, you accrue no proficiency.

**Voluntary opening:**

Once you have opened Rank One, you master it: you may open Rank One voluntarily as a Free Action. While open, it costs 1 Will point per round. Voluntary Rank One openings per film cannot exceed your highest Legendary Stat.

**Release:**

Releasing Rank One is a Free Action.

**Effects:**

While Rank One is open, you gain one of the following two effects, chosen when you open it:

1. Every skill you use counts as (Gene Lock rank × 1) levels above its true level — gaining no new AS and no extra effects. You likewise count as holding every Profession of every skill, gaining no Profession Bonuses you don't possess — merely escaping the missing-Profession penalty.

2. On first opening the Gene Lock and choosing this effect, pick one ability from: darkvision; spirit sight; spider climb; immunity to No-Tier disease and toxins; falling immunity; immunity to wound-fainting and deterioration; perfect recall; water-walking. Once chosen it cannot change; from then on, opening this rank with this effect grants the chosen ability. With the ST's leave, you may also invent another ability as your power for this rank.

This is a superpower developed from your own genetic memory, so no negative effect can cancel it.

**Duration:**

You can hold Rank One open for rounds equal to **the lowest of your EDR, CMT, and DTR**. Past that, each extra round costs extra Will equal to the rounds exceeded (the first extra round costs 2, the second 3, and so on) and inflicts 2 unmitigable agony points per round. While maintaining the open state you feel none of the agony or EDR damage — it accumulates, landing the instant you release the lock. But should the agony points reach a ruinous consequence, the open state ends at once.

Agony points from the Gene Lock stack.

**Bonus reward:**

Only on the first successful opening of Rank One should the ST grant the player one extraordinary performance.

**Example:**

Player A, in a Resident Evil film, is first beaten ragged by zombies — conditions met, opening failed. His next attempt gets +1.

Next a Tyrant hammers him — conditions met, opening failed. His next attempt gets +2.

Then the BOSS takes him from full HP to death's door in one move — conditions met, opening failed. His next attempt gets +3.

**Gene Lock — Rank Two:**

The second rank means command of one's body at the cellular level, cutting wasted effort in every motion to the minimum.

**Passive opening:**

From the film after your Rank One opening onward, facing similar situations grants a chance to raise the lock's rank. Roll 2d10: 18 or higher opens the second rank. Each failure adds +1 to the next Rank Two result — proficiency — and while Rank Two stays unopened, proficiency rises +1 at each film's end. Without a Gene Lock, no proficiency.

**Voluntary opening:**

Once opened, mastered: while in Rank One you may open Rank Two as a Free Action. While open, 1 Will point per round. Voluntary Rank Two openings per film cannot exceed your highest Legendary Stat.

**Release:**

Releasing Rank Two is a Free Action. Note that you release every rank outright — there is no falling back from two to one.

**Effects:**

While Rank Two is open you keep Rank One's effect, and gain one of the following three, chosen at opening:

1. Choose one: sensory range +（Gene Lock rank）× 30; base speed +（Gene Lock rank）× 5; Initiative +（Gene Lock rank）× 3. If other effects also act on that derived stat, Rank Two's effect adds last.

2. On first opening this rank with this effect, pick one from: difficult-terrain immunity; immunity to D-Tier disease and toxins; called-shot immunity; flight at base speed (Mobility average); underwater movement at base speed (Mobility average); Rapid Healing 1; blindsense out to sensory range (a D-Tier Natural-Category detection means); invisibility (a D-Tier Natural-Category anti-detection means). Once chosen it cannot change; thereafter this rank with this effect grants that ability. With the ST's leave, you may invent another ability as this rank's power.

3. Your next skill check gains an extra (Gene Lock rank × 2) AS. Specially, choosing this effect ends the Gene Lock state the moment the check resolves.

**Duration:**

You can hold Rank Two open for rounds equal to **the lowest of your EDR, CMT, and DTR**. Past that, each extra round costs extra Will equal to the rounds exceeded (2, then 3, and so on) and inflicts 2 unmitigable agony points per round. The agony and EDR damage accumulate, landing when you release the lock; if the agony reaches a ruinous consequence, the open state ends at once.

Agony points from the Gene Lock stack.

**Bonus reward:**

Only on the first successful opening of Rank Two should the ST grant one extraordinary performance.

**Gene Lock — Rank Three:**

The third rank turns from without to within: it lets you keep developing your own mind's reaches, or simulate the thought patterns of others.

**Passive opening:**

Two films after your first Rank Two opening (not counting the film of that opening), facing similar situations grants a chance to raise the rank. Roll 3d10: 27 or higher opens the third rank. Each failure adds +1 to the next Rank Three result — proficiency — and while it stays unopened, proficiency rises +1 at each film's end. Without a Gene Lock, no proficiency.

**Voluntary opening:**

After you have opened Rank Three, you may open it voluntarily from Rank Two — a Free Action. While open, 1 Will point per round. Voluntary Rank Three openings per film cannot exceed half your highest Legendary Stat.

**Release:**

Releasing Rank Three is a Free Action. You release every rank outright — no falling back to two or one.

**Effects:**

While Rank Three is open you keep the effects of Ranks One and Two, and gain one of the following three, chosen at opening:

1. One of some ability's range, area, or duration briefly rises to (base value × Gene Lock rank / 2). If other effects also act on that derived value, Rank Three's effect computes first.

2. On first opening this rank with this effect, pick one from: immunity to B-Tier disease and toxins; Rapid Healing 2; blindsight out to sensory range (a C-Tier Natural-Category detection means); invisibility (a C-Tier Natural-Category anti-detection means); tremorsense out to rough range; becoming incorporeal; Tri-Protection. Once chosen it cannot change; thereafter this rank with this effect grants that ability. With the ST's leave, you may invent another.
Specially, while this effect runs, the lower ranks' effects are no longer kept.

3. You briefly lose one active skill you own — as though never bought — every effect it brought ending at once. Then choose a skill of equal or lower Tier in a skill tree you own, whose learning conditions you meet, and simulate it as though purchased. When Rank Three releases, everything the simulated skill brought ends at once.

After successfully opening Rank Three, the time you can hold Ranks One and Two doubles.

**Duration:**

You can hold Rank Three for rounds equal to **the lowest of your CMT, DTR, and INT**. Past that, each extra round costs extra Will equal to the rounds exceeded (2, then 3, and so on) and deals 1 INT damage per round. Unlike Ranks One and Two, this drawback bites *while* you maintain Rank Three.

**Notes:**

In no case can Energy Pools' basic uses, or resources received as free inclusions, be simulated.

No skill with limited uses can be lost or simulated by Rank Three.

For research developments, uptiering, and the like: you may pay the resources to gain the developed or uptiered effects, and later simulations retain that developed, uptiered state at no further cost.

**Gene Lock — Rank Four:**

Rank Four splits into initial, middle, and high grades.

**Rank Four — Initial:**

**Passive opening:**

From the film after your first Rank Three opening, facing similar situations grants a chance to raise the rank. Roll 4d10: 36 or higher opens the fourth rank. Each failure adds +1 to the next result — proficiency — and while it stays unopened, proficiency rises +1 at each film's end. Without a Gene Lock, no proficiency.

**Voluntary opening:**

After you have opened Rank Four, you may open it voluntarily from Rank Three — a Free Action. While open, 2 Will points per round. Voluntary Rank Four openings per film cannot exceed a quarter of your highest Legendary Stat.

**Release:**

A Free Action releases all ranks. You cannot fall back from a higher rank to a lower one.

**Effects:**

While Rank Four is open you keep the effects of Ranks One through Three, and gain these two abilities besides:

Command of one's own genes: as a Free Action, spend 10 Willpower to grant one Stat check a temporary 20-point Perfect Bonus. Every target within (that Stat × 10) meters whose matching Stat is below yours feels a crushing pressure, their assessment of your threat rising to maximum — an S-Tier mind effect.

Fine attunement: this power shows itself in control of energy, with two broad uses — commanding your own energy and channeling others'.

When using an energy-costing ability, you may spend an extra amount equal to the base cost (if the base cost isn't fixed, double it once determined). The extra energy brings no other effects and triggers nothing. The ability's area and range double; abilities without area or range are unaffected. If other effects also act on area and range, Rank Four's effect computes last.

If someone shares a General Energy Pool with you, you may spend a Prepare Action on an adjacent target so that their expenditures may draw on your matching energy. Likewise, in the same circumstances, you may spend a Movement Action so that your own General-energy expenditures this turn may draw on an adjacent, willing target's matching energy.

After successfully opening Rank Four, the time you can hold Rank Three doubles — making your hold on Ranks One, Two, and Three equal.

**Duration:**

You can hold Rank Four for rounds equal to **the lowest of your CMT, DTR, and INT**. Past that, each extra round costs extra Will equal to the rounds exceeded (2, then 3, and so on), inflicts 2 unmitigable agony points per round, and every three rounds deals 2 more EDR damage. The agony and EDR damage accumulate, landing when you release the lock; if the agony reaches a ruinous consequence, the open state ends at once.

Agony points from the Gene Lock stack.

**Rank Four — Middle:**

**Voluntary opening:**

Rank Four Initial's proficiency keeps accruing after your success — every passive attempt, success or failure, now adds +1. When you can open Rank Four Initial with full reliability (proficiency +36), you pass beyond the initial grade straight into Rank Four Middle.

**Effects:**

You gain the following:

Erasure immunity: you are henceforth immune to the Lord God's erasure.

Mind barrier: you are immune to every mind-based effect aimed at you — an A-Tier immunity for rulings — though you may accept an effect willingly, and break it at any moment with a Free Action. Unless the effect's user has likewise reached Rank Four Middle, even an ability that pierces mind immunity is nullified by the gulf between Gene Locks.

Your base number of Rank Four openings doubles.

You gain the Light of the Mind — counting as immediately gaining an A+8000 self-created skill, usable only with Rank Four open; when Rank Four ends, everything the Light of the Mind brought vanishes.

Light of the Mind: the Light of the Mind is the signature power belonging to each samsara traveler alone. You define its effect yourself, negotiating with the ST until agreement is reached.

**Duration:**

You can hold Rank Four for rounds equal to **your lowest Legendary Stat × 3**. Past that, each extra round inflicts 2 unmitigable agony points, and every three rounds 2 more EDR damage. The agony and EDR damage accumulate, landing when you release the lock; if the agony reaches a ruinous consequence, the open state ends at once.

**Rank Four — High:**

**Voluntary opening:**

To rise from Rank Four Middle to Rank Four High, the character must pass through their inner demon.

Once per horror film, you may choose to challenge the inner demon.

Challenging it, you enter the phantasm of your own mind while outside time flows in step — however long you spend within, the same passes without. Triggered mid-battle this is desperately dangerous: to the outside world you cannot control your character, though you act on instinct.

The perilous form it takes differs by person: the demon itself is usually woven from the character's Sin and background, and the way through it from the character's Virtue. The ST holds the specific shape and the judgment of passage — designing an independent story drawn from the character's Infinite experiences or backstory, or simply duplicating the character for a perfectly fair duel with themselves.

**Effects:**

Besides keeping everything above, you additionally gain:

You gain divinity — counting as Transcendent Divinity rank 0.

Your Light of the Mind upgrades into an S+16000 skill. Under no circumstances can the Light of the Mind be copied or learned by any other being. Some examples of these powers rendered into numbers:

Hidden Dragon Transformation: (Rank 4 High) — the novel's own protagonist Zheng Zha

Prerequisites: STR 30+, AGI 30+, EDR 30+

Opens for 5 Will; lasts (your EDR) rounds, renewable for 1 Will per expiry, restarting the clock.

Stat surge: gain 10 STR, 10 AGI, and 10 EDR as Perfect Bonuses, counting for certain effects as XP-bought.

Dragon's Wings: gain perfect flight at your base land speed.

Dragon's Scales: gain Born Defense equal to your EDR.

Inch Radiance: (Rank 4 Middle) — the novel's Zhao Zhuikong

Prerequisites: AGI 40+

Opens for 5 Will; lasts (your Will) rounds, renewable for 1 Will per expiry, restarting the clock.

Stat surge: gain 20 AGI as a Perfect Bonus, counting for certain effects as XP-bought.

Always a Step Faster: against attacks, your Base Defense doubles as though you had moved out of the attacker's range — area attacks included. You are immune to flat-footedness, High Speed, and the multiple-attack penalty — an S-Tier effect. And against your attacks, targets are flat-footed — an S-Tier flat-footed effect.

Absolute Speed: you compute your Base Defense from your AGI.

Mirror: (Rank 4 Middle) — the novel's Zhao Yingkong

Prerequisites: mental disorder: schizophrenia

A Free Action on your own turn; 1 Will per activation.

Doppelgänger: summon a double anywhere within your sensory range, its Stats identical to yours, with a full set of actions and the use of all your Techniques and skills. Targets are flat-footed against your double's first attack. At the end of each turn you may keep the double or dismiss it.

**Duration:**

This grade's effects are passive, so there is no duration.

**Gene Lock — Rank Five:**

The Gene Lock's fifth rank is command of energy — the so-called sage's stratum, which in the Infinite Horror setting already requires a "sacred seat" to enter. How it is gained and what it does are the ST's to define, and are not detailed here.

In essence, sacred seats are finite: a being must win one in the story — gaining the matching divine domain or Dao. The most direct route is deicide while holding divinity and the fourth rank, absorbing the god's domain through your own divinity.

Note: though many abilities grant divinity enough to hold a sacred seat, without Rank Four High even towering divinity opens no fifth rank — you could wield such power, but you would not understand it.

**Voluntary opening:**

Only from the state of Rank Four High can the fifth rank be entered.

The exact requirements remain unclear.

**Effects:**

See the Transcendent Divinity rules.

**Duration:**

Unclear.
