# Categories and Bonuses

**Categories and bonus types**

**Categories**

A Category marks the fundamental source of a state, ability, or effect. Depending on circumstances, some environments or effects suppress effects and abilities of particular Categories.

Every exchange carries one of four Categories: Natural, Tech, Eldritch, Fantasy.

**The Natural Category**

Things described as "Natural" occur naturally in reality, or with light handiwork — reproducible with simple hand tools and no electrified equipment.

The great majority of "Natural" things have countermeasures that can be understood, guessed at, or achieved in reality: a natural toxin, say, can often be neutralized by herbs or folk remedies.

In theory at least, samsara-team members can generate Natural items, things, or effects inside their personal spaces — though anything existing in a non-natural way withers and wastes by its own natural law.

Examples of Natural descriptions: snake venom, mineral poisons, herbal medicine, an animal's low-light vision.

In films where technology fails, Natural items keep working. (Powered weapons and modern firearms fail; ordinary swords do not. Cars won't start; bicycles still roll.)

**The Tech Category**

Things described as "Tech" are tools or consumables assembled through science and technology to achieve some effect, explicable wholly or partly by scientific theory. Anything with this Category is necessarily an item — tool or consumable.

Some effects nullify "Tech" tools — the vampire power "tech items fail", say. Sometimes the tool isn't wholly disabled but degraded to the level of a lower-tech counterpart: powered armor turning as useful as Gothic plate.

In theory at least, samsara-team members can build "Tech" items themselves — but most demand deep theory, heavy precision work, and assembly-line labor. A member who would build must therefore hold knowledge, craft, and tools far beyond the ordinary.

Examples of Tech descriptions: every ordinary real-world tool, plus rapid-healing medicine, antidotes, future firearms, cybernetic conversion, refined drugs, the T-virus.

Note: some abilities have technological backstories or causes without being Tech in Category — the Hulk's transformation, say.

In films where technology fails, Tech items cannot work normally. (The Gundam may not start; guns jam; rapid-healing medicine heals nothing.)

**The Fantasy Category**

Things described as "Fantasy" come with magical, fantastical, occult, xianxia, or xuanhuan backgrounds — running on rules of their own, wholly loose from reality. Yet however far from our world's reality, a Fantasy thing still must carry a self-consistent theory of how it works: the spirit-qi of heaven and earth, aether, and so on.

Some effects suppress or dispel certain "Fantasy" effects — the spell "Fire Purge Curse" lifts curse effects, for instance.

The great majority of "Fantasy" things are bought from the Lord God, though some such abilities can be self-cultivated once a member meets the prerequisites.

Examples of Fantasy descriptions: Tomes, the Muscle Dominator, vampire powers, Nature-Transmuting Embodiment.

Since the Tech Category enjoys the advantage of "being manufacturable", for balance's sake the rules do not support films with a "fantasy fails" trait.

Everything of the Fantasy Category counts as supernatural.

**The Eldritch Category**

Things described as "Eldritch" resist explanation by science and fantasy alike; most fit no common notion of magic or spellcraft. They defy science, yet are not fantasy. Certain ancient — or extremely science-fictional — skills are Eldritch.

The great majority of "Eldritch" things are bought from the Lord God, though some such abilities can be self-cultivated once a member meets the prerequisites.

Examples of Eldritch descriptions: Zerg prosthetics, mutants, the Hulk's transformation, the xenomorph's acid blood, mutant powers, the Force, Inner Force, the adept's realms, super-robot conversion.

Note: some abilities run on Eldritch Energy Pools or powers while themselves being Fantasy in Category — the Lesser Celestial Star Sword, say.

Eldritch items cannot be manufactured the way Tech items can; and even where some technological principle lurks inside them, they do not fail in tech-fails films.

Everything of the Eldritch Category counts as supernatural.

Changing Categories

Some effects turn a thing from one Category to another: it loses the old and gains the new. "Nature-Transmuting Embodiment", for example, fuses a Tech tool with a living body by Fantasy means — the tool loses the "Tech" Category and takes on "Fantasy".

**Bonuses, penalties, and their types**

Bonuses:

Some of the Lord God's enhancements grant numerical bonuses, each with its own type. Unless specially stated, multiple same-typed bonuses on one item (a Stat, a skill check, a special-ability check) do not add — the highest applies. Same-typed bonuses across several different items compute separately.

Of the wordings involved, only the first ("obtain") refuses to stack; all others stack even across sources.

Same-typed bonuses from the same source stack with each other, even worded "obtain".

E.g.: a Bloodline's D rank grants attack checks a 3 DP Intrinsic Bonus, and its C rank 6 DP — total 9 DP.

Here "same source" means the thing itself: the Bloodline itself, the Alternation itself, the Eye Technique itself, the Title itself. Items and skill trees hanging under them do not count as the same source.

E.g.: a Bloodline's D rank grants attack checks 3 DP Intrinsic, and the same Bloodline's skill tree grants 6 DP Intrinsic — total 6 DP.

Bonuses split into check-DP bonuses and Stat bonuses — two distinct classes, so same-named bonuses across the two stack.

For example, skill A grants +2 Reinforcement Bonus STR while skill B grants 2 DP Reinforcement Bonus on STR-related checks: the character enjoys 4 DP in total, both being Reinforcement Bonuses. Beyond that, if the Stat bonus pushes STR into new Legendary STR, the new Legendary point applies too.

Same-typed bonuses converging on a single check don't stack even when they sit on different items:

skill A grants 2 DP Reinforcement on STR checks, skill B grants the weapon 2 DP Reinforcement; attacking with STR + Blade + weapon damage, only 2 DP applies.

Stat bonuses, unless stated otherwise, do bring their matching Legendary Stats.

AS effects on a Stat apply only to that Stat's checks, nowhere else.

E.g.: +5 Reinforcement EDR gives the player 5 more HP and 1 Legendary EDR — hence 1 extra AS on EDR-related checks, plus the Legendary EDR's extra HP.

E.g.: +1 EDR AS gives the player 1 extra AS on Fortitude-type checks — no Legendary EDR.

Generally a bonus attaches to one kind of act or its related checks.

E.g.: "3 DP Intrinsic Bonus on STR-related checks" applies to every check made with STR — but if the target substitutes AGI for STR and checks with AGI, the bonus is lost.

E.g.: "3 DP Intrinsic Bonus on attack checks" applies only in acts bearing the word "attack".

Penalties:

Penalties are the special case: Negative Statuses produce them, and some abilities inflict them; some Bloodlines and special powers penalize the enhanced party in some respect. Penalties may carry bonus types too; when a same-typed bonus and penalty coexist:

take the largest bonus and the largest penalty, and both apply.

Bonuses also divide into permanent and temporary:

permanent bonuses are yours for good, with no duration;

temporary bonuses arrive through some ability and have durations — note that even "n/a" or "until dismissed" counts as a duration.

On defense penalties:
A defense penalty reduces the target's total defense, stacking with other defense-lowering abilities, computed favorably. It does not disable abilities the target derives from any given defense, and unless stated otherwise cannot push defense negative.
E.g.: a target with 3 Base Defense and 3 Armor Defense takes a 3-point defense penalty:
1. The attacker has no other defense-lowering means — the target's defense is 3.
2. The attacker has 3 Breach — defense 0, and the armor's 【Bulletproof】 and kin fail.
3. The attacker has 3 High Speed — defense 0, but the armor's 【Bulletproof】 and kin do not fail.
4. The attacker has 1 Breach and 1 High Speed — defense 1, and the armor's 【Bulletproof】 and kin fail.
5. The attacker has 3 Breach and 3 High Speed — defense 0, and the armor's 【Bulletproof】 and kin fail.

BUFFs:

Same-sourced BUFF effects apply to one target or one area only once at a time — higher grades override lower, and among equals the newer overrides the older. Unless stated otherwise, DEBUFFs likewise refuse to stack, their Negative-point payloads included.

E.g.: a resource grants a Perfect Bonus — Perfect Bonuses stack, but one target still cannot carry two instances of that resource's effect.

E.g.: Gengjin Divine Wind's area wind-speed change is a regional BUFF, so it cannot stack on itself — without other wind-changing abilities it shifts the wind at most 1 grade.

Intrinsic:

Intrinsic Bonuses arise from changes in the character's own being. Ranks within one enhancement tree stack their Intrinsic Bonuses — the D-rank and C-rank werewolf Bloodline add together. Otherwise, Intrinsic Bonuses don't stack.

Unless stated otherwise, bonuses from Bloodlines, Alternations, and Eye Techniques are Intrinsic.

Practicing:

Practicing Bonuses come of training a skill or ability. Items within one enhancement tree stack their Practicing Bonuses; otherwise they don't stack.

Reinforcement:

Reinforcement Bonuses may arrive from anything, generally attaching to a Stat, a class of check, or some narrow item — some factor has simply strengthened one facet of you. A Reinforcement Bonus counts as part of whatever it strengthens.

Reinforcement Bonuses don't stack.

Machinery / Assistive:

Machinery Bonuses generally come from a tool, attaching to some skill check — the tool is well designed, capable, a pleasure in the hand. Conversely, an ill-fitting tool imposes a Machinery Penalty.

Machinery Bonuses don't stack; Machinery Penalties stack without limit.

Speed:

Speed Bonuses generally arise from sheer swiftness, attaching to Physical checks and defense.

Speed Bonuses don't stack; Speed Penalties stack without limit.

Morale:

Morale Bonuses may attach to anything, reflecting a stirred spirit. Morale Bonuses and Penalties are both mind-affecting effects — the penalty a negative one.

Mindless units, being immune to mind-affecting effects, are immune to Morale Bonuses and Penalties alike.

Neither Morale Bonuses nor Penalties stack.

Performance:

Performance Bonuses usually flow from your fine showing, granting skill bonuses or defense — your excellence made visible.

Performance Bonuses don't stack.

Technique:

Technique Bonuses generally attach to attack, defense, or other combat items, arriving from special abilities you use or maintain.

Technique Bonuses don't stack.

Base Defense:

Base Defense is the character's basic response to attack — parrying, dodging.

Unlike other bonuses, Base Defense always stacks.

Evade:

Evade bonuses generally attach to defense or to checks resisting area effects — nimble weaving left and right.

Unlike other bonuses, Evade bonuses and Evade penalties both stack.

For historical reasons, in this book "Evade Defense" and "Evade bonus" name the same bonus: Evade only ever attaches to defense, so an Evade bonus on defense may be shortened to "Evade Defense".

Born Defense:

Born Defense attaches only to defense — thick hide, iron muscle, unbreakable bone.

Two wordings: "add X Born Defense" stacks; "obtain X Born Defense" doesn't.

Armor Defense:

Armor Defense attaches only to defense — you wear protective gear.

Armor Defense doesn't stack.

Shield Defense:

Shield Defense attaches only to defense — guarding with a shield or similar weapon.

Shield Defense doesn't stack.

Insight:

Insight Bonuses generally attach to defense or some check — superb observation that sees where the trouble lies and slips past it.

Two wordings: "add X Insight Bonus" stacks; "obtain X Insight Bonus" doesn't.

Guard:

Guard Defense attaches only to defense — turning the enemy's blows with weapon or bare hands.

Two wordings: "add X Guard Defense" stacks; "obtain X Guard Defense" doesn't.

Environmental:

Environmental Bonuses generally attach to defense or some check — an external condition for or against you.

Environmental Bonuses don't stack; Environmental Penalties stack without limit.

Luck:

Luck Bonuses may attach to anything — heaven happens to be on your side.

Luck Bonuses don't stack.

Field:

Field Bonuses generally attach to defense or some check — a Field effect is assisting some facet of you.

Field Bonuses don't stack.

Energy:

Energy Bonuses generally attach to defense or attack — your strikes or your guard ride on charged energy, the mightier for it.

Energy Bonuses don't stack.

Deflection:

Deflection Bonuses generally attach to defense and to checks resisting area effects — attacks and hostile matter partly miss your body, whether because you aren't quite where you seem or because space itself bends.

Deflection Bonuses don't stack.

Holy:

Holy may attach to anything — the Holy Bonus stands for devotion to benevolent gods, to morality, to love.

Holy Bonuses don't stack.

Unholy:

Unholy may attach to anything — the Unholy Bonus stands for demon-worship, power drawn from sin, rites of blood.

Unholy Bonuses don't stack.

Covered:

Covered Bonuses may attach to defense and to checks resisting area effects — it must state which; unstated, read defense. It means something stands between you and the danger.

Covered Bonuses don't stack.

Unnamed:

Some special abilities and equipment declare no bonus type; these we call Unnamed Bonuses.

Unnamed Bonuses don't stack.

Profession:

Profession Bonuses come from the "Professions" you learn, attaching to the matching skill checks — a class of work sits easily in your hands.

Profession Bonuses don't stack unless specially stated.

Feat:

Feat Bonuses come from the "Feats" you learn, attaching to certain skills or special events — you are simply very good at this.

Feat Bonuses don't stack unless specially stated.

Size:

Size Bonuses are the bonuses and penalties your changing Size brings.

Whatever the sources of the Size changes, the changes generally carry these bonuses and penalties —

and they all stack.

RP:

The RP Bonus is a special one: the bonus a PC earns for a peerless real-world performance — the player's own conduct moved the ST.

RP Bonuses stack with every bonus type, other RP Bonuses included, across sources, even on a single check — the ST's recognition made numerical.

Perfect:

This ruleset's special bonus type — your incomparable determination, from an incomparable source.

Perfect Bonuses stack with every bonus type, other Perfect Bonuses included, across sources, even on a single check.

Every bonus falls into one of the classes above.

Stacking summarized:
Same-typed bonuses from one source — D-rank and C-rank of one Bloodline, say — stack by default, unless the bonus reads "instead", which doesn't. Transformations and their kin follow the same clause.
Same-typed bonuses from different sources — generally don't stack; only the rarest cases do.
The same source and rank bought multiple times — by default doesn't stack unless specially stated.

Note: the following are not bonuses, and all stack by default:
Initiative, Size, speed.
Note: the following are also not bonuses, stacking or not per their own wording:
High Speed, Breach, Dispelling.
