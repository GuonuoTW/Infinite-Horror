# Miscellaneous Rules

**Overlapping explosions and cluster-explosive rules**

Where several explosives' blast areas overlap, take the highest single damage in the overlap as the baseline; sum the other area damages, and each time the sum reaches the baseline, the overlap's damage rises 1 point — the baseline rising with it. The total damage gained this way cannot exceed five times the highest same-kind area damage in the overlap other than the original baseline.

Binding explosives into a cluster explosive computes damage under the same overlap rules. To build one, make an **INT + Crafts–Explosives** check at DC equal to the explosive's expected damage. If the check turns up more 1s than 10s, the charge detonates, dealing damage equal to the successes accumulated so far.

E.g. 1: Bomb-Dominator Crow detonates two yaoqi bombs at once; each deals 4, so where their blasts overlap, actual damage is 5. With three such bombs at once, the base damage of 4 falls short of the raised baseline, so the overlap still deals 5.

E.g. 2: Bomber Gokudera lashes several grenades into a cluster bomb. Grenades deal a base 4L, so he needs at least two to make a cluster dealing 5L; he must then supply 6L of combined explosives to raise it again — binding four grenades, two grenades' summed base damage is 8, so the cluster's damage climbs to 6L.

**Improvised weapons**

Objects never meant as weapons — tables, stools — pressed into service. Such improvised weapons generally deal damage equal to the lower of their Hardness and Size, attack at a 2 DP Machinery Penalty, and deal Blunt damage only — special cases (a smashed beer bottle) being the ST's call.

**Flight Mobility**

Clumsy: takeoff and landing each need a Movement Action. Each 45° turn needs a Movement Action, with (own Size) meters flown between turns; at least half speed must be flown per round or you fall. Maximum climb angle 30°. In this state your Base Defense drops to a quarter.

Average: each 90° turn needs a Movement Action, with (own Size) meters between turns. Maximum climb 45°; at least half speed per round or you fall. Base Defense drops to half.

Good/agile: each 180° turn needs a Movement Action; you automatically gain the "Circling" Feat at level 1. Maximum climb 60°. Base Defense drops to three quarters.

Perfect: turn freely, hover, fly backward, or climb at full speed. Full Base Defense retained.

Special flight actions:

Hover: costs no action, holding still in midair. Only perfect Mobility or a levitation ability allows hovering.

**Speed set to a fixed value**

When a resource fixes your speed at a set figure — 777 meters per round, say — your maximum distance per Movement Action becomes half that figure (round down), compared against your normal speed and taking the higher; the two never stack.

Nor do speed-boosting effects touch such a speed — it isn't your base speed.

**"Counts as raised" effects**

Some resources read "counts as gaining X points of Stat" or "the skill counts as raised X levels".

Stats and skills raised in this counted-as fashion grant their DP as usual, bring new AS as usual, and flow into derived effects computed from them — a counted-as INT rise boosts casting checks, and shapes the Occult-scaled parts of spells (range, damage, duration).

But counted-as Stats and skills never satisfy prerequisites for casting or learning spells.

And counted-as skills, unless stated otherwise, cannot break 15.

**Defining Size**

Size in play is measured in 40 cm base units: every 40 cm adds 1 point of Size.

E.g.: Size 1 is 0–40 cm, Size 2 is 41–80, Size 3 is 81–120, Size 4 is 121–160, Size 5 is 161–200, and so on.

Put differently, a weapon of about Size 3 gives adjacent-1-meter reach; at Size 4 or above reach gains 1 meter, and past 6 further still.

Likewise, a weapon said to add 2 meters of reach can be pictured at about Size 5.

Broadly, Size abstracts a creature or object to a cube and measures its longest edge —

so under the Infinite Horror rules, a grain of rice, a ping-pong ball, and a basketball are all Size 1.

An object's Size also shifts with its state: a wound spool of sewing thread is Size 1, but unwound and pulled straight it might run 5 meters — 5/0.4 = 12.5, so Size 13.

**Creature Size adjustment rules**

An ordinary adult is Size 5; only the matching Feat or Defect raises it to 6 or lowers it to 4.

Some abilities and effects change a character's Size, and such supernatural change works on the flesh itself: greater bulk makes a character stronger, tougher, longer-strided — and easier to hit.

Size change has these effects:

- The character's base speed depends on Size.
- The character's maximum HP depends on Size.

Stat shifts from Size:

- Each 2 points of Size above 5 grants 1 DP Size Bonus on STR-related checks, imposes 1 DP Size Penalty on melee to-hit checks, and raises the melee damage cap by 1. For meeting equipment STR requirements the character counts as STR +1, likewise for encumbrance; the character also gains 1 Born Defense and takes a 1-point penalty across Base and Evade Defense (Evade reduced first), never pushing either negative.

- Each 2 points of Size below 5 imposes 1 DP Size Penalty on STR-related checks, grants 1 DP Size Bonus on melee to-hit checks, and lowers the melee damage cap by 1. For equipment STR requirements the character counts as STR −1, likewise for encumbrance; the character gains 1 Evade Defense and loses 1 Born Defense, never pushing Born Defense negative.

Note: only permanent Size gains and losses bring the adjustments above — temporary Size changes gain no Size adjustment.

**Size change and items**

Items do not change with their owner's Size.

Change Size far enough and an item may stop working:

Weapons and tools: if character and weapon/tool differ in Size by more than 5, each point beyond imposes a stacking 2 DP Machinery Penalty on checks using it, for the poor fit — stacking with Machinery Penalties from unmet prerequisites. Past a total of 6, as with prerequisites 3 short, the weapon or tool is unusable.

Armor and shields: if character and armor/shield differ in Size by more than 5, each point beyond imposes an extra 2 points of Armor Penalty, or strips 2 Shield Defense, for the poor fit. Past 6, as with prerequisites 3 short, the armor or shield is unusable.

E.g. 1: a STR-1 player "simply cannot use" a Size-4 kite shield (the STR-requirement Machinery Penalty reaches 6).

E.g. 2: a STR-3, Size-3 dwarf also "simply cannot use" that kite shield sized for a Size-5 wearer (a STR-requirement penalty of 4, plus a fit-derived guard penalty of 2).

Odd cases are the ST's call — a dagger two Sizes too big serving penalty-free as a greatsword (Size 3), say.

Unless stated otherwise, items in the rules are sized for Size-5 users.

**Climbing large creatures**

Facing a creature more than double your Size, you may forgo the grapple check and cling to it with a climb check instead: roll STR + Athletics (Climbing) against its AGI + Athletics (Balance). Success means you're aboard, acting further per your margin — a margin above 3 lets you take Standard Actions as normal. Fail, and you're flung off: roll 1d8 for direction, flying a distance set by your margin of failure.

On its turn the target may shake you loose with a Movement Action — or, if it moved this turn, a Swift Action — rolling AGI + Athletics (Balance) against your STR + Athletics (Climbing). If it wins, you're flung off: 1d8 for direction, distance by your margin of failure.

**Compressed Size**

When Size is squeezed artificially —

two people wedged into a cramped space each count as Size −1, and −1 more per extra person;

squeezing along a narrow passage computes Size from the passage's width.

In these cases, Size reduced by 1 costs 1 Evade Defense or Base Defense; reduced by 2, all Base and Evade Defense is lost.

Absent special abilities, no one can force their own Size down by 3 or more.

**Rolling one check several times, keeping the best or worst**

Some abilities have players roll a single check several times, keeping the highest or the lowest.

In such cases, tally the counts with plain addition and subtraction:

E.g. 1: "roll 3, keep best" plus "roll 2, keep best" — subtracting the base roll, that's 2 extra plus 1 extra = 3 extra: roll 4, keep best.

E.g. 2: "roll 2, keep best" plus "roll 2, keep worst" — 1 extra minus 1 extra = 0: roll once.

**Identity-binding contracts**

Contract abilities that fix identities between people (master-and-servant bonds, say), unless stated otherwise, allow one per character, the later replacing the earlier.

E.g., two parties signed under Gandálfr cannot also sign a Servant contract with anyone else (see the Heroic Spirit Alternation) — neither as Master nor as Servant.

Exceptions may be spelled out: someone confirmed as Servant in a Servant contract may still take the Master's side of another Servant contract, gaining a Servant of their own — the Servant contract itself provides for it. Even so, they cannot sign anyone else's Gandálfr contract.

**Resource upgrades**

A note on later upgrades along a resource's branch paths: without words like "additionally", "further", or "on top of the original", identical effects replace rather than stack.

And where a resource reads "choose one D-Tier skill/spell to gain", unless stated otherwise you may also pick a DD-Tier one.

**NPC disposition toward you**

How to quantify NPC goodwill in play, and what they do at each grade of it — the quantified table:

| Disposition | Meaning | Likely actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hostile | Trading their life for yours would be a bargain | Attacks you, obstructs you, denounces you |
| Unfriendly | Makes everything go wrong for you so long as they stay safe | Misleads in conversation, gossips behind your back, suspects you first, insults you, flees you |
| Wary | Holds some suspicion toward you | Withholds important matters (without lying), avoids you deliberately |
| Indifferent | Doesn't care about you | Ordinary social interaction |
| Friendly | Mildly well-disposed | Gladly shares information and opinions, small favors |
| Warm | Helps you as far as their interests allow | Friendly exchange, talks you out of reckless or vile acts, limited help, backs you when needed, heals you out of combat, includes you in group buffs |
| Devoted | Would give their life to help you | Protects you in combat, supports your acts (however mad or vile), heals you mid-combat, spends combat time single-target-buffing you |
| Obedient\* | Beasts only — complete tameness | Nuzzles you, plays with you, bares weak points and taboos, lets you ride |

**Follow-up strikes**

When an ability lets you repeat checks off its own check result or the like,

everything that modifies the check touches only the first check's numbers — no bonus recomputes across the follow-ups.

**Attacking each target in an area separately**

Using such abilities, you may still only attack targets you have located.

If the attack remains single-target in essence, it still cannot affect swarm creatures.

**Time-rewinding abilities**

Time rewinds cannot restore:

1. Spent consumables.

2. The uses of limited-use abilities and items.

3. Exchanges.

4. Spent XP.

**Independent entities belonging to a player**

Summons, doubles, artificial intelligences, and AIs with bodily structures of their own (Haro, say), unless stated otherwise, get one Swift, one Movement, and one Standard Action per round like a normal character. Without an Initiative statistic or special note, they act on their master's Round, before or after it as the master chooses.

Summons summoned by summons belong to the earliest summoner, so caps, counts, Energy Pools, and everything similar compute from the original summoner; energy and uses the summons spend all draw on the master's pools and counts.

Your own summons never count as enemies under any circumstances — even one attacking you.

Ordinarily a summon leaves no corpse on death or vanishing, and everything it carried (BUFFs included) vanishes with it, unless stated otherwise. And no matter what, nothing on a summon can serve as prerequisite or material for your abilities.

You cannot satisfy any condition through interaction with a summon or through its state — true love, "a friendly target dies", and so on — unless specially stated.

Specially, since a double is your own double, it cannot be the target of copying, duplication, or mimicry, nor join any faction, organization, or system.

Whatever a double's current state, abilities that read or scan its data (Stats, skills, and the rest) return its owner's original numbers.

A double's death, vanishing, or dispelling triggers no on-death ability — even passive ones.

A double inherits every state active on the original — buffs, Negative Statuses, all of it — durations included, though inherited durations run separately.

Unless stated otherwise, doubles cannot use Willpower's basic use.

Unless stated otherwise, doubles cannot use active abilities.

Unless stated otherwise, a double shares the original's creature type.

Unless stated otherwise, threatening acts by your independent entities are still your acts — if you hold an ability whereby A cannot attack you while you don't attack A, and you order a willed creature of yours to attack A, that act is yours and the creature's together, and A may attack you as normal.

Unless stated otherwise, doubles inherit none of your Gene Lock, Light of the Mind, or anything Gene Lock-related — proficiency and unlocking checks included.

**Substituted checks**

Sometimes a check's dice components are replaced by something else.

E.g.: Blade attacks roll STR + Blade + weapon damage; gaining "you may substitute AGI for STR on Blade attack checks" makes the roll AGI + Blade + weapon damage.

Unless stated otherwise, the substitute cannot stand in for the original's derived values and derived content, nor be substituted a second time.

E.g.: substituting AGI for STR on the check does not let AGI set the damage cap.

E.g.: if DMR substitutes on attack checks, DMR cannot then substitute for INT on casting attack checks.

**"Lasts 1 round"**

A special note: owing to resolution timing, any "lasts 1 round" ability activated after this round's resolution point has already passed does not end at the start of next round — it ends immediately after triggering once instead.

E.g.: you gain Rapid Healing 3 for 1 round, mid-round, after your resolution point has passed. It cannot fire this round; by "lasts 1 round" it would end before your next round begins — but having never triggered, the effect instead lingers until it triggers once, and then its duration ends.

**Extra HP bars**

Resources include the likes of "use maximum Willpower as a second HP bar" or "use mana as a second HP bar". Activating such a trait builds a new HP maximum from the current Willpower maximum / Mana pool (or matching pool) maximum — the Secondary HP. From then on, spending Willpower or mana (or the matching energy) does not affect the Secondary HP.

**Amplification**

Some resource abilities allow amplification.

Amplification covers the Amplify cost, effect, and cap, each generally spelled out in the ability.

Some abilities strengthen amplification, e.g.:

"If the ability can Amplify, it counts as already Amplified X points (times)": treat as pre-Amplified; below the cap you may keep Amplifying, but the cap doesn't change, and further steps build on the amplified state. (If the 1st Amplify costs 1 mana and the 2nd costs 2, being pre-Amplified once means the next costs 2.)

"If the ability can Amplify, the Amplify cost drops X": Amplify as normal, waiving X of the extra cost.

"If the ability can Amplify, the cap rises X points (times)": the cap gains X points — or possibly X uses.

Some abilities cost more than 1 energy per Amplify step: a character pre-Amplified 3 points whose steps cost 2 mana each pays nothing for the first step and 1 for the second.

**Copying abilities**

Resources may include abilities that copy others' abilities. Unless stated otherwise, casting a copied ability still demands you meet its usage conditions — holding the same energy type that drives the Technique or skill, and its prerequisites: without the Nanaya bloodline you cannot copy the Nanaya body technique; not being a vampire, you cannot copy vampire powers; without Sword Cultivator at C-Tier, you cannot copy the sword move "One Step Ends the Mortal Dust". For spells, you must watch the full casting from start to finish and yourself hold a matching caster role of equal rank; and whatever the original's uptiering and research, you learn only the earliest version. Limited-use abilities cannot be copied at all.

A copied ability can serve as no other ability's prerequisite, nor earn you anything other abilities grant through it.

**Damage riders**

Some abilities attach effects scaled to the damage dealt. In such cases count only the original damage — extra damage and damage from other abilities are excluded.

E.g.: with "if you damage the target, inflict burn points equal to the damage", dealing 3 damage plus 2 extra damage inflicts 3 burn points.

**Techniques**

A Technique is any purchased ability requiring active activation — inner methods, movement arts, effects attached to an attack action, Standard-Action-activated skills, and more.

Activating a Technique generally defines that attack's mode; duration-type Techniques (inner methods, movement arts) specify their bonuses.

Wherever a resource says "make a basic attack", the attack can carry no Technique.

Special: passives are not Techniques.

**Prerequisites**

When gaining an ability requires a prerequisite:

lose the prerequisite, and everything the ability brought — creature type, skills, Stats, Energy Pools, altered appearance, all of it — is lost until the prerequisite is met again. The slot it occupied stays occupied.

For purchase and usage prerequisites, temporary Stats never count — items being the one exception, which do count temporary Stats.
E.g.: a character with base AGI 1 wearing a +2 AGI weapon cannot enhance an Alternation requiring AGI 3, but can use an item requiring AGI 3.

Temporary conditions imposed by the Lord God don't count as losing a prerequisite.

E.g.: an exchange requires Chinese nationality; dropped into a story world, the player is assigned American nationality — the exchange doesn't lapse; its prerequisite is temporarily waived.

For odd prerequisites on odd resources, the ST rules as fits.

E.g.: the Starship Troopers exchange requires male anatomy; when a player takes a groin kick, or loses said anatomy inside a horror film, the ST rules by the table's mood and the situation.

**Self-harm abilities**

Some resources carry "deal yourself XX unavoidable damage" or "inflict XX unavoidable Negative Status points on yourself" — self-harm abilities.

These buy their bonuses and effects with self-inflicted wounds or states; unless stated otherwise, the unavoidable damage, the matching penalties (gravity bracelets), and the states (Negative Statuses, mania) can be neither immunized nor offset by any means of your own —

no "ignore the first X points", no mitigation; however mighty your immunities, you take the penalty or damage exactly as the skill describes. If a skill's activation cost is damage that would kill you, the prerequisite counts as unmet and it cannot activate.

**On conflicts**

E.g.: a weapon classified both "tachi" and "long spear"; the character knows both "City-Burning Spear Art" and "Shinmei-ryū".

City-Burning Spear Art: gain the 【Long Spear】 Profession on Blade; using the art requires a piercing polearm classified as spear or lance, checked with STR as the key Stat.

Shinmei-ryū: wielding tachi-Profession weapons, whatever their Size, you must substitute AGI for STR on Blade checks, unless the weapon itself forbids it.

The two abilities now conflict — and in that case no Blade check can be made at all.

Handle similar collisions the same way: the conflicted action simply cannot be taken.

**Teleportation**

Teleportation is generally a space ability.
Unless stated otherwise, teleporting frees you outright from grapples and from physical restraints and bindings.
If the teleport has a somatic component, first judge whether the restraint permits the posture; if not, the ability cannot fire.
The destination must generally be open ground unoccupied by other objects; otherwise the teleport fails or lands you in an adjacent square — specifically the nearest to the original spot, chosen at random among ties.
Unless stated otherwise, teleportation requires line of effect and line of sight as usual.

**Gaining AS equal to some value's AS**

In such cases you gain all of that value's current AS.

E.g.: "your STR checks gain AS equal to your Will checks'" — when rolling STR, tally all current Will-check AS and take that many.

**Lifespan**

A natural human lives about 70 + 1d20 years. Counting a character's enhancements, each D Tier adds 5 years of natural lifespan, and each ability that changes their creature essence adds 10 (becoming a creature of darkness, say).

**Recomputing skills**

1. Bonuses from the same skill take the higher.
2. One trait or skill computes only once per resolution chain — even if mid-chain state changes would let it slot in again, it doesn't recompute; and once the chain resolves, its participants cannot open a fresh chain off those state changes. Example: trait A adds Legendary INT to PCT as a Perfect Bonus; trait B adds Legendary PCT to INT as a Perfect Bonus; skill A grants an INT Perfect Bonus that raises Legendary INT. Resolve skill A, then trait A; if trait A raises Legendary PCT, resolve trait B; if trait B would allow resolving trait A again — it doesn't compute.
Specially, players choose the traits' firing order, but the skill that triggers them must head the chain.
