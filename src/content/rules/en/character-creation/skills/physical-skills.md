# Physical Skills

Detailed skill descriptions:

Note that everything detailed here covers the Natural-category cases.

Note that for weapons (pistols, swords, etc.), the matching Profession is the specific weapon — there is no such Profession as "Blade attack" or "Brawl attack" (throwing is covered below).

===

**Athletics: walking, running, jumping, throwing weapons — movement of every form.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Thrown-weapon attacks: AGI + Athletics (Light Throwing Weapons), AGI + Athletics (Throwing Weapons), or STR + Athletics (Heavy Throwing Weapons)**

See the "Battle Rules" page.

**Reflex Saves:**

**Reflex check: AGI + Athletics (Self-Preservation)**

Note: because self-preservation is a human instinct, Reflex Saves take no missing-Profession penalty even without the "Self-Preservation" Profession.

**Ray attacks:**

**Ray attack: AGI + Athletics (Ray)**

Normally unobtainable through the Natural category — gained through supernatural abilities; see the granting ability for specifics.

**Breath attacks:**

**Breath attack: AGI + Athletics (Breath)**

Normally unobtainable through the Natural category — gained through supernatural abilities; see the granting ability for specifics.

**Shooting:**

**Energy: AGI + Athletics (Shooting)**

Normally unobtainable through the Natural category — gained through supernatural abilities; see the granting ability for specifics.

Resources that name an Athletics Profession of "energy shooting" mean this Profession.

**Overrun: AGI + Athletics (Overrun) or STR + Athletics (Overrun)**

See the "Battle Rules" page.

**Balance:**

**Balance: AGI + Athletics (Balance)**

Description: keeps the character steady when walking a rope, a narrow beam, a rock ledge, or uneven ground.

Balance checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs the check 1 DP.

Action: none. A balance check isn't its own action; it happens as part of another action or as a reaction to a situation.

Use: the character can walk on unstable ground.

On a success, the character moves on that surface at half speed for 1 round.

If successes double the DC, the character moves at full speed for 1 round.

On a failure, the character cannot move for 1 round.

On a failure with successes below half the DC (round down), the character falls prone.

DC depends on the surface:

| Narrow surface | DC | Rough ground | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 30–40 cm | DC 1 | Slick or icy | DC +1 |
| 10–30 cm | DC 2 | Obstructed (loose gravel, natural ground) | DC +1 |
| Under 10 cm | DC 3 | Sloped or angled | DC +1 |

If attacked in the turn your balance check failed, you count as No-Tier flat-footed. And if that attack hits, you must make another AGI + Athletics (Balance) check; failing it, you fall.

Also, a double move or a charge requires passing two checks.

**Jumping:**

**Jump: STR + Athletics (Jumping)**

Description: leap over pits and fences, reach low-hanging branches, or jump up to grab things.

Jump checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs the check 2 DP.

Action: none. A jump check is contained in movement, as part of a Movement Action. If the character exhausts their speed mid-jump, their next action (Standard Action, or next turn's Movement Action, and so on) must complete the jump with the remaining movement.

Jump checks relate directly to speed — the run-up.

Before jumping, the character may move in a straight line: each meter moved grants 1 DP Reinforcement Bonus on the coming jump check, capped by the character's STR.

Without a run-up, the jump check's final successes are halved.

Abilities reading "your jumps count as having a run-up" (or similar) mean the jump's final successes are not halved even without a run-up.

Jump movement counts inside the round's maximum movement:

E.g., a character who moves 20 m per round runs 15 m and jumps 3 m — 2 m of movement remain.

If they jump 5 m, they exactly exhaust one Movement Action's speed and stop there.

If they jump 10 m, they exceed one Movement Action's maximum; if they still have a Standard Action this turn it must downgrade to a Movement Action to settle the movement — otherwise they lose next round's Movement Action to complete the jump, counting as "airborne" in between.

Before takeoff, the character can judge the landing point — generally anything with INT 2+ can. Doing so, first roll INT + Athletics (Jumping): each success may shave 1 success off the jump check to adjust distance.

Jumps come in three kinds — high jump, long jump, and jumping down:

High jump: a vertical leap, to reach or grab something above. Height: 0.4 m × successes.

Long jump: a horizontal leap across gaps, streams, rooftops. At the apex, height above ground is (distance / 4). Distance: 1.6 m × successes.

Jumping down: use the falling rules.

**Climbing:**

**Climb: STR + Athletics (Climbing)**

Use: scale cliffs, buildings, ropes, and the like.

Climb checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs the check 1 DP.

Action: climbing is movement, usually part of a Movement Action, so it can combine with other movement into one Movement Action. Each climb resolves separately.

E.g., a character climbs 3 m up a tree, leaps 2 m to another tree, and climbs 3 m more; with a 10 m Movement Action all of this fits in one action — but the two climbs are two separate checks.

Special: stopping yourself mid-fall, or grabbing a falling target, costs no action — meaning you can catch a falling target outside your turn, though it takes a Reflex Action.

Use: move on slopes, walls and other inclines, trees, rock faces.

On a success, move at 1/4 base speed for 1 round.

If successes double the DC, move at 1/2 base speed for 1 round.

On a failure, no movement for 1 round.

On a failure with successes below half the DC (round down), the character falls.

Bonuses and penalties by situation:

| Surface | DC | Special situation | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Rope, ladder, etc. | DC 1 | Two parallel walls available | DC −2 |
| Some holds, e.g. railings | DC 2 | Two perpendicular walls available | DC −1 |
| Hands only; natural wall, rope | DC 3 | Slippery surface | DC +1 |
| Sheer and uneven; brick wall | DC 4 |  |  |
| Feet dangling, hands only, with holds | DC 5 |  |  |
| Flat, perfectly smooth | DC 10 |  |  |
| Feet dangling, no holds, perfectly smooth | DC 20 |  |  |

A climbing creature must hold its posture: it loses Evade Defense and cannot guard.

Climbing generally needs both hands, but clinging to a surface needs only one — freeing one hand for one-handed actions.

Special:

A character with a climb speed moves on any surface with no checks at all.

A character with spider climb moves on any solid surface at base land speed with no checks, loses no defense, and may guard as normal.

If damaged while climbing, the character must make a climb check at the surface's DC or fall from that spot.

With proper tools or abilities, a character can carve holds or footings into a surface; the altered surface counts as having holds, changing the DC per the table.

Stopping mid-fall:

Make a climb check at double the local climb DC.

Catching a falling target:

First make a melee Brawl touch-attack check against the falling target; a hit means you've grabbed them. Failing this check means no grab, and the target keeps falling.

Then make a climb check at DC +2. Success means you hold the target and steady yourself. Failure means you caught them but couldn't hold on — the target keeps falling.

If you fail that check with successes under half the total DC, you fall together with the target.

Also, if grabbing the target brings your load to maximum, the climb check automatically fails.

**Escape:**

**Escape: AGI + Athletics (Escape)**

Wriggle out of bonds, break grapples, squeeze through tight spaces, and so on.

Action: escaping tangible restraints — ropes, shackles — is a 1-minute check. Escaping grapples and pins is a Standard Action. Squeezing through tight spaces depends on their length. For escapes in special resources, see the resource.

If you attempt escape while watched, roll AGI + Larceny (Theft) against the target's PCT + Investigation (Scouting); fail, and you are noticed.

**Swimming:**

**Swim: STR + Athletics (Swimming)**

Lets land creatures without underwater mobility swim, dive, and act under water.

Underwater is deeply unfriendly to most characters — most cannot breathe there, let alone move freely.

Underwater terrain is less varied than land, but the seabed holds many oddities; one location can mix many terrain features.

Swim checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs the check 3 DP.

Action: swimming is movement, usually part of a Movement Action, combinable with other movement.

Use: the character can act in water.

On a success, move at 1/4 base speed; under water this counts as clumsy Mobility as if in air.

If successes double the DC, move at 1/2 base speed, counting as good Mobility.

On a failure, no movement for 1 round, and you lose Base Defense and Evade Defense.

On a failure with successes below half the DC (round down), you sink below the surface, losing all actions, Base, Evade, and Guard Defense.

A character below the surface follows the suffocation (drowning) rules.

Note: an ability that lets you breathe under water does not grant free underwater movement — swim checks still apply.

| Water conditions | DC | Environmental modifier | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Calm | DC 2 | Holding position or heading upstream in slow current | DC +3 |
| Rough | DC 3 | Holding position or heading upstream in rapids | DC doubled, then +3 |
| Storm | DC 4 |  |  |

Water terrain divides in two:

Still water: any waterway flowing under 5 m — ordinary rivers, lakes, seas. Only normal swim checks are needed; current has no effect.

Moving water: fast currents — flow of at least 6 m per round. Two kinds:

Slow current (6–25 m per round): holding position or moving upstream costs a swim check 4 successes. Success resolves normally; failure means being swept away.

Rapids (over 25 m per round): holding position or moving upstream halves the check's successes, then costs 3 more. Success resolves normally; failure means being swept away.

Swept away: the character is forced downstream at the current's speed. In rapids, next round they must spend a Standard Action on a STR + Athletics (Swimming) check at DC 3 after halving successes; success steadies them, otherwise they lose the full turn's actions and are swept on.

Each hour of swimming past the first forces a Fortitude check at DC 4; failure inflicts fatigue points equal to the shortfall, which accumulate toward ruinous consequences. The DC rises 1 per 2 hours.

**Brawl: unarmed fighting — fists and feet, martial arts of every school.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Brawl attacks:** see "Battle Rules" for specifics.

**Attacking with hand/arm natural weapons: STR + Brawl (Hands)**

Note: for version reasons, wherever resources name a Profession for brawl attacks using "palm", "fist", "claw", "elbow" — anything of arm or hand — that means the "Hands" Profession.

**Attacking with leg/foot natural weapons: STR + Brawl (Legs)**

Note: likewise, "leg", "kick", "foot" and anything below the hip means the "Legs" Profession.

**Attacking with other natural weapons — teeth, wings, etc.: STR + Brawl (matching limb)**

E.g. teeth: STR + Brawl (Teeth); wings: STR + Brawl (Wings); tentacles: STR + Brawl (Tentacles); tail: STR + Brawl (Tail).

**Brawl grapple:**

**Grapple: STR + Brawl (Grapple)** — see "Battle Rules".

**Brawl slam:**

**Slam: STR + Brawl (Slam)** — see "Battle Rules".

**Brawl trip:**

**Trip: STR + Brawl (Trip)** — see "Battle Rules".

**Attacking specific body parts:**

**Brawl attack: STR + Brawl (Called Shots)**

A very special Profession: instead of adding DP, it ignores 1 point of called-shot penalty, and it works alongside other Professions.

E.g. attacking with STR + Brawl (Hands, Called Shots) at a target's eyes: you gain the "Hands" Profession's 1 DP bonus and ignore 1 point of the eye-shot penalty.

Special: abilities that change your Profession Bonus from 1 DP to something else do not affect the "Called Shots" Profession.

**Brawl guard:**

**A defensive act — no check.**

A very special Profession: instead of adding DP, it grants 1 extra point of Guard Defense when you guard.

Special: abilities that change your Profession Bonus from 1 DP to another value also change this Profession's extra Guard Defense to that value.

**Firearms: from pistols to heavy machine guns — using, maintaining, and repairing guns of every kind.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Firearm attacks:** see "Battle Rules" for specifics.

**Because firearms have their own item classifications, Professions map one-to-one onto them:**

**Pistol attacks: AGI + Firearms (Pistol)**

**Machine-gun attacks: AGI + Firearms (Machine Gun)**

**Rifle attacks: AGI + Firearms (Rifle)**

**Cannon attacks: INT + Firearms (Cannon)**

**Crossbow attacks: AGI + Firearms (Crossbow)**

**Firearm aiming:**

**A very special Profession: instead of adding DP, it speeds your aiming.**

When you aim, your first round of aiming counts as 2 rounds — your first aiming round gets +1.

Note only the first round becomes 2; later aiming is unaffected.

Special: abilities that change your Profession Bonus from 1 DP do not affect "Firearm Aiming".

**Called shots:**

**Firearm attack: AGI + Firearms (Called Shots)**

As with Brawl: ignores 1 point of called-shot penalty and stacks with other Professions; unaffected by Profession Bonus changes.

**Firearm guard:**

**A defensive act — no check.**

Normally, guarding with a firearm in hand uses your Blade guard, so this Profession usually means nothing. But if any ability lets you guard using your Firearms skill, this Profession grants 1 extra Guard Defense when doing so.

Special: abilities that change your Profession Bonus from 1 DP to another value change this extra Guard Defense to match.

**Larceny: everything from defeating security systems to picking locks to pickpocketing.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Lockpicking:**

**INT + Larceny (Lockpicking)**

Open locks with keyholes and keys.

Note that picking requires proper tools at hand (chisels, pry bars, key blanks, wire...).

Time is usually written on the lock; if not, it is a Whole-round Action.

The DC is usually written on the lock.

Beyond the basic use, this skill can also open traps or tamper with a device —

touching it, starting it, or letting it run briefly and then fail or break after a while (usually 1d4 rounds or minutes after use).

Action: a disable-device check's time depends on the task. A simple device takes one round — a Whole-round Action. A more complex one takes 1d4 or 2d4 rounds.

Success: you alter the device's state as you intended.

Failure: the device keeps running as designed.

Failure with successes under half the DC (round down): possible mishaps — a ruined keyhole, a sprung trap, an undrivable car — ST's call.

| Task difficulty | Comparable situation | DC | Time |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Simple | Jamming a lock | DC 1 | 1 round |
| Harder | Sabotaging a car engine | DC 2 | 1d4 rounds |
| Difficult | Hot-wiring a car | DC 3 | 2d4 rounds |
| Nearly impossible | Opening a bank vault door with precision tools | DC 10 | 1d4 minutes |

Note: this skill cannot open electronic locks, fingerprint locks, iris locks, or computer-controlled devices.

**Theft:**

**AGI + Larceny (Theft)**

Steal others' belongings.

Action: usually a Standard Action; or take a 5 DP penalty to make the AGI + Larceny (Theft) check as a Free Action.

| Situation | DC | Environmental modifier | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unattended | DC 2 | Item Size 1 | DC +1 |
| Off someone's person | DC 4 | Item Size 2 | DC +2 |
|  |  | and so on | and so on |

If the stolen item's Size exceeds half your Size or the target's, all your theft successes are halved.

If it exceeds your Size or the target's, the theft automatically fails.

If the item is held by the target, or is armor, a helmet, shoes, or gloves being worn, the theft automatically fails.

When you make this check, everyone in a position to notice may roll PCT + Investigation (Scouting) to catch the act. Their check doesn't hinder you — it only decides whether they notice.

If your current state has a high anti-detection level and the target cannot detect or locate you, they may still use this check to notice something missing — but even on success, they won't know you took it.

Note: whether or not the theft succeeds, if the target's PCT + Investigation (Scouting) beats you, they still notice something off about your movements.

(Also: the ST may unconditionally stop you from stealing from teammates.)

**Concealing items:**

**INT + Larceny (Concealment)**

Hide things you'd rather not show on your person and carry them somewhere.

Action: hiding takes preparation — a Standard Action; or take a 5 DP penalty to do it as a Free Action.

Roll INT + Larceny (Concealment) and record the successes.

When someone frisks or searches you, they roll PCT + Investigation (Search/Scouting); if their successes meet or beat yours, they find what you hid.

| Item size | Modifier |
| --- | --- |
| Size under 1 | check +1 AS |
| Size 1 | none |
| Size 2 | check −1 success |
| Size 3 | check −2 successes |
| and so on |  |
| Searcher touches your body | searcher's check +2 AS |

If the hidden item's Size exceeds half your own, all concealment successes are halved.

If it exceeds your Size, concealment automatically fails.

But if you have any ability like "you may hide XX items of Size X or below on your person", ignore the above Size requirements and modifiers.

**Binding:**

**INT + Larceny (Binding)** — see the "binding rules".

**Stealth: moving, shadowing, and hiding without being noticed.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Hiding:**

**AGI + Stealth (Hiding)** — see "Detection and Locating" for specifics.

Hiding checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs 1 DP.

Action: generally, hiding is a Movement Action.

Use: duck behind cover or concealment so others cannot find you.

Hiding requires cover or concealment.

A successful hide counts as a No-Tier, Natural-category anti-detection measure.

If other characters already have their attention on you, you cannot hide — unless you distract them with somewhere nearby to hide, or slip behind cover where they can't see you.

**Sneaking:**

**AGI + Stealth (Sneaking)**

Approach targets silently, or steal away unnoticed.

Sneaking checks suffer the Armor Penalty: each point costs 2 DP.

Action: sneaking is movement, usually part of a Movement Action.

Sneaking is aimed at particular targets: declare one or more targets, or sneak with respect to a direction.

As with hiding, you cannot sneak while a target's attention is on you.

Your sneaking check resists the targets' PCT + Investigation (Listening) and PCT + Investigation (Scouting). Win, and the sneak succeeds; otherwise it fails.

A target of your sneaking may re-contest at the start of each of their rounds while you are in their detection range.

While sneaking, your base speed halves. Refuse the speed cut and your sneaking successes are halved.

Some environments hamper sneaking; see the environment rules.

When a sneaker successfully acts against a target, the target is flat-footed — a Natural-category, No-Tier flat-footedness. If it is the first round of combat, the sneaker springs a surprise round. If several mutually-aware sneakers act together, they all enter the surprise round.

**Survival: living outdoors — finding food and shelter, avoiding beasts, resisting toxins and disease, and tracking.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Wilderness survival:**

**PCT + Survival (Wilderness)**

Track, hunt, lead a party safely across an icefield, tell whether owlbears live nearby, predict weather, avoid quicksand and other natural hazards.

Action: variable. A single survival check may take hours or a whole day; a track-finding check takes at least a Whole-round Action, possibly longer.

Each task in the wild is a separate check; common ones:

| Wilderness task | DC | Time | Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Find north/south/east/west | DC 1 | 1 whole round | none |
| Avoid man-eating bogs or quicksand | DC 2 | passive check | none |
| Forecast weather within 24 h | DC 3 | 1 whole round | none |
| Guide the way | DC 3 | 10 minutes | failure = lost |
| Find food and water | 1 ration per success | a full day | half speed all day |
| +2 DP on Fortitude vs. harsh weather (fire and warmth) | one person per success | 1d4 hours | half speed for the duration |

**Fortitude Saves:**

**Fortitude check: EDR + Survival (Self-Preservation)**

Note: since self-preservation is instinct, Fortitude Saves take no missing-Profession penalty even without the Profession.

**Tracking:**

**PCT + Survival (Tracking)**

Follow a target's trail.

Action: none.

While tracking, every 2,000 m requires a PCT + Survival (Tracking) check; success continues the pursuit, failure ends it.

Also, at the ST's call, whenever the trail becomes hard to read — overwritten by other tracks, deliberately erased, doubling back, smudged — a fresh check is needed.

Generally trails lie only on the ground, so you must stay on the ground to track.

A flying creature hardly leaves a trail.

While tracking, your base speed halves. Refuse the cut and your tracking successes halve.

Adjust the DC by:

| Ground | DC | Size effect | Adj. | Environment | Adj. |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unusually soft (snow) | DC 1 | Size 1 | DC +2 | Per 5 extra people tracked | DC −1 |
| Soft (mud) | DC 2 | Size 2–3 | DC +1 | Per 5 days since the trail | DC +1 |
| Firm (grass) | DC 3 | Size 4–6 | unchanged | Per 5 h of rain since | DC +1 |
| Hard (rock) | DC 4 | Size 7–10 | DC −1 | Target covering tracks (half speed) | DC +1 |
|  |  | Size 11–15 | DC −2 | Dim light | DC +1 |
|  |  | and so on | and so on | Fog or rain | DC +2 |
|  |  |  |  | Covered by fresh snow (soil) | DC +2 |
|  |  |  |  | Darkness | DC +5 |
