# Social Skills

Detailed skill descriptions:

In play, how do you quantify an NPC's disposition toward players? What will they do at each level of friendliness? Below is the quantified disposition table.

NPC disposition affects several Social checks, so the table lives here:

| Disposition | Meaning | Likely actions |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hostile | Trading their life for yours would be a bargain | Attack you, obstruct you, denounce you |
| Unfriendly | Makes everything go wrong for you so long as they stay safe | Misleads you in conversation, gossips behind your back, suspects you first in any incident, insults you, flees from you |
| Wary | Holds some suspicion toward you | Withholds important things in conversation (without lying), deliberately avoids you |
| Indifferent | Doesn't care about you | Ordinary social interaction |
| Friendly | Mildly well-disposed | Happy to share information and opinions, offers small favors |
| Warm | Helps you as far as their own interests allow | Friendly exchanges, talks you out of reckless or vile acts, limited assistance, backs you when needed, heals you outside combat, includes you in group buffs |
| Devoted | Would give their life to help you | Protects you in combat, supports your actions (however mad or vile they sound), heals you in combat, spends combat time single-target-buffing you |
| Obedient\* | Only beasts reach this level — complete tameness | Nuzzles you, plays with you, exposes weak points and taboos to you, lets you ride |

Note that everything detailed here covers the Natural-category cases.

**Animal Ken: understanding animals' habits and feelings, getting along with them, and training them.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Mounted combat:**

**Mounted combat: CTR + Animal Ken (Riding), a.k.a. (Mounted)**

A special Profession: once gained, all your mounted-combat checks gain a 1 DP Profession Bonus, and this bonus stacks with other Profession Bonuses.

Without it, every skill check you make in mounted combat takes the missing-Profession penalty.

E.g., attacking with Blade while riding: besides the normal Blade Profession Bonus, this Profession adds 1 more.

**Training animals:**

**Training: CTR + Animal Ken (Beast Training)**

Train animals to do work: pulling carts, fighting, standing guard, and so on.

Action: a periodic check with a period of days. You must stay with your animal companion while checking.

You must teach your animal companion specific commands before it will execute them.

Command DCs are below; note an untaught animal will not perform the command:

| Command | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Combat: knows how to fight; you control it as you would a character | DC 40 |
| Guard: stays in place to keep creatures away, or prisoners in | DC 30 |
| Carry: light loads need no training; medium and heavy do | DC 20 |
| Track & search: sweep an area, follow lingering scents, chase enemies | DC 30 |
| Fetch: send the animal away from you to retrieve an item | DC 30 |
| Protect: guard a target | DC 30 |
| Perform: tricks — rolling, sitting, hoop-jumping; each is trained separately | DC 20 |
| Follow & come: follows you, comes, goes, and stays on command | DC 20 |

Commands only work from the master. To let others command your companion, multiply the command's training DC by 1.5; each further ×0.5 lets one more person give that command.

There is no cap on commands learned —

but each additional command raises the next one's DC: add the DCs of all previously learned commands (excluding multi-person surcharges) to the new command's own DC. For multi-person commands, only the base-DC portion multiplies.

Example: teaching Combat is DC 40; letting 2 extra people command it in combat makes DC 80. Teaching Guard afterward costs DC 40 + 30 = 70; if Guard should also obey 2 extra people, DC 40 + 30×2 = 100.

Note: all animal companions and mounts exchanged in the Lord God Space count as knowing every command, but obey only the exchanger (their master); letting them obey other characters still requires normal training.

**Improving an animal's attitude:**

**CTR + Animal Ken (Beast Tongue)**

Rules follow the "Negotiation" section, with every "Socialize (Negotiation)" replaced by "Animal Ken (Beast Tongue)",

plus these changes:

Domestic animals generally start Indifferent, wild creatures Wary, and hungry large predators Hostile.

For supernatural animals, the DC rises with Tier —

a simple version: +1 DC per D Tier the supernatural animal holds.

If a supernatural animal has high intelligence, use Socialize instead.

Also, with ST permission, PCs may use Animal Ken on beings reduced (for whatever reason) to animal intellect —

say, a person rendered feeble-minded by a spell.

**Empathy: reading others' feelings and motives and resonating with them — while being more easily moved by their moods.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Steadfastness**

**Steadfast check: higher of DTR/CMT + Empathy (Steadfastness)**

Used to resist a target's negotiation, persuasion, intimidation, and bellowing checks; see the "Negotiation", "Persuasion", "Intimidation", and "Bellow" rules.

Note: everyone has their own stance and attitude, so Steadfastness checks take no missing-Profession penalty even without the Profession —

but without it, roll the lower of DTR and CMT instead.

**Reading people**

**Reading people: PCT + Empathy (Reading People)**

Judge a target's emotions, state of mind, and the like.

Action: reading a target generally requires normal interaction with them for over a minute (10 consecutive Whole-round Actions); seeing through a lie told to you, however, takes no action.

You can tell whether a target is under charm, mind control, or similar effects — such supernatural effects constrain behavior, so they can be recognized. The DC is the ability's casting-check successes, or its Will check if it had no casting check.

You can also read the target's current mood at DC 3 — success reveals their state of mind: fear, tension, excitement, dejection, and so on.

You may also oppose a target's Bluff check with Reading People; see the "Bluff" rules. Since everyone has some instinct for guarding against deception, Reading People takes no missing-Profession penalty — but if you hold the Profession and win the contest against a Bluff, you clearly perceive the attempt to deceive you.

Note that reading people is normally one-shot: one check per conversation, event, or target (unless the topic changes or an unrelated conversation begins).

**Expression: familiarity with a performing art — oratory, music, song, and so on.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

Expression is a sub-categorized skill; sub-skills don't affect one another, and "all-Professions" abilities cannot affect it.

Without the matching sub-skill you cannot make an Expression check, unless stated otherwise.

Learning Expression means picking a sub-skill at the same time, then leveling it —

written "Expression–(sub-skill)".

Level the matching sub-skill; check with the matching sub-skill.

E.g. Expression–Performance 2 and Expression–Oratory 5: speeches check at Oratory 5, playing music at Performance 2.

A check calling only for "Expression" without naming the skill uses your highest sub-skill.

Expression's sub-skills:

Music:

**Singing:**

**Singing: DMR + Expression–Singing**

Singing uses the singer's own vocal cords and requires the mouth, so beings without speech cannot use it; while singing, all language-related abilities are unusable — occupied.

**Performance:**

**Performance: DMR + Expression–Performance**

Playing an instrument requires the hands — usually both — so hand actions are unavailable while playing; some instruments also occupy the mouth (a trumpet, say), in which case language-related abilities are all occupied too.

Note: to save resources we don't split orchestral, percussion, etc. — all instruments count as Performance.

Movement:

**Dance:**

**Dance: DMR + Expression–Dance**

The dancer performs with their body — whole-body movement. Hands don't usually count as occupied, but no posture-based abilities can be used while dancing — occupied.

**Physical performance:**

**Physical performance: DMR + Expression–Physical Performance**

Performing with body or craft — mostly whole-body, sometimes partial: stage magic and acrobatics both live here. Generally, a physical performer cannot use posture-based abilities.

Speech:

**Oratory:**

**Oratory: DMR + Expression–Oratory (Recitation)**

Performing through speech requires the mouth, so beings without speech cannot use it; while performing, all language-related abilities are occupied.

Note: to save resources, everything speech-based counts as Expression–Oratory — stand-up and crosstalk included.

Note: music and speech must carry by sound; movement must be seen.

The types above cover nearly every ability in the resources; where a resource doesn't specify, the ST assigns the sub-skill.

Also, you can always perform for renown. These are Natural-category performances, checked with CTR + Expression–(matching sub-skill). Time runs from one act to a full day, ST's call. Reference DCs:

| Result | DC |
| --- | --- |
| A dreadful show — busker-at-best | DC 1 |
| An ordinary show — some fun in it | DC 3 |
| A good show — professional grade | DC 5 |
| A stunning show — the best of the professionals; award-winners at most | DC 7 |
| An extraordinary show — one for the history books | DC 10 |

**Intimidation: using physical violence or psychological threat to frighten others into following your orders.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Intimidate**

**Intimidate: CTR + Intimidation (Intimidate)**

Temporarily change a target's attitude through fear and fright — at the cost of your long-term relationship.

Action: changing someone's attitude takes at least a minute (10 consecutive Whole-round Actions); special situations may take much longer, ST's call. You may also make a hasty intimidate check as one Whole-round Action, losing half your successes.

Use this check to temporarily shift a target's attitude: roll CTR + Intimidation (Intimidate) against the target's Steadfastness. Success raises the target's attitude toward you to Friendly temporarily (no change if already above Friendly) for one Scene — or drives the threatened person or object away.

The "Friendly" attitude shows only in the target's behavior while intimidated — their true attitude hasn't changed, but for the duration they help you as though Friendly.

When the duration ends, the target's original attitude immediately drops 1 step.

E.g.: a target starts Wary; a successful intimidate makes them temporarily Friendly; when it ends, their attitude falls to Unfriendly.

Note: to coerce, you must have capability enough to threaten with (at least in the target's eyes) — a gun or weapon on a civilian, a hostage, and so on. Without adequate leverage, your intimidation successes halve.

With adequate leverage, you may gain an Environmental Bonus per your circumstances, ST's call.

Additionally, if the target carries fear points tied to you, you may intimidate; each such fear point grants your check 1 AS.

Since intimidation works through the target's fear, fear-immune targets are immune to intimidation, and mindless creatures cannot be intimidated.

**Bellow**

**Bellow: STR + Intimidation (Bellow)**

Terrify opponents mid-combat and crack their confidence.

Action: a Standard Action in combat.

Combat only.

Roll STR + Intimidation (Bellow) against the target's Steadfastness. Win, and the target gains the Fear Negative Status at your margin of victory. You can only threaten one enemy you can reach in melee, and they must be able to see you.

If the target holds the Steadfastness Profession, fear points from this ability tick down 1 per turn automatically; without it, they save against the fear normally.

Even on success, a target can only be so intimidated: repeats take the higher, never stack.

Fail, and bellowing the same target again this battle has no effect.

Since it works through fear, fear-immune targets are immune, and mindless creatures cannot be bellowed at.

**Socialize: most skills of the silver tongue — closing deals, fast talk, enticement, agitation, persuasion, and more.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Negotiation:**

**Negotiation: CTR + Socialize (Negotiation)**

Change others' attitudes toward you by way of the tongue.

Action: at least one minute (10 consecutive Whole-round Actions); may take much longer, ST's call. A hasty check as one Whole-round Action takes a 5 DP Unnamed Penalty.

A successful negotiation check changes an NPC's attitude; the target resists with Steadfastness, whose successes add to the DC for your final result.

Generally, negotiation works only once per NPC per matter — most people have bottom lines: however sweet your pitch, attitudes shift some fixed amount, not forever.

Likewise, a failed check may harden — even sour — the target's stance toward you or the matter.

| Initial \ New attitude | Hostile | Unfriendly | Wary | Indifferent | Friendly | Warm | Devoted |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Hostile | – | DC 4 | DC 7 | DC 10 | DC 13 | DC 16 | DC 20 |
| Unfriendly | – | – | DC 3 | DC 6 | DC 9 | DC 12 | DC 15 |
| Wary | – | – | – | DC 3 | DC 6 | DC 9 | DC 12 |
| Indifferent | – | – | – | – | DC 3 | DC 6 | DC 9 |
| Friendly | – | – | – | – | – | DC 3 | DC 7 |
| Warm | – | – | – | – | – | – | DC 5 |
| Devoted | – | – | – | – | – | – | – |

Hostile: will risk harm to hurt you — attacks, obstruction, abuse, flight.

Unfriendly: wants harm and misfortune upon you — misleads, gossips, avoids, watches with suspicion, insults.

Wary: somewhat suspicious — withholds important matters in conversation, avoids you in action.

Indifferent: cares nothing for you; a stranger.

Friendly: wishes you well — chats, advises, helps within limits, sticks up for you.

Warm: will take risks to help — protects, supports, heals, assists.

Devoted: would die to help you — rescues you at mortal risk, shields you, gives support.

Strangers ordinarily start "Indifferent" toward you.

Special circumstances change the DC —

different factions, different colors, looks, tastes, faiths on either side; the ST adjudicates.

Because TRPGs are roleplaying games, we'd rather players act than just throw dice — this rule is a reference for the ST, who may at any time set it aside and demand honest roleplay.

Others can assist a negotiation check — but a helper must stand at Friendly or above with the negotiation's target to assist under the "assistance rules".

**Persuasion:**

**Persuasion: CTR + Socialize (Persuasion)**

Temporarily change someone's view of a particular matter by way of the tongue.

Action: at least one minute (10 consecutive Whole-round Actions); may take much longer, ST's call. A hasty check as one Whole-round Action takes a 5 DP Unnamed Penalty.

Persuasion targets a single matter: state your demand when checking — "drop the weapon", "release the hostage", "surrender". The target resists with Steadfastness; if you win, the target does as asked.

Each charm point the target holds toward you grants your check a 1 DP Unnamed Bonus, and the target's attitude adjusts the difficulty:

| Target's attitude toward you | DC effect |
| --- | --- |
| Hostile | DC +15 |
| Unfriendly | DC +11 |
| Wary | DC +7 |
| Indifferent | DC +3 |
| Friendly | DC −1 |
| Warm | DC −3 |
| Devoted | DC −5 |

Note: your demand must not damage the target's interests — or must promise greater gain if obeyed. You cannot persuade targets into the absurd — suicide, emptying their pockets into your hands — unless you produce genuinely convincing reasons.

The ST may raise or lower difficulty according to the demand.

Special circumstances change the DC — factions, looks, tastes, faiths; ST's call.

Note: TRPGs are roleplaying games — we encourage acting over dice; the ST may set this rule aside at any time and demand honest roleplay.

You may also compete with others to persuade the same target toward different choices — you and A each urging B to kill the other, say. All would-be persuaders make persuasion contests against the target; among those who win, the highest margin prevails.

**Gathering information**

**Gathering information: CTR + Socialize (Information Gathering)**

Learn local rumor, spread rumors, or collect general intelligence through your channels.

Action: a typical check takes 1d4+1 hours.

In a socially fertile setting — a tavern, a nightclub, a public square — roll CTR + Socialize (Information Gathering) to pick up recent local news or chase specific intelligence:

| Sought information | Base DC | Special situation | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| News everyone knows | DC 1 | Wrong species | DC +2 |
| Specific information (directions, getting a map) | DC 2 | No common language | DC +3 |
| Specific and uncommon (an item's provenance) | DC 4 | Older news | +1 DC per week old |
| Known only to particular people (the guards' shift times) | DC 7 |  |  |
| Secrets (a drug deal) | DC 10 |  |  |

Note: information gathering is not a periodic check — fail, and you may simply start over.

If your successes run high, distribute them (rounded down) across the hours spent; once a unit of time holds enough for the DC, you finish early.

E.g.: you want a map to Grey Vale, base DC 2. Your check scores 4 successes; the time roll (1d4+1) gives 2 hours — 2 successes per hour, so the first hour already suffices, and you can stop at 1 hour, map in hand.

You may chase the same news repeatedly: restart the check, paying the time each attempt. Pressing repeatedly after one class of information may, however, draw attention.

**Subterfuge: hiding yourself and deceiving others — honeyed words and grand deceptions.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Feint: INT + Subterfuge (Feint) (see the combat chapter)**

**Bluff**

**Bluff: CTR + Subterfuge (Bluff)**

Make falsehood sound true — the art of deceiving.

Action: deceiving someone takes at least a minute (10 consecutive Whole-round Actions); may take much longer, ST's call. A hasty check as one Whole-round Action takes a 5 DP Unnamed Penalty.

Roll CTR + Subterfuge (Bluff) against the target's Reading People. Success makes the target act on your intended deception for a short while (usually within 1 round); a masterly lie may last longer.

E.g.: convince a woman there's a cockroach at her feet so she vacates her spot; or convince someone their house is on fire so they rush home to fight it.

E.g.: you want to close with someone and sucker-punch them — success means they don't see the attack coming, granting your assault a surprise round.

Bluffing accomplishes two things: the target believes the lie and acts, and there is the action itself.

E.g.: you trick a target into believing they left their belongings at the tavern, so they go search it.

The searching is the action; the believed lie is that their things are at the tavern.

When the search ends empty-handed, the target stops that action — but may well conclude the item is still at the tavern, merely unfound.

That is: the deceived behavior has ended, but the target remains deceived.

So a bluff's duration splits in two:

Truth time — when the target discovers they were deceived.

Action time — when the target finishes the act the deception set them on.

Either way, the moment the target learns the truth — action finished or not — the deception breaks at once, and they stop acting on it.

Difficulty depends on the lie and your relationship with the target:

| The lie | DC adjustment | Attitude | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| The target wants to believe | DC −1 | Hostile | DC +3 |
| Plausible, costs nothing | DC ±0 | Unfriendly | DC +2 |
| Against common sense, slight cost | DC +2 | Wary | DC +1 |
| Hard to believe, real risk | DC +4 | Indifferent | DC +0 |
| Beggars belief, huge risk | DC +7 | Friendly | DC −1 |
| Beyond imagining, flatly impossible | DC +12 | Warm | DC −2 |
|  |  | Devoted | DC −4 |

Your mark must have the INT to understand you — against the mindless or the near-mindless (beasts), bluffs automatically fail.

Ordinarily, after a failed bluff that leaves the target unsuspicious, you may keep bluffing until they grow suspicious or run out of patience.

Special circumstances change the DC — factions, looks, tastes, faiths; ST's call.

Note: TRPGs are roleplaying games — we encourage acting over dice; the ST may set this rule aside at any time and demand honest roleplay.

**Disguise (impersonation)**

**Disguise: INT/CTR + Subterfuge (Disguise)**

Change your own or another's appearance.

Action: one disguise takes about 1d6 × 10 minutes.

The character can impersonate a specific person; you may disguise yourself or someone else.

When disguising, roll INT/CTR + Subterfuge (Disguise) and record the successes, adjusted for the disguise's difficulty.

While disguised, you must contest anyone paying attention to you with reason to question your identity; they roll PCT + Empathy (Reading People) or PCT + Investigation (Scouting). Lose, and the disguise is pierced.

E.g.: disguised as an officer, entering a watchtower — a guard who knows (or has even glimpsed) that officer may contest. A soldier who has never seen the officer contests your cover story (impersonation of a type), not the disguise.

Typically a target checks immediately on meeting you, then hourly. Multiple different creatures each check separately.

Beyond the base contest, difficulty adjusts:

| Special situation | Adjustment |
| --- | --- |
| Posing as the opposite sex | disguise successes −2 |
| Posing as another humanoid species | disguise successes −2 |
| Changing age bracket (youth–adult–middle-aged–elderly) | −2 successes per bracket crossed |
| The checker's attitude toward the person you pose as | starting from Indifferent, −3 successes per step away |

Disguise won't get you past iris scanners, fingerprints, or anything demanding the actual person — you've only changed your face.

Generally, a person carries unique tokens of self beyond mere identity:

in a company, employees walk the halls freely, but only the chairman enters the chairman's office.

Getting into the building needs a cover check; getting into the chairman's office needs a disguise check (barring stealth, of course).

The difference: disguise changes your face — it may pass you through doors that open on a face; a mere cover cannot.

Special: disguise requires this Profession to check at all — without it, no check.

Note: this is Natural-category disguise, not illusion; illusion-piercing abilities cannot pierce it.

Usually a disguised character is also maintaining a cover, so targets may contest disguise and cover separately — one success on either, and you're exposed.

**Cover (posing)**

**Cover: INT/CTR + Subterfuge (Cover)**

Pose as a member of some group rather than a specific person.

Action: about 1d2 × 10 minutes.

Cover applies only to yourself: voice, bearing, clothes changed to look like some group — a Wehrmacht soldier, a fanatic believer — not a specific person.

While covered, you must contest anyone paying attention with reason to question you; they roll PCT + Empathy (Reading People) or PCT + Investigation (Scouting). Lose, and the cover is blown.

Since you aren't posing as an individual, one successful cover usually holds against a given target with no re-checks — unless you slip and rouse suspicion.

Beyond the base contest, difficulty adjusts:

| Special situation | Adjustment |
| --- | --- |
| Posing as the opposite sex | DC +2 |
| Posing as another faith, religion, or faction | DC +1 |
| Posing as another humanoid species | DC +2 |
| Changing age bracket (youth–adult–middle-aged–elderly) | DC +1 per bracket |
| The checker's familiarity with the group | ST's call |
| No common language | DC +5 |

Cover cannot pass you into places or events restricted to specific persons — you changed your affiliation, not who you are.

Generally, a person carries unique tokens of self beyond identity:

employees walk the company freely; only the chairman enters the chairman's office.

The building takes a cover check; the chairman's office takes disguise (barring stealth).

The difference: disguise changes the face and may open face-controlled doors; cover cannot.

Note: this is a Natural-category cover, not illusion; illusion-piercing abilities cannot pierce it.

Usually a disguised character also maintains a cover, so targets may contest each separately — one success on either, and you're exposed.
