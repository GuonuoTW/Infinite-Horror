# Mental Skills

Detailed skill descriptions:

Note that everything detailed here covers the Natural-category cases.

===

**Academics: philosophy, history, literature — broad knowledge of every kind.**

A note up front:

Player and character are two entirely different entities, so their knowledge differs — a PC who is a computer expert in real life may be playing a boxer. Real-world knowledge the player carries into the game may not fit the character's definition.

Some STs encourage players to solve problems with their own experience; others want PC roleplay to hew as closely as possible to the character sheet. Most STs sit between the two. Players and ST must settle this together.

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Document interpretation:**

**Interpretation: INT + Academics (Interpretation)**

Decipher codes, the writing on treasure maps, mysterious murals, hieroglyphs, and the meaning of ancient texts.

Action: varies with the document's length.

Use: decode hidden information.

The character may attempt to read messages in wholly unfamiliar scripts, codes composed in known scripts, or to restore incomplete information.

| Document | DC | Variations | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Simple message | DC 4 | Matching language Feat at level 5 | DC −1 |
| Difficult message | DC 6 | No matching language Feat | DC +2 |
| Complex, bizarre message | DC 8 | Document 10% damaged/illegible | DC +1 |
|  |  | 20% damaged | DC +3 |
|  |  | 30% damaged | DC +6 |
|  |  | and so on | and so on |

Each success in this check represents 1 round spent interpreting. If the character doesn't reach the DC, interpretation fails — but the time is spent all the same.

On a failure with successes under half the DC (round down), the character may misread the document's meaning (ST's call).

**Knowledge:**

**Knowledge: INT + Academics (matching knowledge Profession)**

Knowledge is the study of a discipline; because Science exists separately, all Professions under Academics are "humanities" learning.

Action: generally none.

Use: obtain information in the matching field, e.g.:

Geography: soil, geology, terrain, climate, culture, animals, seasons, and the like.

History: royalty, myth, religion, war, colonization, dynasties, martial arts, and the like.

Players can gain information through knowledge checks.

This is an active check, and the player must hold the field's Profession to attempt it —

a character without the "Geography" Profession cannot roll INT + Academics (Geography) to learn anything about geography.

| Knowledge | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Simple questions (common knowledge) | DC 2 |
| Ordinary questions (fundamentals) | DC 3 |
| Hard questions (genuinely difficult) | DC 4 |
| Obscure questions (specialist matters) | DC 6 |

If a knowledge check fails, you cannot re-check the same matter.

**Monsters**

**Knowledge: INT + Academics (Monsters)**

Learn about the monsters of myth and film.

Action: generally none.

Use: identify monsters common to films and myth via Academics, learning their special abilities or weaknesses.

The DC equals the square of the monster's Tier (D 1, C 2, B 3, A 4, S 5, SS 6).

On a success, the PC learns one piece of information about the monster — a weakness, a trait, habits (behavior patterns), Stats, background, etc.

For every additional (monster's Tier) successes beyond the DC, learn one more piece.

E.g.: a C-Tier monster, check scores 10 successes; base DC is 4, 6 over — three more pieces of information.

===

**Computer: full command of modern state-of-the-art computers and their systems and software.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Hacking:**

**Hacking: INT + Computer (Hacking)**

Break into others' electronic devices.

Note: for version reasons, "intrusion" in rules and resources means "Hacking".

Action: unless stated otherwise, a 15-minute periodic Resisting Check, both sides maintaining Focus throughout.

Use: intrude on another's computer to achieve particular ends —

using one computer to break into another electronic device, to interfere with or influence it: shutting it down, surveilling it, taking control of it, and so on.

To hack a device, the two devices must be connected, wired or wireless; otherwise no intrusion is possible.

Ordinary devices come with firewalls; unless stated otherwise, firewall level equals the square of (the device's Tier + 1).

When you make an intrusion check, the target device's user may roll INT + Computer (Firewall).

If your successes are more than double the target's, the target never notices your intrusion.

If your successes exceed the target's but not doubly so, add the target's successes to the firewall when judging whether the intrusion succeeds.

Once the target notices your intrusion — successful or not — they may immediately launch INT + Computer (Hacking) against you under the same rules: counter-hacking.

Hacking and counter-hacking checks may run simultaneously.

Note: devices generally have power switches, so the moment one shuts down, intrusion or counter-intrusion is cut off — final result by ST's ruling.

**Firewall:**

**Firewall: INT + Computer (Firewall)**

Prevent others from hacking your devices.

Usage is described under "Hacking"; there is one more use besides —

Building a firewall:

You can build a firewall for your own device with this skill.

Roll INT + Computer (Firewall); the successes replace the device's original firewall level as its new level.

Each success in this check costs 1 day of work.

Each of your devices needs its own firewall.

**Gaming:**

**Gaming: INT + Computer (Video Games)**

Clear video games, or beat opponents in PVP games.

Action: each game has its own play time.

Single-player: a fixed DC set by the ST; a successful check clears the game. Each failure costs one full play-through of time.

PVP: both sides roll INT + Computer (Video Games) as a Resisting Check; the winner takes the game. A tie is a draw.

For multiplayer, sum each team's participants' successes and compare.

**Using computers (programming, controlling devices, etc.):**

**Programming: INT + Computer (Computer Use)**

Operate electronic devices.

Usage is generally described per device; no details here.

**Crafts: familiarity with a craft, livelihood, or art — alchemy, armorsmithing, weaponsmithing, gem-cutting, vehicle-building, and so on.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

Crafts is a sub-categorized skill; sub-skills don't affect one another. Because of this peculiarity, "all-Professions" abilities and the like cannot affect Crafts.

When making a Crafts check without the matching sub-skill, you cannot check at all, unless stated otherwise.

When learning Crafts you must pick a sub-skill at the same time, and only then level it —

written "Crafts–(sub-skill)".

Level the sub-skill's Crafts when advancing; use the matching sub-skill when checking.

E.g. a character can hold Crafts–Tech Ranged Weaponsmithing 2 and Crafts–Tech Armorsmithing 5: making Tech-category armor uses Armorsmithing 5; making Tech-category weapons uses Weaponsmithing 2.

If a check calls only for "Crafts" without naming a skill, use your highest Crafts sub-skill.

**Making Tech-line items:**

**Making Tech items: INT + Crafts–(matching sub-skill)**

For the actual checks see the "Crafting Rules".

Sub-skills:

Tech ranged weapons: Crafts–Tech Ranged Weaponsmithing (covering tech guns, cannon, bows, concealed weapons)

Tech melee weapons: Crafts–Tech Melee Weaponsmithing (tech versions of melee cold weapons)

Tech armor: Crafts–Tech Armorsmithing (tech items classed light or heavy armor)

Tech shields: Crafts–Tech Shieldsmithing (tech items classed shields)

Tech devices: Crafts–Tech Devices (tech items classed devices)

Tech items: Crafts–Tech Items (non-device tech items — helmets, cloaks, etc.)

Tech explosives: Crafts–Tech Explosives (tech items classed explosives; ammunition also falls here)

Tech pharmaceuticals: Crafts–Tech Pharmaceuticals (drugs, potions, and similar tech items)

Tech light vehicles: Crafts–Tech Light Vehicles

Tech heavy vehicles: Crafts–Tech Heavy Vehicles

Tech combat robots: Crafts–Tech Combat Robots

Tech-category food: Crafts–Cooking (cuisine; Tech- or Natural-category food)

Tech item modifications: Crafts–Tech Item Modifications

Note: an item with 2 classifications or 2 properties checks with the lower of the 2 skills.

Note: upgradeable items may be made, and later stages may also be made under the self-made item rules.

Note: items classed as systems cannot be made.

Note: biological vehicles cannot be made.

Note: repairs use the sub-skill matching the item's type.

Note: special-material modifications need no special sub-skill, but you can only produce Tech-category material modifications.

Note: self-made vehicles all lose the **[Unlimited Ammo]** property and track ammunition normally.

Certain special Natural-category Professions — Crafts–Gardening, Crafts–Cooking — have their checks described where relevant; otherwise the ST rules as appropriate.

Special Professions:

The following sub-skills cannot be gained naturally — they unlock for purchase only after buying certain special abilities.

**Making Fantasy-line items:**

**Making Fantasy items: INT + Crafts–(matching sub-skill)**

The rules match Tech-line crafting, except for the two sub-skills "Alchemy of the Dan" and "Immortal Artifice".

Sub-skills:

Fantasy ranged weapons: Crafts–Fantasy Ranged Weaponsmithing (fantasy guns and cannon)

Fantasy melee weapons: Crafts–Fantasy Melee Weaponsmithing

Fantasy armor: Crafts–Fantasy Armorsmithing (light or heavy)

Fantasy shields: Crafts–Fantasy Shieldsmithing

Fantasy foci: Crafts–Fantasy Focussmithing (fantasy items classed as foci)

Fantasy items: Crafts–Fantasy Items (non-focus fantasy items — shoes, belts, props)

Fantasy ornaments: Crafts–Fantasy Jewel-Cutting (rings, amulets, necklaces)

Fantasy potions: Crafts–Alchemy (potions, sacred oils, poisons)

Fantasy light vehicles: Crafts–Fantasy Light Vehicles

Fantasy heavy vehicles: Crafts–Fantasy Heavy Vehicles

Fantasy combat robots: Crafts–Fantasy Combat Robots

Fantasy enchantments: Crafts–Fantasy Enchanting

Note: an item with 2 classifications or 2 properties checks with the lower skill.

Note: the lowest stage of upgradeable items may be made, but later upgrades must be bought normally.

Note: items with the **[Divine Arms]** property cannot be made unless stated otherwise.

Note: concept armaments cannot be made.

Note: biological vehicles cannot be made.

Note: repairs use the matching sub-skill.

Note: special-material modifications need no special sub-skill; you may produce Fantasy-category material modifications.

**Dan-alchemy:**

**Dan: INT + Crafts–Dan Alchemy**

For making dan elixirs. See the "Dan crafting" rules.

**Immortal artifice:**

**Artifice: INT + Crafts–Immortal Artifice**

For making treasure artifacts. See the "Immortal artifice crafting" rules.

**Investigation: noticing the clues ordinary people miss — extracting more information from the same things.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Search:**

**Search: INT + Investigation (Search)**

Find objects, gather clues, hidden treasure, secret rooms, and anything else tucked away.

Action: each 2 m³ or 2 m² searched is a Whole-round Action.

Search's virtue: fail, and you can keep searching the area until you give up.

Searching generally takes hands and feet, so the searched area is normally limited to your reach.

Search is not a periodic check — you must reach the DC in one check to succeed.

| Target | DC | Variation | DC |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A specific item in a junk box | DC 1 | Size under 1 | DC +2 |
| A specific item in a dumpster | DC 2 | Size 1–3 | normal |
| A simple trap | DC 4+ | Size 4–6 | DC −1 |
| A hidden door | DC 5+ | Size 7–9 | DC −2 |
| A well-made trap | DC 7+ | and so on | and so on |
| A well-made hidden door | DC 8+ |  |  |

Note: every "+" entry is the ST's call; these are ordinary minimums.

**Listening:**

**Listening: PCT + Investigation (Listening)**

Hear faint sounds, notice footsteps, eavesdrop on conversations — and stranger noises.

Action: passive listening — sounds reaching you — takes none. Active listening, like pressing up to a door to eavesdrop, costs a Movement Action.

The source must be within your rough range, and the character must have hearing to attempt this.

Each point of tinnitus costs the result 1 success.

Difficulty depends on the source's loudness; beating the DC means hearing the sound.

If the sound is conversation, your successes must exceed double the DC to make out the words.

DCs are either fixed or contested. Fixed DCs:

| Sound | DC | Variation | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Battle | DC −2 | Per 15 m beyond the source | DC +1 |
| Normal conversation | DC 0 | Through an ordinary door | DC +1 |
| Movement | DC 1 | Through a vault door | DC +3 |
| A cat walking | DC 3 | Listener asleep | DC +5 |
| An owl diving | DC 6 |  |  |

When the source acts to hide its sound — whispering, slow sneaking —

it contests your PCT + Investigation (Listening), gaining the matching environmental adjustments.

**Scouting:**

**Scouting: PCT + Investigation (Scouting)**

Spot distant enemies, hidden targets, disguises, monsters in the shadows.

Action: passive scouting — noticing environmental changes others cause — takes none. Carefully surveying your surroundings costs a Movement Action.

You can only scout within your rough range, and must have a matching means of detection — a blind character cannot scout distant things by sight.

Generally, spotting a particular target or situation needs 1 success; the larger it is, the easier to see (see the "sensory range rules").

Adjustments:

| Situation | DC adjustment |
| --- | --- |
| Many similar targets around the one you seek | DC +2 |
| Per 15 m of distance | DC +1 |

If the other side actively resists, see the "Detection and Locating rules".

**Lip-reading**

**Lip-reading: PCT + Investigation (Lip-reading)**

Understand what a target is saying without hearing.

Action: concentrate for a full minute, then make a scouting check; during that minute you may do nothing else (beyond moving at up to half speed).

To understand speech by lip-reading, the speaker must be within your sensory range; you must have sight and see them speaking, and know the speaker's language (this use of the skill is language-dependent). Base DC 3; complex sentences or mumbling speakers raise it (ST's call). You must keep line of sight to their lips.

**Medicine: everything from first aid to surgery — all knowledge of the body.**

Special: Medicine checks can only treat normal organisms. Abnormal beings — undead, dark creatures, light creatures, swarms, spirits, incorporeals, constructs, elementals, plants, etc. — gain no benefit from any Medicine-related check unless stated otherwise.

Also, because medicine needs professional instruments and equipment, environment affects Medicine checks:

Next-generation hospital: +8 DP Environmental Bonus.

Hospital sterile room: +4 DP Environmental Bonus.

An ordinary open street: no bonus or penalty.

Wild jungle: −4 DP Environmental Penalty.

A mire of who-knows-what bacteria: −8 DP Environmental Penalty.

Common uses and matching Professions:

**First aid:**

**First aid: AGI + Medicine (First Aid)**

Stop a target's wounds from worsening; stave off death.

Action: usually a Standard Action.

First aid targets are generally those with no Healthy HP left, whose worst state is Aggravated damage, and whose wounds are still deteriorating.

The target must be within your reach. Make a touch-attack check against them (unnecessary if unconscious); on success, roll AGI + Medicine (First Aid) — with even 1 success, the target's wounds stop deteriorating unless they take damage again.

First aid can also treat bleeding, used the same way — except each success removes 1 point of the bleeding condition.

**Long-term care (a.k.a. Nursing):**

**Long-term care: INT + Medicine (Long-term Care)**

Accelerate a target's natural recovery.

Action: a periodic check with a period of hours; during each period neither patient nor carer may take any other action.

Long-term care boosts the target's own recuperation — it counts as natural recovery.

Once per patient per 24 hours.

Before checking, choose one of two goals for this treatment:

— At 5 accumulated successes, convert 1 of the target's HP(L) to HP(B).

— At 10 accumulated successes, convert 1 of the target's HP(A) to HP(L).

**Surgery:**

The Surgery Profession is quite special.

Normally it is not used —

it is a Profession invoked by particular situations or resources, whose usage and requirements are described there. It is listed here only as a standalone Profession, without usage details.

**Diagnosis:**

**Diagnosis: INT + Medicine (Diagnosis)**

Learn a target's physical condition.

Action: concentrate for a full minute, then make a diagnosis check; during the minute you may do nothing else.

You can only diagnose targets within your sensory range.

If you cannot touch the target but have sight, your successes are halved.

If you can touch the target but lack sight, your successes are halved.

If you can neither touch nor see, you cannot check at all.

Diagnosing wound state is generally DC 1:

- Unharmed: the creature is uninjured.
- Minor injuries: converting all wounds to Blunt (1 Lethal = 2 Blunt, 1 Aggravated = 3), the total does not exceed the target's HP.
- Moderate injuries: the converted total does not exceed twice their HP.
- Severe injuries: the converted total does not exceed three times their HP.
- Dead: the creature is dead.

Diagnosing toxins and diseases is DC (Tier + 1)². Success reveals the toxin or disease in full — its Save, Save DC, remedies (if any), everything.

**Treatment:**

**Treatment: INT + Medicine (Treatment)**

Help a target fight disease and toxins and regain health.

Action: a special check whose action depends on the ailment's onset cycle; you must spend at least half the cycle to attempt it. (If the cycle is 1 round, it takes a Standard Action.)

Make a treatment check; the target's next Save or Resisting Check against the disease or toxin gains a Reinforcement Bonus equal to your successes.

===

**Occult: knowledge of curses, magic, legends, the dark world — true or not.**

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Casting checks: INT + Occult (matching Profession)**

**See the combat chapter; for matching Professions see the spell overview chapter.**

Note: if a spell needs several Professions, all must be held, or the missing-Profession penalty applies.

A spell with no Profession incurs no missing-Profession penalty.

Some other casting abilities let you cast with other Stats.

**Spell identification:**

**Spellcraft: INT + Occult (Spell Identification)**

Identify spells being cast, or in effect, and their effects.

Action: two uses. Identifying a spell as it is being cast takes no action. Examining a spell in effect takes one minute.

To identify a spell being cast, the caster must be within your sensory range and normally observable.

Examining a spell in effect means the object must be within reach — scrolls, potions, magic items, a ward, and so on.

Identifying a spell being cast: on failure, no retry.

Examining a spell in effect: on failure, retry at the cost of the same time again.

If the spell involves casting Professions you lack, your result loses half its successes.

DC to identify a spell being cast: (the spell's original Tier)² + its current stage.

E.g. an originally C-Tier spell upgraded to B: 2² + 3 = 7.

DC to examine a spell in effect: (original Tier + 1)² + current stage.

E.g. an originally C-Tier spell, not uptiered: (2+1)² + 2 = 11.

**Daoist arts:**

The Daoist Arts Profession is quite special.

All Eastern daoist spells require the "Daoist Arts" Profession by default, and certain resources also require it.

Usage and requirements are described in the relevant resources or situations; it is listed here only as a standalone Profession.

For casting checks with daoist arts, see the casting checks above.

**Prerequisites for reading Fantasy-category blueprints:**

Reading blueprints requires a number of matching key-skill Professions, so here are some of them. Besides serving as key blueprint-reading Professions, each has particular uses of its own — not enumerated here, ST's call:

Enchanting: itself a casting Profession.

Creation: itself a casting Profession.

Astrology: besides being a prerequisite, tells time, date, and direction by the stars.

Alchemy: checks on plants' uses — magical effects, toxicity, and so on.

Daoist Arts: required for Eastern-line blueprints; see the "Daoist Arts" Profession.

Smithing: checks on metals' uses — quality, smelting method, magical effects, hardness.

Jewelcraft: checks on gems — quality, magical effects, hardness.

Spell Identification: see that Profession.

**Science: knowledge of physics, chemistry, biology, geology and the other hard sciences — known and applicable.**

A note up front:

Player and character are two entirely different entities, so their knowledge differs — a PC who is a computer expert in real life may be playing a boxer. Real-world knowledge the player carries into the game may not fit the character's definition.

Some STs encourage players to solve problems with their own experience; others want PC roleplay to hew closely to the character sheet. Most sit between. Settle it together.

Common uses and matching Professions:

**Knowledge:**

**Knowledge: INT + Science (matching science Profession)**

Science is the study of a hard discipline; because Academics exists separately, all Professions under Science are "STEM" learning.

Action: generally none.

Use: obtain information in the matching field, e.g.:

Classical physics (a.k.a. Physics): only the simplest mechanics, optics, and related fields.

Optics: spectroscopy in particular.

Mechanics: buoyancy, gravity, attraction, and so on.

Quantum physics: physics of the microscopic.

Chemistry: the composition, structure, transformation, and uses of matter.

Biology: the kinds, structures, behaviors, functions, and origins of living things.

Mathematics: the study of quantity, structure, and change.

Most disciplines have sub-fields of their own, not detailed here.

This is an active check, and the player must hold the field's Profession —

a character without the "Mathematics" Profession cannot roll INT + Science (Mathematics) to learn anything mathematical.

| Knowledge | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Simple questions (common knowledge) | DC 2 |
| Ordinary questions (fundamentals) | DC 3 |
| Hard questions (genuinely difficult) | DC 4 |
| Obscure questions (specialist matters) | DC 6 |

If a knowledge check fails, you cannot re-check the same matter.
