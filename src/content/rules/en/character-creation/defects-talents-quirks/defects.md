# Defects

**Defect basic rules**

You may choose Defects while building your character card, to make your character more vivid and alive.

Each Defect grants Defect points equal to its level. For intuitive reading, Defect levels are written as negative numbers — but when counting Defect points, read them as positive.

Every 2 Defect points may be exchanged for 1 Talent point, which you can spend on Talents; the rules work like Feats.

Note: no Defect can be removed by having the Lord God repair your body or limbs. Defects that can be removed on their own say so themselves.

Defects fall into three classes — physical, mental, and anomalous:

Physical: these Defects and Talents work through your physiology. If you have body-snatching or a similar body-swapping ability, or a means of leaving the old body such as a departing primordial spirit, then in the new body you are no longer affected by physical Defects and physical Talents. If you can return to the old body, its physical Defects and Talents affect you again.

Mental: these Defects and Talents work through your state of mind. With personality-switching or similar abilities, the old personality's mental Defects and Talents are not inherited by the new one — until you switch back.

Anomalous: how these Defects and Talents work simply cannot be explained. What is certain is that the way out of them is death — and sometimes not even that.

Unless stated otherwise, Defects and Talents grant Feat Bonuses and Feat Penalties.

**Physical Defects:**

Lame Leg (−1 to −5):
Something is wrong with your legs — an old injury, perhaps.
You gain permanent, unsaveable leg limb-impairment points equal to this Defect's level.
If this Defect reduces your speed to zero, you are crippled, permanently losing land speed.

One-Armed (−5):
You lost a limb somehow — an accident, perhaps.
You gain a permanent 5-point limb-impairment effect, and with only one arm you cannot use spells with somatic components, two-handed weapons, and the like.
Special: you may take this Defect twice, losing both arms.

Motor Dyspraxia (−1 to −4):
You are a walking humanoid disaster — chaos and scorched earth wherever you go, courtesy of your catastrophic clumsiness.
Level 1: Physical skill checks take a 1-point penalty.
Level 2: Physical-Stat-related checks take a 1-point penalty, stacking with level 1.
Level 3: the level-1 penalty grows by 1.
Level 4: the level-2 penalty grows by 1.

Old Wound (−1 to −3):
You once took a near-fatal wound, and it haunts you still.
Your maximum HP drops by this Defect's level; you cannot use this Defect to push maximum HP to 5 or below.

Immune Deficiency (−1 to −2):
Prerequisite: taking this Defect bars the "Endurance" Feat, the "Diehard" Feat, and similar Feats or Talents that grant resistance to toxins and disease — and vice versa.
Your immune system is fragile; a cold could kill you.
Fortitude checks against toxins and disease take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Impaired Vision (−1 to −5):
Your eyes have problems — nearsightedness, farsightedness, astigmatism, presbyopia, glaucoma, cataracts, take your pick.
You gain permanent, unsaveable dazzle points equal to this Defect's level.
Your visual sensory range becomes PCT × (10 − Defect level) meters, which also affects your rough visual range.
At level 5, you are blind.

Impaired Hearing (−1 to −4):
Your hearing isn't sharp — injured ears, or illness.
You gain permanent, unsaveable tinnitus points equal to this Defect's level.
Your auditory sensory range becomes PCT × (10 − Defect level) meters.
At level 4, you are deaf.

Insomnia (−1 to −3):
You can't sleep. Simple as that — and for a human being, is there any worse torment?
Long Rests take (Defect level × 1) extra hours; where normal rest takes 8 hours, you toss and turn longer before it counts.
Sleep too little and, naturally, you're fatigued the next day: until you complete one full Long Rest, you get no natural recovery.

Trembling Hands (−1 to −3):
Your hands shake and mistakes follow; by now it's habit.
All your limb-based attack checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Cannot Aim (−2):
You can't "zero" your ranged attacks. When aiming, you gain none of aiming's benefits.

Mute (−5):
For whatever reason, you cannot speak.
You cannot use abilities requiring speech — casting, the Librarian, and so on — nor talk or negotiate, imposing a 5 DP penalty on Social-skill-related checks.

Dwarfish Build (−2):
Prerequisite: you cannot hold the "Towering Build" Feat, and taking this Defect bars choosing it.
Your Size drops to 4, with HP and speed each −1 accordingly. For carrying capacity, STR counts as 1 lower.

Impaired Healing (−5):
Prerequisite: taking this Defect bars any Feat or Talent that changes natural recovery speed.
Illness or otherwise, your body barely heals itself — untreated, a cut finger could follow you for life.
Your natural recovery cycle doubles, and Rapid Healing and Regeneration each drop 1 point; if you hold several such abilities, all of them drop 1.

Flat as a Plain (−0):
Female characters only. Incompatible with "Magnificent Figure". Taking this Defect grants the Talent of the same name. Your bust is fixed at an A cup or flatter. Whenever anyone mentions your cup size — deliberately or not — all your checks this Scene take a 1 DP Morale Penalty. Moreover, while any female character with the Magnificent Figure Talent is within your perception, all your checks take a further 1 DP Morale Penalty. The Defect only applies while your visible appearance is humanoid. If any ability removes this Defect's effects (breast augmentation, shapeshifting, fully sealed armor, and so on), you simultaneously lose all benefits of the same-named Talent.

Magnificent Figure (−0):
Female characters only. Incompatible with "Flat as a Plain". Taking this Defect forcibly grants the Talent of the same name. Your bust is fixed at a D cup or larger. Your Evade Defense permanently drops 1, and your Reflex checks take a 1 DP Size Penalty. When lifted, carried, or similar, you count as 1 Size larger. When buying torso armor, your ST may require an extra 10 points or 1/100 of the item's price (whichever is lower) for a custom fit; items that reshape to the wearer need no surcharge. Your ST may invoke this Defect to wedge you tight in cave crawls and the like. The Defect only applies while your visible appearance is humanoid. If any ability removes its effects (reduction surgery, shapeshifting, fully sealed armor…), you simultaneously lose all benefits of the same-named Talent.

**Mental Defects:**

Gluttony (−3):
This Defect makes you a bottomless glutton: given the chance you eat far past your body's needs, and you never willingly miss a meal.
While hungry, all checks take a 1 DP penalty. Facing a lavish feast, make a Will check at DC 20 to resist; fail, and you gorge heedless of consequences, and for the hour after — digesting the excess — all checks take a 1 DP penalty.

On Another Wavelength (−1 to −2):
You are a walking transmitter of noise; nobody can follow your train of thought.
All your Social-related checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Scatterbrained (−2):
You're careless, prone to losing what matters.
Whenever you leave a place you stayed at for over an hour, make an Opportunity Roll. Fail, and you randomly lose one ordinary item (a whole wallet counts as one item); on a critical failure, you randomly lose an item with a Tier. Weapons, armor, and clothing are never lost, nor vehicles — though a vehicle collapsed into capsule form can be.

Lone Wolf (−3 to −5):
You come from pirates, bandits, or some similar milieu where brothers knife each other on schedule; you've learned to rely on no one but yourself. Allies distract you — you need space that is yours alone to do your best work.
When acting together with allies, all your checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Slow to React (−1 to −3):
You're always a beat late to notice danger.
Your Initiative takes a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Sentimental (−1 to −2):
You're too soft-hearted, and your mind wanders at the worst times.
When fighting a target whose original attitude toward you was Friendly or better, all your combat checks take a penalty of (Defect level × 2).

Haughty (−1 to −2):
Only people like you deserve trust; you treat those of other species, peoples, or nationalities with genuine nastiness.
Social-related checks with such people take a (Defect level × 2) DP penalty.

Rigidly Honest (−1 to −2):
You are stiffly law-abiding and press others to be too. Where no law binds, you still bind yourself as though at home — and you assume others are honest unless proven otherwise. It's a Defect because it cages your behavior. You cannot use Subterfuge checks in negotiations; if you force one anyway, the Subterfuge check takes a (Defect level × 2) DP penalty, and your conscience then racks you — all actions take a (Defect level × 2) DP penalty for one Scene.

Curiosity (−1 to −2):
You are curious by nature — not the ordinary kind everyone has ("what's in that hole?" "where did the saucer come from?") but the probing kind ("what happens if I press this?"). Faced with an interesting scene or object, dangerous or not, you go and investigate. If you don't, all your checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level for the next Scene.

Little Terror (−2):
Prerequisite: the "Curiosity" Defect
You're an unstoppable brat with hyperactive curiosity: anything interesting must be investigated regardless of consequences, or you take a 2 DP penalty on all checks for the rest of the film (non-stacking). You may, of course, send a teammate in while you watch — that also counts as satisfying your curiosity.

Sheltered Upbringing (−2):
You grew up in comfort, ignorant of violence. It will take some incidents to acclimate you.
When you take damage exceeding half your HP in one blow, you become terror-stricken toward the source, and this injury cannot trigger a Gene Lock check.
Removal: this Defect expires at the end of your first film.

Nervous Tension (−1 to −2):
You live keyed-up, scanning for dangers that might appear, even when you know full well you're safe.
Level 1: your PCT-related checks take a 1 DP penalty.
Level 2: you watch your surroundings always — even asleep. You never get proper rest: energy recovered per rest is halved, minimum 1.

Phobia (−1 to −3):
You carry an unconquerable phobia — of an object or environment (spiders, elevators) or of a situation (arguments, gunfights).
Whenever you meet the feared object or situation, you take unavoidable fear points toward it equal to this Defect's level.
If your feared object is vanishingly rare, the Defect's level may drop — fear of winged human-faced spiders is worth about level 0, such things barely existing. Conversely a ubiquitous object of fear (air, say) may raise the Defect's worth.

Imaginary Friend (−2):
You have an imaginary friend. You talk to him regularly and pay him fifty a week.
You are so pitiable that everyone holds you in contempt: their Social-related checks against you gain a 2 DP bonus.

Half a Beat Slow (−2 to −4):
You reliably turn the wrong way in a sudden swerve.
Your Reflex checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Moe Weakness (−2):
Name a moe attribute — glasses girls, sailor uniforms, blue-striped underthings, shrine maidens, tsundere, lolis… Whenever you see something bearing it, you are instantly captivated, filled with entirely understandable but possibly illegal urges to hug it, squish it, keep it.
You gain a 1 DP focus bonus on any action that furthers this (the action must be fairly direct — no elaborate long cons), and on resisting any attempt to stop you; all your other actions take a 2 DP distraction penalty. If it can talk, you'll also want it to join the party.
The moe object gains a 2 DP bonus on social checks against you.
If it refuses you, or others stop you, everything you do for 24 hours takes a 2 DP Morale Penalty.
If the moe object appears as an enemy: you may not attack it while other targets remain; if it hasn't attacked you in a round, you may not attack it first; all your attacks on it take a 2 DP penalty, and you may not attempt to down or kill it. Should it die by your damage or otherwise directly, your CMT permanently drops 1 — a Stats Drain.
All of the above can be avoided with a Will check at first sight, DC equal to the moe object's DMR.

Superstition (−2):
Everyone you grew up among held all supernatural power dangerous, whatever its source. You can't even trust the spells that help you.
Whenever another character's ability with a duration and no harm affects you (skills, spells, and more) — beneficial or not — each such ability imposes a 1 DP penalty on all your checks until it ends. Buffs you cast on yourself are exempt.

Cowardski (−1 to −2):
You are a coward through and through; wetting yourself isn't slang to you, it's routine.
In combat you can only fight normally with overwhelming advantage, cover, concealment, a maintained guard, or full defense; otherwise all your checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.

Faint-Hearted (−1 to −3):
You are hopeless at fighting — you couldn't bring yourself to kill a chicken.
All your attack checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level.
Special: opening Gene Lock rank 1 reduces this Defect by 1 level; rank 2 by 1 more; rank 3 by 1 more.

Paranoia (−4):
You always suspect you're being used, so trusting anyone truly is hard.
Whenever anyone tries to help you — combat assistance, teaming up, healing, gifts, trading points, anything — you must pass a Will check at DC equal to your DTR or refuse the help. And if you were helped without knowing (healed while unconscious, say), then upon discovering it, all your actions take a 4 DP penalty for the rest of the horror film, non-stacking.

Compulsion (−1 to −5):
You carry an irresistible compulsive behavior — perhaps washing your hands every two hours, perhaps reciting a eulogy after every shot fired.
The level depends on its real impact: the more easily triggered and the longer it takes, the higher the level. Multiple compulsions are taken separately.
For example: a Swift Action of prayer after each kill might be level 1; a Swift Action of prayer after every shot might be level 3. Hand-washing every eight hours is worth about level 1; five minutes of scrubbing every half hour might be level 3. A minute of self-introduction to every living thing you meet, or three minutes of washing every five — congratulations on the level-5 achievement.
When the compulsive act is possible, you must perform it, even at risk of your life. When it truly cannot be performed (you need to wash but there's no water for a hundred li), you sink into deep malaise — all checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level until the act is done.
Though called a compulsion, this Defect can also model bad habits, addictions, or bizarre convictions — the effects are alike.

Emotional Flatness (−1 to −2):
You are silent and expressionless — you neither know what feeling is nor how to show it. The difference between you and an emotionless-deadpan girl is that she might be adorable; you, not necessarily.
Against mental effects, Reading People, mind-probing and the like, you gain a bonus equal to this Defect's level. But all Social-related checks you initiate take (Defect level × 3) as a penalty.

Sadism (−2):
Since childhood you have delighted in crushing opponents body and soul, and worn it proudly; you find you especially enjoy grinding an enemy's face into the mud.
In battle, if you spot a badly wounded or helpless enemy within your sensory range, all aggressive actions against them gain a 1 DP bonus, and killing them restores 1 Willpower — at most once per Scene. But meanwhile all your active checks against anyone else take a 4 DP penalty and you cannot spend Willpower, lasting one Scene.

Masochism (−2 to −4):
You are the legendary M.
While your HP is undamaged, all your checks take a 1 DP penalty.
At level 2, the penalty applies while you have no Lethal damage.
At level 3, while you have no Aggravated damage.

Avarice (−1):
You drool at wealth — wages after a job, an adventure's spoils, criminal loot or money laid as bait, Tiers and points included.
All positive actions toward gaining that wealth take a 1 DP bonus, and success restores 1 Willpower. But fail or abandon the goal, and your active checks take a 4 DP penalty and you lose 1 Willpower.

Airhead (−1):
Naturalness needs no reason! You're just that natural!
Your INT and PCT checks take a 2 DP penalty, but your Opportunity Roll results gain +1.

Naïveté (−1):
You believe nobody is born wicked. Some may mock your misplaced optimism; you don't mind.
Checks resisting targets using "Subterfuge", "Theft", or "Stealth" take a 2 DP penalty. Most people start with a good first impression of you.

Illiteracy (−1 to −3):
You cannot read — not even your mother tongue. Level 1 halves the language points you can gain; level 2 forces a level-1-only native tongue; level 3 means no reading or writing whatsoever, not even the simplest characters.
This Defect cuts you off from many enhancements, especially textual ones like Tomes.
Removal: spend (Defect level × 3) XP to erase this Defect.

Dependency (−1 to −3):
You have a fierce emotional dependency — a terror of losing someone or something.
Choose the object of dependency (something carryable or a creature at your side, settled between ST and PC). Whenever it is threatened, endangered, or claimed by another, all your checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level. If the person or object is lost, dies, or is destroyed, this Defect becomes the same-level "Self-Doubt" Defect.

Self-Doubt (−2 to −4):
You lack confidence, forever feeling inadequate.
Whenever any check of yours fails (missed attacks included), all subsequent checks take a penalty equal to this Defect's level for one Scene.

Guardedness (−2):
Some childhood failure to hold your tongue cost you dearly — perhaps you accidentally exposed your family to enemy interrogators — and you have weighed every word since.
Your social-related checks take a 2 DP penalty, and your careful phrasing slows your speech: in combat, talking rises to a Swift Action.

Combat Shakes (−1 to −3):
Battle sets you trembling and sick.
Each round of combat you fight, make a Will check at DC (5 + Defect level); failure gives you 1 stacking nausea point.

**Anomalous Defects:**

Luck E (−3):
You are unlucky — matters reliably worsen at the worst moment.
On your Opportunity Rolls, successes become failures, and failures become critical failures.
