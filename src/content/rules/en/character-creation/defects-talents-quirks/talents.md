# Talents

**Talents**

Unlike ordinary mortals, you may possess some quite exceptional gifts.

Each Defect grants Defect points equal to its level. Every 2 Defect points may be exchanged for 1 Talent point, which you can spend on Talents; the rules work like Feats.

Defects and Talents fall roughly into three classes — physical, mental, and anomalous:

Physical: these Defects and Talents work through your physiology. If you have body-snatching or a similar body-swapping ability, or a means of leaving the old body such as a departing primordial spirit, then in the new body you are no longer affected by physical Defects and physical Talents. If you can return to the old body, its physical Defects and Talents affect you again.

Mental: these Defects and Talents work through your state of mind. With personality-switching or similar abilities, the old personality's mental Defects and Talents are not inherited by the new one — until you switch back.

Anomalous: how these Defects and Talents work simply cannot be explained. What is certain is that the way out of them is death — and sometimes not even that.

Unless stated otherwise, Defects and Talents grant Feat Bonuses and Feat Penalties.

**Physical Talents:**

Absolute Time Sense (1–2):
There seems to be a precision watch inside your head. Two levels.
Level 1: you always know the time, to the second, and can measure elapsed time to the same precision. Time-zone changes and dozing off don't affect this, and you can wake at a moment chosen in advance. Being knocked unconscious, hypnosis, and the like can block the ability. Entering a world in a different era, the flow of time confuses you until you learn the "new" time.
Level 2: shifts of time and space no longer affect you — you always know the current time in the absolute sense. Note that daylight saving or calendar changes can still trip you up. When you travel through time, the ST will tell you "you have moved 92,876.3 days into the past" and leave you — or someone else — to work out questions like "then how the hell do leap years count?"

Weightlifting (1–3):
You can lift things heavier than yourself.
For carrying and encumbrance calculations, your STR counts as higher by this Talent's level.

Swift Recovery (2–3):
Prerequisite: you cannot have the "Impaired Healing" Defect.
Two levels.
Level 2: your wounds' natural recovery is twice as fast.
Level 3: your abnormal-status points' natural recovery is twice as fast.

Keen Senses line (1–3):
Prerequisite: you cannot hold the matching sense's Defect — no pairing "Impaired Vision" with "Keen Sight".
You have superhuman senses.
Each keen sense is its own Talent, across the five senses of sight, hearing, touch, smell, and taste.
On checks that use the sense, gain a Feat Bonus of this Talent's level; and where only that sense is involved, your sensory range counts as (Talent level × 5) meters longer.
Examples:
Keen Hearing: bonuses when listening or attending to sound (dodging gunfire in darkness, say) — e.g. Investigation (Listening).
Keen Touch: when probing things by touch (frisking a suspect for hidden weapons) — e.g. Investigation (Search).
Keen Sight: when scouting by eye or making visual searches (hunting traps or tracks) — e.g. Investigation (Scouting).
Special: if you lose a sense, you may petition the ST to gain keen senses elsewhere in compensation — blind (Impaired Vision 5), you may take Keen Hearing at no cost.

Supple Body (1–2):
Your body is uncommonly flexible. Two levels.
Level 1: you gain a Feat Bonus equal to this Talent's level on climb checks and grapple contests.
Level 2: shrinking your Size by 1 no longer costs you defense — though shrinking by 2 still costs Base Defense and Evade Defense.

Lightning Calculator (1):
You have the gift of instant mental arithmetic.
You may use the "calculator" at any time, on anything you care to compute — even with your life on the line.
For simple math problems, the ST may simply rule that you know the answer.

Short Sleeper (1–4):
You need less Long Rest than normal people. A normal person needs 8 hours a night; you need (Talent level) hours fewer.
If sleep is the condition for something — recovering an Energy Pool, say — you still need the full 8 hours.

Perfect Balance (1–3):
Ordinarily, however narrow or slick the surface, you do not fall.
Level 1: gain a bonus equal to this Talent's level on balance checks and contests against trips.
Level 2: you are immune to the half-speed effect of difficult terrain from natural environments.
Level 3: you can charge normally across natural difficult terrain.

Danger Sense (5):
You carry a keen survival instinct that warns when danger draws near — perhaps practiced reading of small clues in the surroundings, perhaps an uncanny sixth sense that clears trouble before it lands. When danger approaches, something strange runs through you — hair standing, goosebumps, a chill up the spine — granting a 3 DP bonus on contests against targets' Subterfuge and Stealth checks. And you are never flat-footed from surprise or low Initiative — a No-Tier immunity.

Night Vision (2):
Prerequisite: you cannot have Impaired Vision at level 5.
You gain low-light vision and ignore the effects of dim environments.

Farsight (2–4):
Prerequisite: you cannot have Impaired Vision at level 5.
You can focus your eyes as though through a telescope.
Your visual sensory range rises by (Talent level × PCT) meters, with your rough visual range rising in step. This does not stack with bonuses from technological devices like telescopes — holding both, use one.

Natural Immunity (1–2):
Prerequisite: you cannot have the "Immune Deficiency" Defect.
Your serious illnesses can be counted on one hand.
Level 1: gain a bonus equal to this Talent's level against disease and toxins.
Level 2: against diseases and toxins you have met before, the bonus doubles.

Combat Reflexes (4):
Your reactions are extraordinary; almost nothing stuns you for more than an instant.
While flat-footed you may still use Reflex Actions, provided you have abilities usable as Reflex Actions — though the flat-footed state itself remains.

Flat as a Plain (0):
Available only with the same-named Defect. You know how to work with what you have: your Evade Defense rises 1, and your Reflex Saves gain a 1 DP Speed Bonus. Applies only while your visible appearance is humanoid. If any ability removes the same-named Defect's effects (augmentation, shapeshifting, fully sealed armor…), you lose all of this Talent's benefits too.

Magnificent Figure (0):
Available only with the same-named Defect. You know how to work with what you have: your HP rises 1. Socializing with characters attracted to women, you gain a 1-point Size Bonus. Outside combat you can tuck a pen, a phone, or a similar small object at your chest, gaining a 1 DP Environmental Bonus on Larceny (Concealment) checks for it. In grapple and pin checks you count as 1 Size larger. Applies only while your visible appearance is humanoid. If any ability removes the same-named Defect's effects, you lose all of this Talent's benefits too.

Ambidexterity (5):
Among humans, right-handers run about 90%, left-handers about 10% — and the ambidextrous, under 1%. Your two hands are equally deft, which pays off richly: buying and enhancing Dual-Pistol Shooting / Two-Weapon Fighting / Sword-and-Board Fighting costs 1 XP less per level, and the Feat Bonuses those Feats grant rise by 1 when you use them.

**Mental Talents:**

Common Sense (5):
You have an outstanding grasp of the basics and a practical mind: given a moment's thought, you can usually reach a direct, reliable decision from what you know.
Once per Scene, when you are about to take an action with catastrophic consequences, or find yourself utterly stumped at a plot's crux, make an **INT** or **PCT** check; on a success, describe to the ST what you are about to do, and the ST will point out where it leads — a chance to change course and dodge the risk. Note: per film, this ability may be used no more times than your Legendary INT or Legendary PCT (whichever is higher, minimum 1).
The ST may disable this Talent for any reason.

Foodie (2):
Prerequisite: the "Gluttony" Defect.
You are a gourmand through and through — the day you die, it'll be from overeating.
The penalty after eating your fill now becomes a bonus, and its duration extends until the next time you go hungry.

Sensitivity (3):
Your Empathy checks gain a 5 DP bonus, and you can feel others' moods. On first meeting someone, or reuniting after long absence, you may contest **PCT + Empathy (Reading People)** against their **CTR + Subterfuge**, needing no action. On a success, you perceive how they feel about you; on a failure, the ST answers with a lie. Splendid for spotting impostors, demonic possession, and the like — and for reading an NPC's loyalty and sincerity.

Intuition (3):
You usually guess right. Facing a choice, you may ask the ST to let you use intuition: pay 1 Willpower and make a PCT check at DC 5. On a success, tell the ST the goal you seek, and from the options you offer the ST names the "fastest", the "safest", or the "highest-scoring" route (your choice). Each use raises the next intuition check's DC by 5 for the rest of the film.
Note: you may only choose concrete outcomes — obtaining an item, killing a target — not conceptual ones like "maximize my score". And the options come from you to the ST, not the other way round. If your options are all dreadful, the ST picks the best of the dreadful, which may remain dreadful. If none can reach the goal, the ST may tell you your intuition says every idea you have is hopeless.
The ST may disable this Talent for any reason.

Inspiration (1):
Prerequisite: the "Intuition" Talent.
Your gift is so polished that intuition checks automatically gain 5 base success dice — your first use of intuition needs no roll at all, the second needs 5 successes, and so on.

Chūnibyō (2):
What some call an excess of self-consciousness is in truth the mark of one who unveils the world's underlying truth — a genuine prophet. Such people wield imagination far beyond the common run, and can dimly bend the forces of worldly existence. Your Social-related checks take a 2 DP penalty against ordinary people and gain a 2 DP bonus with those versed in ACG; and you gain a 2 DP bonus when resisting mind-affecting effects.

**Anomalous Talents:**

Snatched from the Jaws (3):
When death looms, Lady Luck seems always to smile on you! In a life-or-death moment (scale set by the ST — typically an instant-death effect, a check whose failure kills you, or an attack that would finish you), if the check is yours or includes you (active checks like leaping a chasm, or Saves), you may reroll every 1 in it. If the check is the enemy's (a casting attack check, an attack check), they gain no + Dice on it. Once per horror film.

The Plot Demands It (3):
You have a knack for being in the right place at the right time.
Once per Scene, you encounter one lucky but plausible coincidence, details the ST's. The ST might declare that one of the guards the PCs must negotiate with happens to be your cousin; or that as the PCs chase fleeing bank robbers, a supercar happens to be parked outside. The ST may also rule that one activation covers several — even all — of a session's "miracles" (the small-town garage happening to stock every part your super-tech anti-gravity belt needs, say).
You may suggest to the ST when and how to trigger this advantage, but the decision is theirs. If they turn down all your proposals and never trigger it themselves, you may bank the use for the next Scene, even the next film.

Fortune's Favorite (3–5):
You seem born under some being's care, luckier than luck itself.
Before making a check, you may invoke this Talent to roll it twice and keep the better result — usable (Talent level) times per horror film.

Lucky Star (1):
Prerequisite: the "Fortune's Favorite" Talent.
You may now bestow Fortune's Favorite's effect on others.
