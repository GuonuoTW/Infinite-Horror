# Quirks

**Quirks**
Quirks are lightweight character features — not Talents, and not necessarily Defects — simply the ways in which you are distinctive and different. For instance, you may be "avaricious"; but if you insist on being paid only in gold coins, that's a Quirk.
Each Quirk grants 2 XP; you may take up to 5 Quirks for 10 extra XP. You may later pay 4 XP to shed a Quirk, though it isn't recommended — Quirks are what make you "come alive".
Quirks divide into physical and mental. The split mainly signals to players what strengths and weaknesses a Quirk represents.

Unless stated otherwise, bonuses and penalties from Quirks are Feat Bonuses and Penalties.

**Physical Quirks:**
Physical Quirks are mild physical Defects. They need no roleplay, but impose slight penalties in particular in-game situations. Unlike mental Quirks, physical ones usually cannot be changed — except by the Lord God's repair, which costs 1,000 points per physical Quirk.

Motion Sickness:
The character is easily sickened by acceleration; choose a type — carsickness, seasickness, and so on.
While sick, all the character's checks take a 1 DP penalty, or the character gains 2 unsaveable nausea points; the effect fades 15 minutes after leaving the offending scene or object.

Addiction-Prone:
Anything habit-forming — even alcohol — "goes straight to your head".
You develop addictions far faster than normal: all addiction-related checks take a 4 DP penalty.

Bowlegs:
You are bowlegged.
Movement is usually unaffected, but your jump checks take a 2 DP penalty.

Sinks Like a Stone:
Your density runs a little high — you sink in water and cannot swim at all.
Your swim checks take a 2 DP penalty.

Distinctive Feature:
You bear a striking physical trait — "vivid blue hair", "mild albinism" — that makes you extremely easy to pick out of a crowd.
Your hiding- and sneaking-related checks take a 1 DP penalty, and attempts to scout or track you gain a 1 DP bonus.

Minor Disability:
You can downgrade almost any physical Defect into a Quirk — "Immune Deficiency" recast as "sickly", say. The problems appear rarely, but are genuinely inconvenient when they do. If you carry such an impairment, the ST may, where logical, impose a 2 DP penalty on the relevant checks.

**Mental Quirks:**
Mental Quirks are character features of very light weight. They are a bargain between PC and ST: "the first party agrees to roleplay these traits; in exchange, the second party grants XP." So you must actually play them out.
If a PC with the "dislikes heights" Quirk cheerfully climbs trees and cliffs at every chance, the ST should punish the poor roleplay — you may lose more XP than you gained. So don't pick Quirks you don't want to play.
None of this stops the ST from being flexible about Quirks. Players may swap Quirks to reflect how a character's personality shifts over a campaign, and the ST should let PCs reserve 5 points of "blank Quirks" at creation to fill in after play begins.

A character may later pay 4 XP to shed a Quirk, though again — they make you "come alive".
To qualify as a Quirk, a feature must satisfy at least one of the following:
1) It is a recurring, specific action, behavior, or personal inclination. It needn't take long or be very inconvenient, but it must be actively performable in play. Wholly passive features cannot be Quirks.
2) It sometimes imposes small penalties, or makes the character unwilling to do certain things. You must explain its game effect to the ST. Any mental Defect may be downgraded into a Quirk, using the Defect's text as reference at a much-reduced severity.

Example: "wears black" is not a valid Quirk — wholly passive, no downside. "Dresses like a textbook necromancer" is valid, provided the ST agrees it carries the downside "particularly dim passers-by regard this character at −2 disposition".

Some examples of mental Quirks:

Hyperfocus:
You are "too" good at not being distracted; "multitasking" might as well be mythology.
On extended checks, the check gains a 1 DP bonus — but if interrupted, your full checks take a 4 DP penalty for the next Scene.

Exotic Tastes / Enemy of the Looks Club:
You get along fine with other races and species: your Social checks with ordinary people take a 2 DP penalty, but with other races, species, and the like gain a 1 DP bonus.

Mild Parochialism:
You always notice differences of sex, color, and so on, though you never treat anyone badly for them. Choose at least 3 points of difference (race excluded — this is mild parochialism, and wholly alien targets escape the Quirk entirely); when interacting with anyone bearing any chosen difference, your Social-related checks take a 2 DP penalty.

Loves Company:
You like having people around and work happily with others; you always choose group action.
Acting alone, your full checks take a 1 DP penalty.

Flights of Fancy / Chūnibyō (mild):
You may take one very weak delusion as a Quirk — an idea that doesn't affect daily behavior and is hardly noticeable, but which you must genuinely believe. Should its truth be uncovered, you lose the Quirk and your full checks take a permanent 1 DP penalty. Sample delusions: "the world is flat", "the Pentagon secretly runs the Boy Scouts and the health-food shops", "socks cause athlete's foot", "a certain fertilizer conglomerate rigs the American election".

Easily Distracted:
You lose the thread readily, and long-term plans fare poorly with you.
On extended checks, the relevant checks take a 1 DP penalty.

By the Book:
You favor "methods proven right by repeated practice". While following them, all your checks gain a 1 DP bonus; when faced with "change" (commonly a changed film plot), your full checks in the changed circumstances take a 2 DP penalty.
Generally — the Infinite worlds being so numerous that little counts as "proven by repeated practice" — staying on a film's original plotline can be ruled to satisfy the condition.

Habits and Catchphrases:
Forever saying "My God!" or "Heaven bless my collar-studs!"… or always hunting up something silver to carry before a flight… or never sitting with your back to a door.
If you skip it in a Scene, the ST may impose a 2 DP penalty on your full checks until the habit or catchphrase is honored.

Helpfulness:
You habitually put the group — or others — first. When divisible benefits are handed out, you take yours last (unless split evenly); if you receive more than others, or before them, your full checks take a 2 DP penalty for the rest of the film.

Overflowing Imagination:
You are full of ideas, and not mildly inclined to share them. Some may be genuine insight — most probably aren't. Each horror film you must float at least 5 ideas (five ideas about one problem count); they may be options for solving a problem, next moves, anything — but they must be ideas. Fall short, and your full checks in the next horror film take a 2 DP penalty.

Collector's Compulsion:
Each horror film you must acquire one significant prop or item — something that helped you during the film, or a personal effect of your adversary. Fail, and all your checks in the next horror film take a 2 DP penalty.

Nosiness:
You are forever prying into other people's business: Investigation-related checks gain a 1 DP bonus; Social checks take a 2 DP penalty.

Grand Ambition:
You may take one not-too-outlandish conviction as a Quirk to reflect a modest goal — saving up to buy your own farm (or ship, spaceship, castle…), say. The goal may not run through illegal means (seizing a village, for instance), nor consist of hoarding points to buy some resource.

Pride:
Personal success, wealth, or standing weighs deeply on the character. With this Quirk, your social checks take a 2 DP penalty when facing orders, insults, or social contempt.

Indiscriminate Compassion:
If, in a Scene, the character discovers someone helpless — a witch at the medieval stake, refugees fleeing famine, a slave of the Roman Empire — the character does everything possible to help. Refuse, and conscience exacts its toll: the character's full checks take a 1 DP penalty for the rest of the film.

Oath:
A trifling vow — never drinking, courtesy to all ladies, or tithing 10% of income to the character's church — makes a Quirk. Break it, and the character's full checks take a 1 DP penalty for the rest of the film.
