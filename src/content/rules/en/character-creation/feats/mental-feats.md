# Mental Feats

===

**Demolitions Expert (1–3):**

Prerequisites: INT 3, AGI 3, Crafts–Explosives skill 2

Level 1: Blaster

Your character is exceptionally good with explosives of every kind, fluent in the whole technology of making them — from building his own devices to identifying and arming ready-made bombs. You are also EOD-trained, able to defuse explosives you've never seen before.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Crafts–Tech Explosives related checks.

Level 2: Demolition Savant

Only you understand where the soul of a bomb lives.

If an explosive's **[Explosive]** property deals damage, that damage rises by 1L when the character uses it; if the property deals damage several times, only the first instance gains the 1L.

Level 3: King of Demolition

You know bombs the way you know your own hands — your art of detonation is finished before anyone else has seen it clearly.

The actions the character needs to "set" and "detonate" explosives downgrade one step (see the Explosive property under items).

**Discernment (1–4)**

Prerequisites: INT 3, Academics skill 3 with the "Monsters" Profession

In every part of this Feat, the Academics (Monsters) check works only once per target — you cannot extract more information by checking repeatedly.

Level 1: Let's see what sort of devil you really are

The character gains a Feat Bonus of (this Feat's level + 1) DP on Academics-related checks.

Level 2: Know the enemy, know yourself

The character gains 1 AS on Academics (Monsters) checks.

Level 3: A hundred battles, no peril

When the character's Academics (Monsters) check meets the minimum bar (the square of the monster's Tier) and the monster has a "weakness", the character learns that weakness.

Level 4: Best keep your vitals hidden from me

For each piece of information the character extracts from a target via Academics (Monsters), they gain 1 Insight Bonus on checks against that target only — until the target changes kind (including but not limited to species, Alternation, etc.).

**New Oriental Chef (1–4)**

Prerequisites: Crafts–Cooking 2

Level 1: gain a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Crafts–Cooking.

Level 2: food you cook, once eaten over at least ten rounds, grants a Morale Bonus equal to half this Feat's level on full checks, lasting one Scene or fifteen minutes (whichever is longer).

Level 3: gain 1 AS on Crafts–Cooking.

Level 4: you may now use Willpower's basic use on your cooking checks, even for extended checks. If you do, the finished dish beams golden light into the sky, its aroma spreading until mouths water helplessly. This counts as a D-Tier Natural-category charm effect sourced from food, provoking appetite only, in a circle of radius (your INT) meters, blockable by **[Sealed]** buildings. The charm is neither demonic nor supernatural: anyone may shake it off at will, no check needed.

**Iron Will (1–5):**

Prerequisites: DTR 3, CMT 3

Level 1:

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Will checks.

Level 2:

When using Willpower's basic use to add DP to an action (or to defense), gain 1 extra DP Perfect Bonus (or 1 point of Perfect defense); 1 more when this Feat reaches level 4.

Level 3:

The character gains 1 extra AS on Will checks.

Level 4:

When contesting, saving against, or removing Negative Status points or inherent Negative Statuses from mind-affecting effects, one of the character's key resisting Stats (your choice) counts as higher by (this Feat's level × 1).

Level 5:

Once per Scene, when contesting or saving against Negative Status points or inherent Negative Statuses from mind-affecting effects, you may roll twice — but you must accept the second roll, even if worse. The character's Willpower pool also grows by 2.

**Healing Hands (1–3):**

Prerequisites: INT 2, Medicine skill 2

Level 1:

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Medicine-related checks.

Level 2:

With a full medical kit at hand (scalpel, disinfectant alcohol, sutures, etc. — ST's call), or in a hospital, operating theater, or similar environment (ST's call), the character's "long-term care" actions take half the total time.

Level 3:

The character may designate a target within reach and perform a special "long-term care" check at DC equal to the target's HP. On success, Lethal damage in the target's health track converts to Blunt, up to the character's Medicine skill level; and Aggravated damage converts to Lethal, up to the character's Medicine AS (minimum 1). This counts as natural recovery.

Whether or not it succeeds, the target counts as having taken one 8-hour Long Rest. The character may use this on one target once per 24 hours; if they already gave the target regular "long-term care", this ability cannot be used on that target within the 24 hours.

**Meditation (1–3):**

Prerequisites: PCT 3

Level 1: once per day, the character may recover 1 Willpower through 2 hours of meditation. With a generic Energy Pool, they may forgo the Willpower and recover 3 points of one generic energy instead.

Level 2: once per day, the character may recover 2 Willpower through 4 hours of meditation. With generic Energy Pools, they may simultaneously recover 3 points of all generic energies, or 6 points of one. This does not stack with the level-1 effect, but the character may meditate once per day in each mode.

Level 3: when the character uses both modes back to back, it counts as a Long Rest with its benefits. And in an environment fit for meditation (a sealed chamber, a cold-jade bed, etc. — ST's call), total meditation time halves.

Special: meditation time counts toward Long Rests — meditate 2 hours at level 1, sleep 6, and it counts as an 8-hour Long Rest. Energy recovered through meditation doesn't count against Energy Pools' daily recovery caps.

**Language (1–5):**

Prerequisites: INT 1

Level 1: basic literacy — at least able to tell the language apart from others.

Level 2: fairly fluent spelling and reading — most newspaper articles make sense.

Level 3: excellent command — idioms and slang can't trip you.

Level 4: dialects and the evolution of the language family are second nature. Buying languages of the same family or branch costs half — an English-4 speaker learns German or French at half cost, both being of the same family (the ST has final say on family classifications).

Level 5: you may create a new language, teachable only by you — and the Lord God's translation mechanism fails against it. When creating it, besides this level-5 language you may weave in up to (your Legendary INT × 1) other languages, each known at level 3+ (you don't lose them). The DC to crack the language equals the sum of all woven-in language Feat levels + your Legendary INT points. Created languages cannot serve as base or woven-in languages. Learning more languages later, you may pay 1 XP each to weave them into a created language.

Special: this Feat may be held multiply — Chinese 3, German 2, English 2. Also, every level of this Feat costs only 3 XP.

Special: at game start you receive total language levels equal to twice your INT as native tongues — INT 2 grants 4 combined levels. A character's native tongue should generally be at least level 2.

**Devotee (1–5):**

Prerequisites: a divine-magic caster role of D-Tier or above

Level 1: Devout Prayer

Description: prayer to the god is the believer's first duty.

Effect: the character's divine casting checks gain a Feat Bonus of this Feat's level in DP.

Level 2: Tempered Will

Description: only the strong-willed pass the god's trials.

Effect: as a Swift Action, the character's Will Saves gain a Feat Bonus of (this Feat's level + 1), lasting one round.

Level 3: Pure Heart

Description: only the utterly pure may step into the holiest of grounds.

Effect: choose a second Virtue from the Virtues. Each of your Virtues can trigger once per film.

Level 4: Grasping the Oracle

Description: read every scripture; glimpse the doctrine.

Effect: choose Occult or Academics — the chosen skill rises one level and gains all its Professions; pick one Profession from Occult or Academics, whose bonus becomes 3 DP.

Level 5: God-Favored

Description: your devotion has caught the god's eye; oracles descend upon you.

Effect: twice per day, the character may pray for 10 minutes, then make a PCT + Academics-or-Occult-level check to put one question to the god. The ST answers according to your successes; the intelligence must be true, but not necessarily complete. In certain special scenes and moments the ST may decline to provide anything.

**Sleepwalker (2):**

Prerequisites: CMT 3

Level 2: legend says Cao Cao could kill in his dreams — you're better: you can finish homework in yours.

While asleep, you are not helpless and may act normally, but on any active check, each relevant Stat above 2 counts as 2 and each relevant skill above 1 counts as 1, and you cannot use supernatural abilities. Nothing you do while sleeping disturbs your rest.
