# Card-Building Feats

These Feats can only be chosen when building the character card.

===

Please note:

Because the Feats below are chosen at card creation, they cannot be bought through normal means —

even if you obtained XP through some special means during card creation, they cannot be bought with XP.

===

**Special Identity (1–5)**

Prerequisites: none

Level 1: perhaps you once spent time at a shooting club, never get lost when camping, or have a modest reputation on certain IT forums… in short, you stand out from those around you just a little.

Designate one relevant skill: its card-building cap rises to 4. On entering a film you may also carry some simple items — a knife, a laptop — with a total value not exceeding 200 points. (For carried items not on the resource lists, the ST sets the price.)

Level 2: you might be a tycoon's illegitimate son, a famous racer's personal mechanic, a mercenary who lives on the knife's edge… whatever the case, your history is out of the ordinary, and rarely seen.

At card creation, gain 2 free Skill points or 4 extra Professions, placed on any skill while still following the other card-building rules. You may carry items ordinary people can hardly obtain — a small revolver, climbing gear — total value not exceeding 300 points. (Unlisted items priced by the ST.)

Level 3: you have won Olympic gold, you command a force loyal to you, you've hacked the M-country defense ministry more than once… you hold a top position in some trade.

After the card is complete, designate up to three level-3 skills and raise them immediately to 4. You may carry items that would astonish ordinary folk — a sniper rifle, a specially modified computer — total value not exceeding 400 points. (Unlisted items priced by the ST.)

Level 5: you possess qualities beyond the ordinary — mental or physical: sometimes just an uncanny affinity for electronics, sometimes an irrepressible thirst for blood… some part of you already belongs to the supernatural.

Choose one exchange of Tier D or below (DD-Tier not allowed); if the exchange is growable, you must take its lowest stage. You gain it — treated as having awakened your dormant nature at a bargain price. The chosen exchange cannot be tradable, and if it is a template ability, you count as holding that template. Explain it fully in your character background.

You may carry any items consistent with your background, total value not exceeding 500 points. (Unlisted items priced by the ST.)

Special: this Feat has no level 4.

Special: higher levels of this Feat replace lower-level effects (they do not stack).

Special: this Feat may be bought repeatedly for different levels' effects. For instance, a guerrilla who used to belong to a shooting club can buy Special Identity 1 and Special Identity 2 separately — raising the Firearms card cap to 4 and gaining 2 free Skill points.

Special: carried items must fit the character's background, and the ST may forbid particular items from entering.

Special: under the created-being rules, no items may be carried in.

**Towering Build (2)**

Prerequisites: taking this Feat bars the "Dwarfish Build" Defect.

Level 2: you stand over 2 meters and weigh over 120 kg.

The character's Size rises to 6, and STR counts as 1 higher when computing carrying capacity.

===

**Uncanny Sixth Sense (3)**

Prerequisites: PCT 2, DTR 2, CMT 2

Level 3: you have a sixth sense for the supernatural. Near such things your body reacts — hair standing on end, goosebumps down the arm, a chill up the spine; it varies by person. You don't necessarily hear or see anything, or know what exactly is happening.
The character gains the Spirit Force Energy Pool and may learn and use generic spirit-force skills.

D-Tier spirit-force skills: DTR 6 or CMT 6

C-Tier: DTR 11 or CMT 11

B-Tier: DTR 16 or CMT 16

A-Tier: DTR 21 or CMT 21

S-Tier: DTR 31 or CMT 31

**Magical Constitution (3)**

Prerequisites: INT 2, Occult skill level 3

Description: you have gained mana that springs from within you.

Level 3: you gain the Mana Energy Pool and may buy and learn generic magic normally, so long as your Stats and Occult meet the requirements.

Note, however: this does not count as holding a high caster rank — you merely qualify to learn high-Tier spells. You cannot uptier low-Tier magic this way, and your caster rank always counts as D.

D-Tier spells: Occult 4, INT 6

C-Tier: Occult 7, INT 11

B-Tier: Occult 10, INT 16

A-Tier: Occult 12, INT 21

S-Tier: Occult 14, INT 31

**City-Toppling Beauty (1–4)**

Prerequisites: DMR 4. If the character's appearance changes through disguise or other abilities, all benefits of this Feat are lost.

Level 1:

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Socialize-related checks.

Level 2:

Units whose initial attitude toward the character is "Indifferent" feel an instinctive fondness: their initial attitude rises 1 step, to at most "Friendly" (see the "Socialize" skill for attitudes). You may still end up Hostile through botched negotiation.

Level 3:

So long as you take no aggressive or hostile action (including but not limited to intimidation, persuasion, buffing teammates, debuffing targets), enemies will not prioritize you as an attack target (with you and another target both in range, they attack the other first — though area attacks may still catch you). This is a DD-Tier mind-affecting effect.

Level 4:

Once per day, the character may spend a Whole-round Action and 2 Willpower to choose one target within 10 meters; the target must have sight and be able to see the character. The character makes a DMR check, gaining a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level, opposed by the target's Will check. If the target loses, every 2 points of margin raise the target's attitude 1 step, to at most "Warm", lasting until the character leaves the target's sensory range. When it ends, the target's attitude drops immediately to "Wary" — or to "Hostile" if their initial attitude was already "Wary". If the target wins the contest, their attitude toward the character immediately drops two steps, to at worst "Hostile".

This ability works only once per target.

Note: this Feat only works on beings that share your species' sense of beauty — as a human, only on species with human aesthetics; ST's call.

**Wolf Child / Tarzan (3)**

Prerequisites: INT 1 or below

Level 3: human children raised by wolves are called wolf children — born of man, living as beasts. Their existence proves that human knowledge and ability are not innate, that walking upright and speech are not born instincts.

You are immune to difficult terrain of natural environments and gain the Feat "Iron Stomach", and your natural weapons can deal Lethal damage. Your only native tongue is Wolf; Feats and skills may only be assigned to beast-related abilities.

If you choose Tarzan instead: you climb without climb checks, at half base speed, and your jump checks always count as having a run-up. Your only native tongue is Ape; Feats and skills may only be assigned to beast-related abilities.

Like a beast you dread fire: on seeing flame you unavoidably gain 1 abnormal-emotion point — Fear.

===

**Natural-Born Femboy (0)**

Prerequisites: male

Description: you were born as adorable or as bewitching as a girl, though you are male.

Effect: your build is slight and delicate, your face like a girl's.

For the prerequisites and effects of resource exchanges other than Feats, Talents, Defects, and Quirks, you count as female rather than male. But for negative effects that target sex you also count as male, and for sex-targeted attacks and organ-targeted effects you still count as male.

===

**Child of the Wasteland (2)**

Prerequisites: EDR 3

Description: you are one who scraped out survival in the wastes of a ruined world.

Effect: you are immune to Natural-category toxins and diseases, and your maximum HP rises by 2.

===

**Barbarian (2)**

Prerequisites: one Physical Stat at 3, Survival skill level 3+

Description: you come from one of the old tribes the world calls "uncivilized".

Effect: you are immune to difficult terrain of natural environments. Choose STR, AGI, or EDR to gain 1 point — this does not change the card-building Stat caps. You dislike over-heavy armor: while not wearing heavy armor, your base speed rises by 4 meters.

===

**Man from the Future (3)**

Prerequisites: INT 3

Description: you were born into a fantastical future world of science fiction.

Effect: you carry evolved genes: your INT-related checks gain 1 AS, and you gain all Professions of the Science skill, with the Profession Bonus becoming 2.

===

**XX Enthusiast (1)**

Prerequisites: any skill at 1

Effect: choose one of your non-zero skills and gain one Profession under it; that Profession's bonus becomes 3.

Special: may be taken multiple times, choosing a different Profession each time.

===

**Life's Potential (1)**

Prerequisites: EDR 4, DMR 4

Description: life carries the potential to evolve — and even among the living, you are the exceptional one.

Effect: your Gene Lock opening checks gain +1 to the result.
