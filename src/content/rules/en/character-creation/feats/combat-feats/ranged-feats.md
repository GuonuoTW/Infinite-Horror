# Ranged Feats

**Gunner (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 3, EDR 3, Firearms skill 2 with the "Machine Gun" Profession

You must use a firearm with the **[Suppressive Fire]** property to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Wild Spray

The machine gun predates the ballpoint pen by fifty years — so you've had that much longer to practice pouring out ammunition instead of writing novels.

When performing **[Suppressive Fire]**, the character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on the firearms attack check.

Level 2: Fierce Suppression

Your rain of bullets keeps their heads down — literally.

When the character performs **[Suppressive Fire]**, its area counts as difficult terrain. Affected enemies may choose to ignore the terrain — but if they do, all their Reflex checks inside it have their successes halved.

Level 3: Precise Suppression

After enough real combat, you know how to pour your bullet-rain onto the crucial spot at the crucial moment, rather than spraying blindly.

When performing **[Suppressive Fire]**, the character may exempt up to (Firearms skill × 1) targets from its effect without their firearms attack check suffering; weapon damage during **[Suppressive Fire]** also rises by 1.

Level 4: Storm and Tempest

A storm woven of gunfire isn't necessarily more precise, mightier, or stronger — it has merely grown from tornado into hurricane.

During **[Suppressive Fire]**, weapon damage rises 1 further, and the suppressed area doubles. Targets saving with Reflex checks against the character's **[Suppressive Fire]** damage also take an Environmental Penalty equal to this Feat's level on those checks.

Level 5: Sweeping Away Armies

Give you one machine gun and you can wipe out an army; your name gets spoken alongside Rambo and Ijuin Hayato.

During **[Suppressive Fire]**, weapon damage rises further by half the character's Firearms skill. And by paying 1 Willpower when firing, you may raise the weapon damage again by your Legendary STR or Legendary AGI (whichever is higher).

**Gun-and-Blade Combat (1–5)**

Prerequisites: INT 3, Blade 3, Firearms 3; one hand holding a blade weapon and the other a firearm of Size 3 or less.

Level 1: Crossing Cover

Description: shot, sword-turn, shot again — nice.

Effect: after making a ranged firearm attack, your next Blade attack's check with the blade weapon gains a (Feat level)-point Feat Bonus — and vice versa.

Level 2: Bravado

Description: "Shff-shff-shff-shff—" "I am the sword-saint of Ashina — Isshin Ashina!" "Bang!"

Effect: as a Standard Action, attack one target once with the firearm and once with the blade — two attacks. The first deals no damage; the target contests its successes with PCT + Empathy (Reading People), and on a loss, loses Evade Defense equal to the margin against the second attack.

Level 3: Opening Gambit of Deceit

Description: hesitation is defeat.

Effect: on your first attack each round, if you feint, you may add the AS from your Firearms or Blade skill level as DP — an Insight Bonus — to the feint contest.

Level 4: Any Means Necessary

Description: "Taste my iaijutsu!!" "You dog — what are you pulling a gun for!"

Effect: after winning a feint contest, the character may borrow next round's Standard Action to make one skill-free Blade attack against the target, gaining a 6 DP Insight Bonus on the attack.

Level 5: The Ashina Style

Description: the Ashina sword arts are invincible.

Effect: in feint contests, gain a DP bonus equal to the weapon damage of the blade weapon or firearm in your hands, capped at half your INT.

===

**Master Sniper (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, CMT 3, DTR 3, Firearms skill 3 with the "Rifle" Profession

You must use a firearm classified "Rifle" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Shooter's Focus

With a gun in hand, every hardship and pain slips your mind.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on ranged attack checks with rifles.

Level 2: Patient Aim

A long silence to herald the fatal shot.

When making aimed attacks, the bonus cap available from aiming rises by this Feat's level; the character's ranged firearm attacks also always count as having aimed 1 round.

Level 3: Tactical Intervention

Split-second choices and iron will let you seize the smallest advantage for a precise shot.

When making aimed attacks, each round aimed ignores 1 point of the target's cover-derived defense bonus, up to your aiming bonus cap. The character's ranged firearm attacks also always count as having aimed 1 round, stacking with the level-2 effect.

Level 4: Pinpoint Strike

Precision that slips through every crack in the defense.

On ranged rifle attacks, you may convert some or all of the attack DP earned from aiming into an equal amount of extra Breach, or ignore an equal amount of called-shot penalty.

Level 5: One Shot, One Kill

When you pick up your rifle, everyone hits the dirt — simple as that.

As a Swift Action, all this round's ranged firearm attacks may take an Unnamed Penalty up to this Feat's level, gaining the same number of AS.

===

**Master of Bombardment (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, INT 3, Firearms skill 2 with the "Cannon" Profession

You must use a firearm classified "Cannon" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Bursting Barrage

The character ignores (Feat level × 2) of the Machinery Penalty from the "heavy weapon" property; once reduced to 0, every 2 surplus points grant the attack check a 1 DP Feat Bonus.

Level 2: Carpet Bombing

Where bombardment areas overlap, targets inside take 1 extra damage.

Level 3: Precision Bombing

When bombarding, the character may exempt up to (Firearms skill × 1) targets; weapon damage while bombarding also rises 1.

Level 4: Interdiction Bombing

The bombardment area counts as Natural-category difficult terrain. Enemies may ignore it — but then all their Reflex checks inside it have successes halved.

The bombardment's area also doubles; targets saving with Reflex checks against its damage take an Environmental Penalty equal to this Feat's level, sourced from the natural environment.

Level 5: Ultimate Bombardment

While bombarding, weapon damage rises further by half the character's Firearms skill. Paying 1 Willpower when firing raises it again by Legendary STR or Legendary AGI (whichever is higher).

===

**Dual-Pistol Shooting (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, Firearms skill 2

The character must hold a firearm of Size 3 or less in each hand to use and gain all of this Feat's benefits.

Level 1: Two-Gun Fire

One report, two lives.

The character may spend a Whole-round Action to make one ranged firearm attack with each hand's gun — not both at the same target. The character loses Base Defense this turn.

Level 2: Two-Gun Rapid Fire

Ever meet Neo?

When using the level-1 effect, the character may move up to their base speed before or after shooting. Declare before attacking whether you'll move this turn; each meter moved (or to be moved) through this ability imposes a 1 DP Unnamed Penalty on all this turn's ranged firearm attack checks.

Level 3: Gun-Fu

The gun extends the arm; the bullet extends the gun.

Using the level-1 effect no longer costs Base Defense, and both ranged firearm attacks gain a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level.

Level 4: Two-Gun Sweep

Standing still, you are a death god announcing itself in bullets. Moving, you are a death god in motion.

Movement through the level-2 effect no longer imposes penalties; and when computing multiple-attack penalties, attacks made with "Two-Gun Fire" no longer count as a single attack.

Level 5: Two-Gun Suppression

Hey pal — know what a hail of bullets looks like?

When both held firearms have the **[Full Auto]** property, the character may perform a **[Suppressive Fire]**: its damage uses the higher of the two guns' weapon damage, its ammunition cost may draw on both guns' ammunition combined, all abilities affecting **[Suppressive Fire]** apply (the "Gunner" Feat, for instance), and the suppression gains both weapons' properties.

===

**Shotgun (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 3, Firearms skill 2 with the "Shotgun" Profession

You must use a firearm classified "Shotgun" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Spreading Shot

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on ranged firearm attack checks.

Level 2: Fragmenting Shot

The character's shotgun counts as having the **[Explosive Projectile]** property.

Level 3: Point-Blank Shot

On ranged shotgun attacks against units within 5 meters, damaged targets must make a STR + Athletics (Balance) or AGI + Athletics (Balance) check at DC equal to the damage taken, gaining +1 DP Unnamed Bonus per point of their Size. On a failure, the character may knock them prone or drive them back the margin in meters; a target slammed into an obstacle takes the remaining meters as blunt Blunt damage.

Level 4: Winding Shot

On ranged shotgun attacks against units within 5 meters, the shotgun counts as having the **[Stun]** property for that shot, plus 2 extra points of **[Breach]**.

Level 5: Overpowered Shot

The character may spend a Standard Action and 1 Willpower to make a ranged firearm attack that also inflicts bleeding points equal to the check's successes; the target saves with Fortitude.

===

**Combat Marksmanship (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 2, AGI 2, CMT 3, Firearms skill 2 with the "Pistol" Profession

You must use a firearm classified "Pistol" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Shoot First

The character's Initiative rises by (this Feat's level × 1).

Level 2: Tactical Reload

Once per round, the character may swap a pistol's magazine as a Free Action, or reload a revolver with a speedloader. Without a speedloader, a Free Action loads a revolver with at most one round.

Level 3: Double Tap

At close-attack distance, targets cannot ignore the firearm's **[High Speed]** advantage; the character also gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on ranged firearm attack checks.

Level 4: Lethal Range

Long bursts and full-auto no longer penalize the character for automatically attacking targets adjacent to the mark; and the first target struck by the character's ranged firearm attack in a battle is flat-footed — a D-Tier flat-footed effect.

Level 5: Rapid Fire

Pistol attacks gain AS equal to the burst grade used (Short Burst = 1, Long Burst = 2, Full Auto = 3). Long bursts and full-auto fired through this ability do not — and cannot — spill over onto targets adjacent to the mark.

**Cowboy (1–4)**

Prerequisites: CMT 2+, AGI and Firearms skill level 4+

Level 1: Do It Proper

Description: emptying your cylinder in 2 seconds is barely the entrance exam.

Effect: your weapon gains the **[Short Burst]** and **[Long Burst]** properties.

Level 2: Under the Blazing Sun

Description: …there is nowhere to dodge.

Effect: on ranged firearm attacks, you may ignore a combined total of up to (this Feat's level + 1) points of the target's Evade or guard bonuses on defense, priority yours.

Level 3: Second-Round Duel

Description: thousands of bullets spread a curtain of brass across the sky.

Effect: so long as the ammunition is in your hands, you may reload your held weapon without an action — even while attacking with it. Special: if some effect lets you spend extra ammunition on an attack for DP or other gains, this Feat cannot be applied while using it.

Level 4: High Noon

Description: It's high noon.

Effect: spend a Whole-round Action to make one ranged firearm attack against up to (this Feat's level × 1) separate units within the weapon's base range.

===

**Brass Coin (1–5)**

Prerequisites: Firearms skill level 3 with the "Pistol" Profession

You must use a revolver to gain this Feat's benefits. (The rules file revolvers under "Pistol", so the ST may extend the benefit to "Pistol"-class weapons generally, or rule which pistols count as revolvers.)

Level 1: Blind Outpour

Effect: on ranged firearm attacks using **[Short Burst]**, **[Long Burst]**, or **[Full Auto]**, the bonus from that property rises by (this Feat's level × 1).

Level 2: Seasoned Hand

Effect: on ranged firearm attacks, the Profession Bonus from Firearms Professions increases by 3.

Level 3: Gun-Blazing Preacher

Description: "The Mass reeked of gunpowder."

Effect: on ranged firearm attacks against prone targets, ignore 6 points of the target's Evade bonus on defense; and if the target has cover but not total cover, the cover grade drops one step (heavy cover → partial cover → slight cover → none).

Level 4: Stopping Power

Description: "…the horseshoe caught Minsk square in the chest; his pectorals caved, and he didn't even scream — his limbs simply went slack, sinking inch by inch until he lay on the ground, and there was nothing left but the sound of the wind."

Effect: on ranged firearm attacks, the attack's damage cap rises by 6.

Level 5: Tap-Dance Tune

Description: "Liven up, darling."

Effect: when a ranged firearm attack targets a single unit and applies **[Long Burst]** or **[Full Auto]**, the attack check gains 1 AS — and solely for computing the multiple-attack penalty they suffer, the attack counts as six attacks against them.

===

**Master of Concealed Weapons (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, Athletics skill 2 with the "Light Throwing Weapons" Profession

Level 1: Cosmos in the Sleeve

Throwing attacks with weapons classified "Concealed Weapon" or "Light Throwing Weapon" gain High Speed equal to this Feat's level.

Level 2: Nimble Fingers

Drawing "Concealed Weapon" / "Light Throwing Weapon" arms is a Free Action, and you may draw as many as you need at once.

Level 3: Rain of Flowers Filling the Sky

Throwing attacks with "Concealed Weapon" / "Light Throwing Weapon" arms may use Short Burst / Long Burst / Full Auto, consuming that many identical concealed weapons. If mixed weapons are thrown at once, base statistics use the lowest among them, and special properties use the lowest Tier (price-lowest on a Tier tie).

Level 4: Two Birds, One Stone

As a Standard Action, a throwing attack with "Concealed Weapon" / "Light Throwing Weapon" arms may strike up to (this Feat's level) targets at once — no repeat attacks on one target, and no target may be caught twice by area-damage abilities (like Full Auto). Paying 1 Willpower removes the action cost, folding the ability into other light-weapon throwing attack actions.

Level 5: The One Great Throw

As a Swift Action, all this round's throwing attacks may take an Unnamed Penalty up to this Feat's level, gaining the same number of AS.

===

**Archer (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 3, Archery skill 3

You must use a weapon classified "Bow" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Archer's Gift

Every muscle in your arm was born to draw and loose.

When judging a bow's STR requirement and maximum range, your STR counts as raised by this Feat's level.

Level 2: Rapid Nocking

You draw and loose too fast for the eye — like a flash of light.

Drawing arrows from a quiver is a Free Action, in whatever number you need.

Level 3: Arcing Shot

You loose the arrow skyward, letting the parabola do the killing.

Ranged bow attacks double their base range. If the target's defense holds cover bonuses, you may ignore up to (this Feat's level × 1) of them.

Level 4: Multishot

Several arrows on the string at once, for several enemies.

As a Standard Action, a ranged bow attack may strike up to (this Feat's level) targets at once — no repeats on one target, and no target caught twice by area-damage abilities (like Full Auto). Paying 1 Willpower removes the action cost, folding it into other archery attack actions.

Level 5: Willow Leaf at a Hundred Paces

Your archery could contend with Lie Yukou himself.

As a Standard Action, make one bow attack gaining a Feat Bonus equal to your Archery skill level. Paying 1 Willpower removes the action cost, folding it into other archery attack actions.

===

**Heavy-Weapon Throwing Expert (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, STR 3, Athletics skill 3

Level 1: Throwing Mastery

Your control over the throw is second nature.

Ranged throwing attack checks with **[Heavy Throwing Weapon]** items gain a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level.

Level 2: Charging Throw

Your throws enjoy the benefits of a charge.

The character may now make charging throws. Besides the charge's benefits (see the "charge attack" rules), each meter moved during the charge adds 2 meters to the throw's maximum range, with distance penalties as normal.

Level 3: Titanic Throw

The thrown weapon is an extension of your arm.

When throwing, the character's STR counts as (this Feat's level) higher for STR requirements, maximum range, and the damage cap.

Level 4: Mighty Throw

Your throws pierce metal and stone, swift beyond reckoning.

The character may make all-out attacks when throwing; doing so, the throw also gains **[High Speed]** advantage equal to this Feat's level.

Level 5: The One Great Throw

You can pour your whole heart into a single throw.

As a Swift Action, all this round's throwing attacks may take an Unnamed Penalty up to this Feat's level, gaining the same number of AS.

===

**Brute Throw (2)**

Prerequisites: STR 3, Athletics skill 3

Level 2: Strength Works Miracles

On any ranged throwing attack check keyed to AGI, the character may substitute STR; heavy-weapon throwing attack checks additionally gain 1 AS. The damage cap is unchanged.

===

**Overdraw (1)**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 3, Archery skill level 3+

Level 1: Mighty Shot

Description: hunting prey with stubborn flesh, the hunters of the wild tribes always find breakthrough strength.

When attacking with a bow, you may substitute STR for AGI on the attack check.
