# Blade Feats

**Lance Knight (1–3):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, Animal Ken skill 2, Blade skill 2

Level 1: Practiced Charge

On a mounted charge, the mount's Base Defense loss from charging is reduced by (this Feat's level × 1) — which may change the bonus cap available through mounted charges.

Level 2: Agile Charge

Before or after a mounted charge, the character's mount may make one move; the mount's total movement per turn cannot exceed its base speed.

Level 3: The Long Spike

While riding a biological vehicle, if the character makes a melee Blade attack with a **[Polearm Weapon]** and deals at least 1 damage, the struck target must make a STR + Athletics (Balance) or AGI + Athletics (Balance) check at DC equal to the damage taken. On a failure, the character may knock the target prone or drive them back a number of meters equal to the margin; a target slammed into an obstacle mid-knockback takes the remaining meters as blunt Blunt damage.

**Saber Arts (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, STR 3, Blade skill 2 with the "Saber" Profession

You must wield a blade weapon of Size above 2 — and no smaller than half your own Size — classified "Curved Saber" or "Saber" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Cross-Saber Parry

The saber wants no cautious defense — only a clean, meteor-swift offense.

When guarding with a saber-class weapon, the character gains an extra guard bonus on defense equal to this Feat's level.

Level 2: Metal-Cleaving Years

Those who cross me stand near death. Not a person — merely a thing of slaughter. Nothing uncut: that is the form I ought to have.

As a Swift Action, the character's melee Blade attacks this round gain **[Breach]** up to the weapon's damage, capped at this Feat's level.

Level 3: One with the Saber

The saber is you; you are the saber. Its artlessness mirrors your character, and so you have reached this state.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on disarm and sunder checks.

Level 4: Straight to the Point

A saber needs few tricks — only plainness and clean flow.

Melee Blade attacks with saber-class weapons gain weapon damage equal to this Feat's level.

Level 5: Tiger Pouncing to Earth

The saber is the "gall of all weapons"; they also say "the saber is a fierce tiger" — bold and forceful, perhaps without the grace of a flying immortal, but of undeniable might.

While the character wields a saber-class weapon, if a unit within melee reach attacks the character and deals no damage, the character may immediately make one melee Blade attack against it as a Reflex Action. The attack may attach no Technique or ability, though passive effects apply as normal.

===

**German Greatsword (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, Blade skill 3

You must wield a blade weapon classified "Greatsword" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: The Hew

The hew is the plainest of powerful blows.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on melee Blade attack checks.

Level 2: Overawe

If your opponent's stance is steady, try roaring at him.

The character's Intimidation (Bellow) checks gain a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level.

Level 3: The Cross-Strike

Weight and force enough to cut man and sword in half together.

When taking a melee attack, the character may spend a Reflex Action on a STR + Blade (Guard) check, using the result as their Guard Defense against that attack. Doing so, they don't lose next turn's Swift Action — they lose next turn's Movement Action instead.

Level 4: Breath in Accord

Mind and body as one; the sword moves like thunder.

When making a melee Blade attack, the character may spend a Movement Action on an Intimidation (Bellow) check, resisted by the target's Will. If the target loses, it takes a Morale Penalty on Base Defense equal to the margin for 1 round; this cannot push defense negative.

Level 5: Bind and Cross-Cut

Catch the attack — then a backhand cut as the enemy's guard falls open.

After taking a melee attack, the character may spend a Reflex Action and 1 Willpower, then make a melee Blade attack against the attacker. In this attack the character takes an Unnamed Penalty equal to the opponent's attack successes, and the attack's damage cap drops by half the key Stat — but the target also takes an Unnamed Penalty on Base Defense equal to the character's Blade skill (against this one attack only). If the target's attack dealt no damage, the character's counter takes no attack penalty.

===

**Russian Knife Fighting (2–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, Blade skill 2 with the "Dagger" Profession

You must wield a blade weapon classified "Dagger" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 2: Attack Prediction

How will you answer this one?

While holding a dagger-class blade, the character's Initiative rises by (this Feat's level) points.

Level 3: Feint Advantage

Feints need not come from the front — the flank, even the back, will do.

On an all-out attack, the character may add half their AGI as a Reinforcement Bonus to the check instead of half their STR.

Level 4: Vital Strike

Lock onto weak points and vitals; kill with one stroke.

The character ignores called-shot penalties up to this Feat's level. And when targets save against called-shot damage, they take a penalty equal to the character's Blade-level AS.

Level 5: Cut and Thrust

This is simply how a knife is meant to be used.

You may spend a Standard Action and a Movement Action to make two consecutive melee Blade attacks on a target. The first deals no damage; the damage it would have dealt is added as DP — a Reinforcement Bonus — to the second attack's check. These are two attacks, so the first inflicts the multiple-attack penalty as usual, and the first attack gains no attack AS.

===

**Axe Warrior (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 4, Blade skill 2 with the "Axe" Profession

You must wield a blade weapon classified "Greataxe" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Mountain-Splitting

Erlang saved his mother by splitting Mount Hua.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on melee Blade attack checks.

Level 2: Earth-Rending

The might that rends the earth!!

The character's weapon gains the **[Mighty]** property. If it already had **[Mighty]**, its value becomes the weapon's full Size rather than half.

Level 3: Sea-Parting

It isn't the axe that parts the sea — it's the conviction lodged in it.

The character's weapon's **[Mighty]** property now also works against the target's Base Defense and Evade Defense, and gains 2 points of **[Mighty]**.

Level 4: Sky-Tearing

A menace that tears heaven and earth apart.

Melee Blade attacks gain weapon damage equal to this Feat's level.

Level 5: Cosmos-Breaking

In the murk of primal chaos, Pangu's axe cleaved the universe open.

As a Swift Action, the character may have all melee Blade attacks this round take an Unnamed Penalty up to this Feat's level, gaining the same number of AS.

===

**Staff Arts (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 2, Blade skill 2

You must wield a weapon classified "Long Staff" or "Short Staff" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Staff Divinity

The greatest craft needs no ornament.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on melee Blade attack checks.

Level 2: Foot-Sweep

A man on the ground is no threat.

The character may trip with "Long Staff" or "Short Staff" weapons, and melee reach increases by 5 meters.

Level 3: Temple Strike

One rap and out cold.

The character's weapon gains the **[Stun]** property, and the character's melee Blade attack checks ignore (this Feat's level × 1) called-shot penalties.

Level 4: Divine Might That Spares

The staff is the weapon of divine might that does not kill.

On a melee Blade attack, if the target's health track is already filled with Lethal damage — or more than half filled with Aggravated — the character may deal no damage and instead inflict an equal number of stun points, stacking; the target saves with Fortitude.

Level 5: Danger Zone

An inch longer, an inch stronger.

While wielding "Long Staff" or "Short Staff" weapons, the space within your reach counts as Natural-category difficult terrain to all your hostile targets. And while a target stays inside your reach, its full checks take a 5 DP Environmental Penalty — an environmental penalty from the surroundings.

Special: glaives, spears, halberds, and other **[Polearm Weapon]** arms developed from the staff may also use this Feat line; ST's call.

===

**Sword-and-Board Fighting (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 3, Blade skill 2

You must hold at least one weapon or shield with the **[Guard]** property to use this Feat.

Level 1: Counter from Defense

You bleed off the enemy's momentum with your shield — and strike as his guard hangs open.

While guarding with a **[Guard]** weapon or shield: if any target makes a melee attack against you and deals no damage, then until the end of your next turn, your next melee attack against that target gains a Feat DP bonus of (this Feat's level × 2) on the attack check.

Level 2: Sword-Shield Combination

While the enemy scrambles from your shield's slam, your sword has already found his throat.

When guarding with a **[Guard]** weapon or shield, you may choose a target within your reach and take up to (this Feat's level) Unnamed Penalty on defense; your next melee attack against that target gains the same amount of **[High Speed]** advantage.

Level 3: Force-Shedding

…and each time the axe crashed against his shield, he eased it back a touch, so even the mightiest blow lost its force — the smooth face could be neither split nor shattered.

While guarding with a **[Guard]** weapon or shield, the character may ignore up to (this Feat's level) points of **[Breach]**.

Level 4: Murmillo

Your round shield turns away everything — a lion's claw, a steel trident, or the net dropping from above.

While guarding with a **[Guard]** weapon or shield, the character gains an extra guard bonus equal to this Feat's level plus 1 AS on defense, and a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Reflex checks.

Level 5: Iron Wall and Fangs

You've lost count of the enemies who lost their balance battering at your guard — you only know the number run through the heart for it is exactly the same.

"Counter from Defense" now reads "if any target makes a melee attack against you" instead of "…and deals no damage". And when it triggers, every 2 points of its Feat Bonus may become 1 AS; if "Sword-Shield Combination" triggers at the same time, your attack check gains a further 5 DP Feat Bonus.

**Fencing / Chinese Swordsmanship (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, Blade skill 3

You must wield a blade weapon of Size 2 or above, classified "Longsword", "Shortsword", or "Estoc", to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: The Thrust

The thrust is the plainest of powerful attacks.

The character gains a Feat Bonus equal to this Feat's level on Blade attack checks.

Level 2: Armor-Threading

As a Swift Action, all this round's melee Blade attacks with qualifying weapons gain **[Breach]** up to the weapon's damage, capped at this Feat's level.

Level 3: The Retreat

To advance ten paces, sometimes first retreat one.

When taking a melee attack, the character may spend a Reflex Action; against that one attack, the character counts as having taken full defense. Doing so, they lose next turn's Movement Action instead of the Swift Action.

Level 4: The Feint

If you cannot open the opponent's guard, try a feint.

The character may now feint using AGI in place of INT and Blade in place of Subterfuge, and may feint as a Movement Action.

Level 5: Riposte

Fall back a quick step — then a sudden, swift counter as the enemy's guard falls open.

After using The Retreat to evade a melee attack, the character may then make a melee Blade attack against the attacker. In this attack the character takes an Unnamed Penalty equal to the opponent's attack successes, and the attack's damage cap drops by half the key Stat — but the target also takes an Unnamed Penalty on Base Defense equal to the character's Blade skill (against this one attack only). If the target's attack dealt no damage, the counter takes no attack penalty.

===

**Shield Bash Mastery (2–3):**

Prerequisites: Blade skill 2

You must wield a weapon classified "Large Shield", "Small Shield", or "Heavy Shield" to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 2: Shield Guard

When guarding with a shield, the character gains an extra (Feat level × 1) shield bonus on defense.

Level 3: Shield Slam

In slam contests the character may count the held shield's Size into their own; and the character may slam with the shield — doing so, they may substitute Blade for Brawl in the slam contest check.

===

**Great-Weapon Warrior (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 4, Blade skill 3

You must wield, two-handed, a melee weapon of Size 3 or more — and no smaller than half your own Size — to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Mighty Blows

One savage stroke and the enemy's nerve wavers.

The character ignores (Feat level × 2) of the Machinery Penalty from the "heavy weapon" property; if the penalty drops to 0, every 2 surplus points grant the attack check a 1 DP Feat Bonus.

Level 2: Tendon-Snapping, Bone-Crushing

Your blows split mountains and crack stone; hearts quail.

As a Swift Action, this round's melee Blade attacks gain **[Breach]** up to the weapon's damage, capped at this Feat's level.

Level 3: Sweeping Cut

Every stroke drags a gale of blood and flesh behind it.

As a Whole-round Action, make one melee Blade attack dealing physical Lethal damage equal to your check's successes to every unit within the attack's reach; Reflex saves. For each unit in your reach (yourself excluded), the attack takes a 2 DP Unnamed Penalty.

Level 4: Full Power

Fell the goblins, fell the trees — even the hillocks flee into the mountains!

When activating this Feat's level-2 effect, the character may pay 1 Willpower; if so, their melee Blade attacks also ignore up to (weapon damage × 1) of the target's damage-reduction abilities — but never DR/− — capped at (this Feat's level × 1).

Level 5: Cut in Two

One single-minded stroke; nothing it cannot sever.

When activating this Feat's level-2 effect, the character may have all melee Blade attacks this round take an Unnamed Penalty up to this Feat's level, gaining the same number of AS.

===

**Spear Arts (1–5):**

Prerequisites: STR 3, AGI 2, Blade skill 2

You must use a weapon with the **[Polearm Weapon]** special property to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Spear Guard

When guarding with a **[Polearm Weapon]**, the character gains an extra guard bonus on defense equal to this Feat's level.

Level 2: Spear Like a Dragon

On a charge attack, the character may forgo the normal benefit and convert the added DP into an equal amount of **[Breach]**, stacking with **[Breach]** from other sources, up to this Feat's level.

Level 3: The Playful Dragon

As a Standard Action, make a STR + Blade check, dealing physical Lethal damage equal to the weapon's damage to enemies within your reach; affected enemies save with Reflex checks, losing 1 DP on the save per success on your attack check.

Level 4: Rainstorm of Thrusts

As a Standard Action, the character may make multiple melee Blade attacks. Each attack takes a Technique Penalty of ((melee Blade attacks already made this round + 1) × 5) DP. When the round's melee Blade attack DP falls to 0 or below, no further attacks may be made; each target may only be struck once. While doing this the character may also move with a Movement Action — attack, move, attack, move again, until they stop attacking or run out of movement.

Level 5: An Inch Longer, an Inch Stronger

With a **[Polearm Weapon]**, melee reach increases by 10 meters, and the area within 5 meters of the character counts as Natural-category difficult terrain to their enemies.

When any unit enters the character's melee reach, the character may spend a Reflex Action and 2 Willpower to immediately make one melee Blade attack against one such unit. The attack may attach no Technique or ability (passives apply as normal), and deals only Blunt damage no matter what — even if the target has no "Healthy" HP left, its damage will not convert the target's Blunt damage into Lethal or Aggravated.

===

**Two-Weapon Fighting (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, Blade skill 2

The character must hold a melee blade weapon in each hand to use and gain all of this Feat's benefits.

Level 1: Two-Weapon Guard

One hand's weapon attacks; the other weaves an airtight defense.

When guarding, the character gains an extra guard bonus on defense equal to this Feat's level.

Level 2: Two-Weapon Feint

Whichever hand strikes, the other blade's poise keeps your foe from defending with full attention.

When making a melee Blade attack, the character may spend a Movement Action to give this turn's next attack an attack DP bonus equal to the idle weapon's damage value.

Level 3: Two-Weapon Master

You are a member of the Drizzt club.

The character may spend a Whole-round Action to attack once with each hand's weapon — but the two attacks must target two different units.

Level 4: Two-Weapon Chain

Two blades mean twice the attacks.

The level-3 effect may now target the same unit, and this does not count as one attack. Also, while using the level-3 effect, the character may move up to (their Blade skill level) meters as a Free Action between the two attacks.

Level 5: Two-Weapon Wild Dance

The highest swordsmanship is, in truth, a dance.

The character may spend a Whole-round Action and 1 Willpower to make up to (Blade skill level × 1) melee Blade attacks. Each attack takes a Technique Penalty of ((melee Blade attacks already made this round + 1) × 5) DP, and these attacks count as one attack. When the round's melee Blade attack DP falls to 0 or below, no further attacks may be made; the same target may be struck repeatedly.

===

**Improvised Weapon Master (1–5):**

Prerequisites: AGI 3, STR 3, Blade skill 1, Brawl skill 1

You must use improvised weapons — or wield proper weapons as improvised ones — to use and gain all of this Feat's effects.

Level 1: Street Tough

Description: no need for words — grab it and go!

The character may treat an improvised weapon as a blade weapon of similar shape and Size, using the matching Techniques and gaining the corresponding base weapon's statistics and properties, as you decide (short-hafted and heavy-headed — warhammer; short and sharp — dagger; huge and thin — greatsword; long and pointed — spear; small and scattered — concealed weapons; and so on). A creature may even serve as an improvised weapon, provided the character has it grappled and the character's STR exceeds twice the creature's Size (counting as held).

Level 2: Brick to the Head

Description: death to all in my way!

As a Swift Action, all this round's melee Blade attacks with improvised weapons gain **[Breach]** up to the weapon's damage, capped at (this Feat's level + 1) points.

Level 3: A Hundred Arts

Description: no skill is dead weight!

While holding an improvised weapon, the character may feint as a Swift Action, substituting STR for INT and Blade for Subterfuge on the feint check.

Level 4: No-Rules Kung Fu

Description: anything can be a weapon in your hands!

Melee Blade attacks with improvised weapons gain (this Feat's level + 1) weapon damage.

Level 5: God of War of the Furniture Mall

Description: the whole furniture mall is your arsenal!

Improvised weapons in the character's hands gain **[Guard]**, **[Mighty]**, **[Magical]**, **[Light Throwing Weapon]**, and **[Stun]**.
