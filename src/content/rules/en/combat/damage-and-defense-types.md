# Damage Types and Defense Types

**Damage types**

**All damage in this game carries a damage type.**

If one instance of damage carries several types — Fire and Holy at once, say — mitigation must cover every one of its types to apply; immunity likewise requires immunity to every one of them.

E.g.: 5 points of fire-and-holy damage against a target with 3 Holy resistance and 2 Fire resistance — 2 points are negated.

If you take none of a given damage, none of its rider effects apply either.

**Physical damage**

Damage from actual objects striking, cutting, or deforming — every material weapon and improvised one: knives, clubs, teeth, half a broken bottle. Some subjects are immune to physical damage, whether for lacking material form (a soul) or because physical deformation is meaningless to them (running water, mist). Others carry a measure of resistance to it, called damage reduction (DR) — extraordinary toughness, or the protection of some strange supernatural power.

Physical damage sometimes carries a material type — adamantine, silver, cold iron — to pierce particular damage reductions.

It also splits by the manner of harm — slashing, piercing, bludgeoning — to pierce other particular damage reductions.

Note that a physical attack can carry a damage type and a material type at once: a hammer forged of adamantine deals bludgeoning damage of the 【adamantine】 material type.

For clarity, damage from firearms, crossbows, and bows carrying the "ammunition" or "Unlimited Ammo" properties but unable to deal energy damage is classed solid-round damage — all of it piercing damage.

**Energy damage**

Damage from immaterial energies. Energy damage ignores damage reduction but is negated by "energy resistance"; some effects grant outright immunity to a particular energy.

Its kinds:

Pure energy damage: energy damage describable only as "energy", inexplicable by physical action. Against objects, Hardness negates first, then 1/2 the remainder lands. Against creatures, Hardness negates first, then the full remainder lands.

Fire damage (a.k.a. burn or searing damage): harm by high heat — flames, scalding steam — covering matter lost to incineration and cells killed by heat. Against objects, Hardness negates first; a non-living object takes 1/2 the remainder, though flammables (paper, dry grass, plants — ST's call) take it all. Creatures take the full remainder.

Cold damage (a.k.a. freezing damage): harm by low temperature — bodily fluids freezing, cell activity failing, or plain cold-fracture effects. Against objects, Hardness negates first, then 1/2 the remainder. Against creatures, Hardness negates first, then the full remainder.

Lightning damage (a.k.a. shock damage): harm from strong current or discharge passing through the subject — resistive heating, physiological failure from electric shock, even molecular restructuring. Against objects, after all damage-lowering abilities, 1/2 the remainder lands — though conductive objects (creatures containing water, metal) take it all. Against creatures, it ignores Hardness and lands in full.

Acid damage (a.k.a. corrosive damage): harm by strong acid or other corrosives; rapid microbial dissolution or degradation of matter counts too. Against objects, acid ignores Hardness but deals only 1/2 damage. Against creatures, it ignores Hardness and lands in full.

Sound damage: harm from sonic vibration driving matter into violent resonance or shock. Against objects, or creatures with Hardness (plants), sound ignores Hardness and lands in full.

Radiant damage: the destructive force of high-pressure, high-resonance light — lasers, lightsabers, light-energy weapons, and spells or skills producing intense light. Against objects, or creatures with Hardness (plants), radiant damage ignores Hardness and lands in full.

Holy damage (a.k.a. sacred or positive-energy damage): damage from supernatural power tied to light or order. Against objects, Hardness negates first and only 1/2 the remainder lands. Against creatures, Hardness negates first, then the full remainder — and damage against creatures of darkness upgrades 1 grade.

Unholy damage (a.k.a. profane or negative-energy damage): damage from supernatural power tied to darkness or chaos. Against objects, Hardness negates first and only 1/2 the remainder lands. Against creatures, Hardness negates first, then the full remainder — and damage against creatures of light upgrades 1 grade.

**Mental damage**

Direct attack by mental energy is vanishingly rare. Such attacks are stopped by no physical armor — Armor Defense, Shield Defense, and Born Defense all do nothing — though defenses of other natures apply as normal. When attacked this way, the defender may add their Will to defense as a Perfect Bonus.

Unless stated otherwise, damage reduction, energy resistance, Hardness, and physical damage absorption do nothing against Mental damage.

**Field damage**

A Field is a palpable spatial anomaly formed of a special energy configuration — though not a space-type ability.

A Field is neither matter nor energy, only a palpable, effective action of force (a Field wall is not an unbreakable glass wall — merely an invisible force felt as a wall's resistance). Field damage resembles physical damage in its results, but damage reduction, physical damage absorption, and Hardness do nothing against it: the Field effect isn't material in essence — it merely produces the outcome of "change imposed on matter". Energy resistance likewise cannot touch Field damage.

Specially, though physical damage reduction fails against it, DR/− applies to Field damage as normal.

**Poison damage**

Poison damage comes from toxins affecting a character — delivered by wounds, inhalation, or otherwise — after which the poisoned target suffers the toxin's effects.

Damage reduction, Hardness, and damage-ignoring effects do nothing against Poison damage. It generally arises inside the body; plain Poison damage carries no Tier, so toxin immunity and the like block it outright. Only Poison damage folded into a Tiered toxin effect requires resisting.

**Falling damage**

Dropping from a height, or being driven into an obstacle by outside force, deals falling damage. It starts counting at 3 meters; every 3 meters beyond deals 1 physical Lethal damage, rounded up. A Reflex check may save against it, each success canceling 2 meters of distance. Because a fall is an omnidirectional concussion, physical damage reduction does nothing against it.

**Falling damage / falling-object damage**

Damage from a dropped object striking another; the dropped object is the falling object. Height counts from 3 meters, each meter above granting 1 DP; on impact, the object's weight rating (the minimum STR to carry it) adds as AS, dealing physical Lethal damage to falling object and target alike — the falling object's share is falling damage, the struck target's share is falling-object damage.

Both sides may Reflex-save, each success saving 1 damage, treated as resisting area damage; if the falling object outsizes the target, each point of difference costs the target 1 DP Size Penalty on the save.

The falling damage cap is (the lower Size of object and target) × 5, +1 per point of Size for each point the target's Hardness exceeds the falling object's.

The falling-object damage cap is (the lower Size of the two) × 5, +1 per point of Size for each point the falling object's Hardness exceeds the target's.

E.g.: a Hardness-2, Size-10 falling object strikes a Hardness-0, Size-5 target — the object's falling-damage cap is 25, the target's falling-object damage cap 35.

Since force acts both ways, an object deals falling-object damage only if it takes falling damage itself.

Note: when the struck target is the ground, treat its Size as infinite. Damaging the ground is usually meaningless, but the ST may let the fall produce physical effects (smashing through a floor, cratering the earth).

**Mixed damage**

Damage types can sometimes mix —

damage that is at once Fire and Unholy, say.

Against such damage, your defenses (absorption, negation, reduction, ignoring, reflection…) must cover all of its types to apply in full — though a defense that covers every type in the mix applies against all of them.

E.g.: you take 5 fire-and-unholy damage holding 2 Fire resistance and 1 Unholy resistance — you take 4 in the end.

E.g.: you take 5 fire-and-unholy damage holding 3 all-energy resistance — you take 2.

E.g.: you take 5 fire-and-physical damage, immune to fire, with 3 physical damage reduction — you take 2.

**Vulnerability**

Every damage type above has a matching "vulnerability". Unless stated otherwise, a target with a vulnerability takes double the final damage of the matching type. With "vulnerability X", the target's final matching damage rises by X, at the grade of the lower portion of the damage dealt.

Multiple "vulnerability X" effects all stack.

E.g.: with 【fire vulnerability 1】, 5 fire damage becomes 6.

E.g.: with 【fire vulnerability 1】 and 【fire vulnerability】, 5 fire damage becomes 11.

E.g.: with 【fire vulnerability 1】 and 【fire vulnerability 2】, 5 fire damage becomes 8.

E.g.: with 【fire vulnerability 1】 and 【unholy vulnerability 2】, 5 mixed fire-and-unholy damage becomes 5 mixed fire-and-unholy damage plus 1 fire damage plus 2 unholy damage.

**Defense types:**

**Every damage-lowering ability in this game bears one of the following keywords:**

**Saving, Absorbing, Negating, Conversion, Reduction, Ignoring. No damage-lowering exists without a keyword; every damage-lowering ability in the resources carries one.**

**Damage Saving**

Damage Saving nullifies damage through a capability of your own —

generally through some check: Will Saves, Reflex Saves, Fortitude Saves.

An ability dealing damage or effects that cannot be saved against will say so; otherwise compute damage normally.

An ability that can be saved against generally states the required save and the save's benefit; Negative Status saves follow the base rules.

Area damage with no stated save defaults to Reflex Saves.

Single-target damage with no stated save defaults to unsaveable.

**Damage Absorbing**

Damage Absorbing nullifies damage. Absorption X means each instance of damage you take has X points absorbed — nullified.

Two kinds:

Physical damage absorption: absorbs only physical damage, falling damage, and falling-object damage.

All-damage absorption: absorbs physical, energy, falling, falling-object, Mental, and Field damage.

Damage absorption does not stack.

E.g. 1: one ability grants 2 all-damage absorption and another grants 3 — take the higher; only 3 applies.

E.g. 2: a character with 5 physical absorption and 3 all-damage absorption, facing physical damage — take the higher; the 5 applies.

**Physical damage reduction (DR)**

Damage reduction nullifies physical damage. DR X means each instance of physical damage has X points resisted — nullified.

DR works only against physical damage. Most DR has a weak point — void against a particular source — written DRX/source.

DR/−: works against all physical damage — the creature or object resists the physical utterly, with no weakness at all.

DR/magic: weapons or damage with the 【Magical】 property pierce it.

DR/divine arms: weapons or damage with the 【Divine Arms】 property pierce it. Some beings fuse mighty will with material existence and grow preternaturally solid; only weapons carrying equally mighty will can wound them.

DR/material: DR/silver, DR/adamantine, and so on — weapons or damage of that material pierce it. Some creatures and objects are acutely sensitive to a material, whether because its substance acts specially on their tissues, or because the material means something to them in philosophical or supernatural terms.

DR/damage type: DR/piercing, DR/bludgeoning, DR/slashing — weapons or damage of that type pierce it. A creature's surface may be firm enough to turn attacks that can't concentrate force, yielding only to point-thrusting weapons; another's structure may be dense enough inside and out to shrug off edged cuts, breakable only by the heavy mass of bludgeons; a third may be so supple that great blunt blows and small punctures alike leave its functions untouched, undone only by broad blades opening long, deep wounds.

Some abilities produce special DR types besides.

Damage reduction does not stack.

E.g. 1: one ability grants DR 2, another DR 3 — take the higher; only 3 applies.

E.g. 2: a character with 5 DR/magic and 3 DR/divine arms, facing ordinary physical damage — take the higher; the 5 DR/magic applies.

**Energy resistance**

Energy resistance mitigates energy damage. Resistance X means each instance of energy damage has X points reduced — nullified.

It works only on energy damage, and most resistances cover one energy each — lightning resistance, fire resistance — while no pure-energy resistance exists.

All-energy resistance: works against every energy damage at once, pure energy included.

Special cases:

Fire resistance, beyond negating its value in damage, ignores the environmental penalties of high-heat environments; fire immunity ignores every environmental effect of heat.

Cold resistance, beyond negating its value in damage, ignores the environmental penalties of low-temperature environments; cold immunity ignores every environmental effect of cold.

Energy resistance does not stack.

E.g. 1: one ability grants 2 all-energy resistance, another 3 — take the higher; only 3 applies.

E.g. 2: a character with 5 fire resistance and 3 all-energy resistance, facing fire — take the higher; the 5 applies.

**Damage Conversion**

Damage Conversion downgrades certain damage through particular protections — 【Bulletproof】 against ranged solid-round weapons, say.

Such defenses generally match specific damage types.

The same conversion ability never applies twice for coming from different sources:

a character wearing 【Bulletproof】 armor who is also personally 【Bulletproof】 doesn't get the conversion twice.

Unless stated otherwise, damage-downgrading abilities bottom out at Blunt damage.

Different conversion abilities resolve in the order their user prefers, and the converted damage may be drawn from whichever portion of the damage the user chooses.

**Hardness**

Hardness is the defense of a character's or object's own physical solidity.

Against physical damage, each effective point of Hardness X means each instance of damage has X points negated — nullified.

Against energy damage, Hardness works differently — see the "energy damage" section.

Mental, Field, and Poison damage ignore Hardness.

Characters generally gain Hardness through special abilities; on gaining it, whether its wording says "obtain" or "gain/add" decides whether it stacks.

**Damage Negating**

Some abilities provide damage negation.

Generally, negation spends something (usually an Energy Pool) to cancel damage; the cost, action, and points negated are detailed in each ability.

Damage negation does not stack.

Unless stated otherwise, negation can cancel physical, energy, falling, falling-object, Mental, and Field damage.

**Damage Ignoring**

Some abilities provide damage ignoring, in two general forms:

1. Ignoring all damage dealt under some condition — ignoring damage from weapons of weapon damage 3 or below, say: any such damage is ignored entirely.

2. Ignore X: each instance of damage you take has X points ignored — nullified.

Each ability details its specifics.

Damage ignoring does not stack.

Abilities that bypass damage ignoring bypass both forms above.

Unless stated otherwise, ignoring can cancel physical, energy, falling, falling-object, Mental, and Field damage.

**Shared damage**

Some abilities let several characters share damage or Negative Status points.

Unless stated otherwise, the original damage and points are split as evenly as possible among them, rounded up.

Damage reduced by another's sharing does not count as a mitigation ability.

The shared portions are unavoidable for all recipients; and in tracing sources, only the character originally struck can trace the source — the others' shares count as sourceless.

Appendix:

A character's defenses apply first to the lower grades (Blunt — Lethal — Aggravated) of the attacker's damage.

1) The declared defense AS subtract from the attacker's base success dice. On a hit, judge immunity to the effect — the user cannot know beforehand whether the target is immune. If not immune, the attacker computes the final damage; the damage cap applies here.

2) Damage immunity applies.

3) Damage Ignoring applies.

4) Hardness applies.

5) Damage Absorbing applies. For mixed damage where your physical absorption applies, set it aside and compute it together at step 7.

6) Damage Conversion applies. With several conversions, the attacker's compute first, then the defender's — the defender's in shield–armor–self order.

7) Energy resistance or damage reduction applies.

8) Damage Negating applies.

Special: if a character immune to Blunt damage computes an attack out to Lethal, and their conversion ability then downgrades it to Blunt — the immunity stage has already passed, so the Blunt damage lands as normal.

Example:

A attacks B: attack DP 100, attack AS 10, dealing fire Lethal damage with a damage cap of 30; on a hit, half the damage upgrades 1 grade and 5 lightning damage lands besides.

B declares 10 defense AS against the attack.

A rolls 35 successes; minus 10 defense AS leaves 20 — a hit — plus 10 attack AS. Total damage 35, but A's cap is 30: so 15 fire Lethal + 15 fire Aggravated, plus the 5 extra lightning.

Now B's defenses:

B is immune to lightning — A's damage is now 15 fire Lethal + 15 fire Aggravated.

B has 2 fire damage ignoring — 13 fire Lethal + 15 fire Aggravated.

B has 2 Hardness — 11 fire Lethal + 15 fire Aggravated.

B has 3 all-damage absorption — 8 fire Lethal + 15 fire Aggravated.

B can downgrade 5 damage one grade — 13 fire Lethal + 10 fire Aggravated.

B has 3 fire resistance — 10 fire Lethal + 10 fire Aggravated.

B has 4 damage negation — 6 fire Lethal + 10 fire Aggravated.

Final result: A deals 6 fire Lethal and 10 fire Aggravated damage.

Were B carrying 【fire vulnerability】, the final tally becomes 12 fire Lethal and 20 fire Aggravated.

Were B also carrying 【fire vulnerability 3】 on top, it becomes 12 fire Lethal and 23 fire Aggravated.

The rules above show that launching an attack, hitting, and dealing damage are distinct things.

Launching an attack is whatever you get merely for attacking, hit or not.

Hitting means that after your attack check, your successes exceed the target's defense AS — decided back at step one.

On a hit, the target's mitigation begins to bite — your damage may even be entirely consumed by their damage reduction and the like, dealing nothing in the end, yet the attack still hit.

Note: where resources say "make a successful attack", they mean a hit.

Note: where resources say "guard succeeds" or "defense succeeds", they mean the attacker missed — not that no damage was dealt.

Note: "defense success" means the attacker failed to hit. "Guard success" means the attacker failed to hit while the defender was guarding.

Note: where a resource reads "make an attack check against the target; this attack deals no damage, instead inflicting XX" — though it never says "on a hit / on dealing damage", the skill remains an on-hit rider: you must still hit before the substituted effect applies.
