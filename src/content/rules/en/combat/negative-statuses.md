# Negative Statuses

**Negative Statuses:**

A Negative Status means the character sits inside some adverse effect, one that generally imposes penalties in certain circumstances.

A Negative Status is a state:

while in it, the character receives certain fixed inherent effects.

Some inherent Negative Statuses need no computation — once gained, the character immediately suffers the fixed effect: held, dazed, airborne, and the like.

Others compute from Negative Status points: the character suffers effects according to the points they carry.

Negative Status points generally have a value plus one or two Stats as their **key resisting Stats**. By the relation of points to key resisting Stats, Negative Status points grade into three levels:

**Mild Negative Status:** while the points are below the higher of the key resisting Stats, the status is mild. The points merely hamper some behavior — blurring vision, loosening the sword-grip — imposing penalties in certain respects only.

**Severe Negative Status:** when the points reach or exceed the higher of the key resisting Stats, the status turns severe. It may then bring on some specific inherent Negative Status, strip a capability outright, forbid certain acts — blinding the character, opening their hands, and so on. Once severe, unless stated otherwise, the mild status's penalties still apply.

**Ruinous consequence:** when the points exceed twice the sum of all key resisting Stats, ruin follows: the character permanently loses some capability or enters an extreme abnormal state — losing eyes, losing ears. Natural recovery and ordinary healing cannot cure this; only certain high-Tier special abilities, or the Lord God, can.

Note: when the points fall back below the key resisting Stats, a severe Negative Status reverts to mild.

**Multiple Negative Statuses:**

Unless specially stated, multiple same-named Negative Status point pools do not stack — at any moment the highest applies. But each pool exists separately and must be removed or resisted separately.

Effects from multiple *different* Negative Status points do stack.

Some effects are one Negative Status delivering another — a drowning curse is a curse whose effect drops the character into the suffocation status. In that case, when the curse ends, the suffocation ends with it.

Some sources inflict several Negative Statuses at once; unless stated otherwise, save against each separately.

If the affected character has a resistance bonus against one of the statuses in an effect, it applies to that status only.

If you remove the Negative Status an effect delivered rather than the effect itself, then as long as any Negative Status from the effect remains on the character, the effect has not ended.

Some effects carry plural sources — a magical toxin is both magic and toxin. If the character has a bonus against only one source, the bonus still applies; with bonuses against all sources, they don't stack — take the higher. But only a character immune to every source of the effect is immune to the effect.

**Computing Negative Status points:**

Negative Status points generally come in stacking and non-stacking varieties; a few come in only one.

Track them separately. If player A takes 10 non-stacking nausea points and then 8 stacking nausea points, his gauge reads:

Nausea (non-stacking): 10

Nausea (stacking): 8

The effective nausea points are 10; judge penalties from that number.

After a 15-minute rest and one save, he removes points as he wishes: dropping 5 from the non-stacking pool leaves —

Nausea (non-stacking): 5

Nausea (stacking): 8

Now the effective points are 8.

If he then takes 4 more non-stacking nausea points, they do nothing, being no higher than the current non-stacking pool (5) — but if he was resting, the 15-minute Short Rest is interrupted and restarts.

If he next takes 3 stacking nausea points:

Nausea (non-stacking): 5

Nausea (stacking): 11

Effective points: 11.

Specially, if an ability directly increases the Negative Status points on a target, the increase applies to every qualifying pool on the target.

E.g., Frost: on dealing damage with this weapon, if the target has freeze points, raise them by 2 —

in which case every freeze pool on the target, stacking or not, rises by 2.

**Resisting Negative Statuses:**

Negative Statuses can generally be resisted:

when one arises — an inherent status directly, or status points — resistance is generally allowed unless stated otherwise. The character may make a resisting check at no action cost; success means no effect. Every status-inflicting ability carries its own resistance notes.

Specially, if the resisting check is undefined, save with **the lower of the status's key resisting Stats + Survival (Self-Preservation)**.

**Enduring Negative Statuses:**

Besides resisting, you may choose to endure Negative Status points already on you.

Only points can be endured, never inherent statuses. Enduring doesn't remove the points — it suppresses them: ignore the endured portion when judging the total.

But note: the moment the endured points exceed twice the higher of the key resisting Stats, the endured points double and take effect at once. Your endurance cap per status is **(the Legendary points on one — or the higher — of its key resisting Stats) × 2**.

**Recovering Negative Status points:**

Every inherent status has some removal method, written on the ability that caused it; if unstated, default to the methods in the "inherent Negative Statuses" rules.

If an inherent status delivers status points, those points recover immediately once the inherent status is lifted — otherwise they cannot fall.

For status points, unless stated otherwise, one 15-minute Short Rest grants one save against one status pool on the character, as under "resisting Negative Statuses".

If points produce a severe status that is an inherent status, the inherent status lifts automatically once the points fall.

**Inherent Negative Status types:**

Each status may arise from more than one cause, and different causes belong to different classes. All inherent Negative Statuses count as severe when judging certain abilities.

**Shapeshift**

Shapeshifting alters a creature's or object's form or structure, or transmutes its constituent matter, obstructing its activity or normal state.

It splits into self-driven and other-driven changes.

Self-driven: werewolf transformation, berserker transformation — generally not counted a Negative Status at all.

Other-driven: the Transmutation spell and its kin; every shapeshifting ability carries the 【form-change】 keyword.

A shapeshifted target shapeshifted again doesn't stack — the later effect overrides the earlier.

Only other-driven shapeshifts can override each other;

a self-driven shapeshift cannot override an other-driven one.

E.g.: transform with self-ability A, then self-ability B — A is forced off, B applies. Then other-driven C lands — B is forced off (and A and B can no longer be used), C applies. Then other-driven D lands — D overrides C, C is forced off, D stands.

Shapeshift immunity blocks all other-driven shapeshifts; your own shapeshifting abilities are untouched by your own immunity.

**Toxins**

Toxins are the effects of special substances entering the body — or supernatural power and magic working in that guise. In the ordinary sense the class spans snake venom, arsenic, cannabis, alcohol, even tobacco; the ill effects of harmless drugs and common stimulants belong here too.

Unless specially stated, undead, incorporeals, constructs, elementals, and metabolism-less monsters are immune to Natural-Category toxins.

Most toxins enter by one of four routes — inhalation, ingestion, contact, injury:

Inhalation: takes effect when breathed; creatures without respiratory systems are immune. Characters in the suffocation state gain a 10 DP Perfect Bonus resisting inhaled toxins.

Ingestion: takes effect only when swallowed; creatures without digestive systems are immune.

Contact: takes effect on touch — and also works through ingestion or wounds. If a toxin-bearing attack misses, the defender takes no contact toxin.

Injury: takes effect only when the toxin meets a wound opened in the skin. If a toxin-bearing attack deals no damage, the character takes no injury toxin.

Some deranged supernatural toxins doubtless have stranger routes; those spell themselves out.

Toxins generally state their own removal methods.

**Possession**

Possession is a spirit, incorporeal, yaoguai, or similar being lodging inside a body or object to work harm.

Unless specially stated, possessor and host count as one: anything attacking the possessor counts as attacking the host — but attacking the host doesn't necessarily touch the possessor.

Some possessors can only inhabit particular hosts, and most produce only one or a few specific effects.

Every possession has its specific effects, described by the granting ability.

Each possession ability generally states its removal. Beyond that, it can be removed by dispelling, dispel magic, dispel special ability, remove curse, and the like. Where a contest is needed, the dispeller's dispel-ability check opposes the possessor's possession-ability check — or the possessor's Will, if it had no ability check. If the dispeller wins, the possession ends.

All possession abilities carry the 【possession】 keyword.

**Illusions and hallucinations**

An illusion is the influence of some false form, sensation, or nonexistent thing; a character may also hallucinate from internal causes or abilities — that too is an illusion.

Some illusions exist objectively whether observed or not, though some show themselves only to particular characters. Either way, an illusion (hallucination) is not a mind-affecting effect.

Illusions' effects vary widely; they generally have durations, and each carries its own notes.

Only two things remove an illusion: seeing through it, and dispelling it.

Means of seeing through are generally stated in the illusion itself; beyond those, illusion immunity and true seeing both pierce illusions. Only a unit that pierces the illusion escapes its influence, and against that unit the illusion's ongoing effects end at once.

To dispel an illusion, first realize it is one; then dispelling, dispel magic, dispel special ability, and the like can remove it. Where a contest is needed, the dispeller's check opposes the illusion-caster's ability check — or the caster's Will, absent an ability check. If the dispeller wins, the illusion's duration ends at once.

All illusion abilities carry the 【illusion (hallucination)】 keyword.

**Disease**

Disease effects span real illnesses and their fantasy and horror versions, effects existing only in imagined worlds, and parasitic infestations — parasites, xenomorph young, ZONAPHA syndrome.

Unless specially stated, undead, incorporeals, constructs, elementals, and metabolism-less monsters are immune to Natural-Category disease.

Some diseases strike only particular kinds of subject, others being immune. Unhappily, unless stated otherwise, every disease affects humans and human-modeled creatures.

Most diseases spread three ways — breath, bodily fluids, contact:

Breath: a healthy person breathing the pathogen-laden air; creatures without respiratory systems are immune. Characters in suffocation gain a 10 DP Perfect Bonus on checks resisting infection.

Fluids: a healthy person's fluids meeting infected fluids.

Contact: a healthy person touching the infected.

Deranged supernatural diseases may spread by stranger routes; those spell themselves out.

Diseases generally state their own removal.

**Curses and magic**

Curses and magic are mighty, ferocious supernatural forces, often issuing from powerful supernatural beings.

Both are Fantasy-Category.

Their effects vary; they generally have durations, and each carries its own notes.

Unless specially stated, magic and curses do not fade naturally — most end the moment their duration runs out. Some special curses and magics carry their own removal (the Frog Prince, say). Beyond those, they can be removed by dispelling, dispel magic, dispel special ability, remove curse, and the like. Where a contest is needed, the dispeller's check opposes the caster's ability check — or the caster's Will, absent one. If the dispeller wins, the curse or magic ends at once.

Note: every effect a spell produces is "magic", and every spell with "Curse" among its Professions is a curse.

**Mind-affecting**

Mind-affecting means abilities working directly on the mind — potent suggestion, mental domination, and the like.

They work only on creatures with complete Mental Stats. So, generally and unless stated otherwise, beings without independent judgment (automata), beings without selves, and single members of hive-minded collectives are immune.

Mind-affecting abilities broadly cover:

Suggestion: planted conclusions, overlooking a target, being steered into an act — the Suggestion arts.

Coercion: the mind pressed hard enough to reverse a conclusion, forcibly bending habit and course — taunts.

Domination: the mind wholly seized, executing commands, its conduct rewritten — enslavement arts.

Note: beyond the above, abilities producing any of the following are mind-affecting by default:

charm points or the infatuated state; abnormal emotions (dejection–world-weariness, mania–frenzy, fear–terror); Mental damage; Morale Bonuses; Morale Penalties.

Unless specially stated, mind effects don't fade naturally — most end when their duration does. Some carry their own removal (the Pope's Phantom Fist). Beyond those, certain special abilities can remove mind effects according to the source that made them —

a mind effect wrought by magic yields to dispel magic.

The remover's check then opposes the mind-effect ability's check — or the user's Will, absent one. If the remover wins, the mind effect ends at once.

**Flat-footed**

Flat-footed means the character cannot react in time.

Only surprise rounds, Initiative order, and special abilities inflict the flat-footed status.

Flat-footed, the character loses Base Defense, Evade bonuses, Insight Defense, and Guard Defense (Shield Defense remains), and cannot use Reflex Actions.

Flat-footedness is relative: among A, B, and C, B may be flat-footed toward A yet not toward C.

Generally each flat-footing ability states its duration. If unstated: self-caused flat-footedness (being surprised, say) lasts 1 round or until your own turn begins; if caused by another — as an attack rider, it lasts one attack; as another special ability, it lasts until that target's turn ends.

**Helpless**

A helpless character has no self-protection at all — meat on the block.

Helpless, the character loses all actions, plus Base, Evade, Insight, and Guard Defense, cannot make Reflex Saves, and cannot trigger even abilities that need no action.

Helpless targets can be given the coup de grâce.

Helplessness never arises alone — it generally rides in on some other Negative Status,

so when the delivering status lifts, helplessness lifts with it.

**Airborne**

Airborne is the state of being knocked skyward by a heavy blow.

An airborne character cannot move, base speed drops to 0, and all Base, Evade, and Guard Defense is lost.

An airborne character landing unprotected takes falling damage as normal.

Generally each launching ability states the duration; if unstated, airborne lasts until the target's round begins.

**Prone**

Prone is lying on the ground.

Rising from prone costs a Movement Action.

A prone character cannot move normally: without spider climb or perfect flight, they can only crawl — see the climbing rules — until they rise.

A prone character gains a 2 DP Environmental Bonus against ranged attacks and on Reflex Saves against area damage.

A prone character's defense takes a 2-point Environmental Penalty against melee attacks.

**Dazed**

Dazed is the blank-minded state.

Dazed, the character loses all actions; anything held slips from their hands.

But the dazed keep their instinct for self-preservation, so they are neither flat-footed nor helpless.

Generally each dazing ability states the duration; if unstated, dazed lasts until the target's round begins.

**Suffocation**

A suffocating character cannot breathe.

Most creatures breathe, plants included, so only creatures without respiratory systems are immune to suffocation.

Every creature breathes with different depth and rhythm, so suffocation bites differently; here only humans and human-modeled creatures are described.

A suffocating creature acts normally for 5 rounds per point of EDR. Past that, each round demands an EDR check at base DC 1, +1 per round thereafter, with the character's Physical-related checks taking an Unnamed Penalty equal to the DC; the first failed check starts respiratory arrest.

Round one of arrest: the character falls unconscious, treated as Blunt damage filling their HP (though the HP itself doesn't change). Round two: near death — the notional Blunt all converts to Lethal (HP still unchanged). Round three: all the Lethal converts to Aggravated (absent special abilities, the character dies). Round four: if somehow alive after round three, the character dies now.

A character may hold their breath and enter suffocation deliberately — a Free Action.

Suffocation can be broken off midway: each round of normal breathing restores suffocation time equal to 1 EDR's worth of duration.

E.g.: EDR 3 gives 15 rounds of normal action; you have suffocated 13, with 2 left. One round of normal breathing later, you can last 7 more.

Suffocation, besides the voluntary kind, may come from environment (drowning) or special abilities. Environmental suffocation ends naturally on leaving the environment; ability-caused suffocation lasts as the ability states.

**Addiction**

Addiction is the state of being unable to leave some stimulus behind. Its cause needn't be harmful in itself (it may even bring passing benefit), but the onset phase brings varied consequences.

Addiction generally runs: exposure to the source — satisfaction phase — onset phase with withdrawal symptoms (repeating some number of times) — cessation.

Source: what causes the addiction — commonly drugs in reality, though psychological illness can hook a person on particular things.

Satisfaction phase: for a while after exposure the character feels satisfied and takes the source's direct effects. Sources are classed variously; resistance to a class adds to withdrawal-resisting checks, and immunity to a class blocks that class's sources — though if something has already addicted the character to a source, withdrawal still bites, and satisfaction is beyond reach.

Onset cycle: after satisfaction ends, one onset cycle later come the withdrawal symptoms. A character failing the withdrawal-resisting check hunts the source heedlessly, wanting satisfaction.

Withdrawal symptoms: going without the source past an onset cycle produces symptoms — generally Negative Status points lasting half an onset cycle. The player rolls an endurance check (usually Fortitude or Will): successes don't reduce the points, but successes above the points master the urge to seek satisfaction. (With several statuses, count the highest pool.) Each onset cycle passed, and each satisfaction phase passed, adds +1 to the next cycle's withdrawal points.

Cessation time: the time, or number of onset cycles, needed to quit a source.

Cessation: after several onset cycles without the source, the character has quit and withdrawal troubles them no more. Using the source before quitting restarts the cessation clock — plus one cycle.

**Banishment**

Banishment is being cast, by some supernatural cause, temporarily into the crevices of space.

Generally, banishment is a space effect.

Banished, the character loses all actions and their Base, Evade, Insight, and Guard Defense. Yet nothing can touch the banished — no buffs, debuffs, damage, impacts, contact; unless stated otherwise, not even space abilities reach them. Durations of effects already on the target run as normal, and the banished can still be observed as usual from the original plane.

Though stripped of actions, a banished target in midair does not fall.

Banishment always comes from some supernatural ability, whose entry states the duration.

**Imprisonment**

Imprisonment is being fixed, by some supernatural cause, to a single point of this space.

Generally, imprisonment is a space effect.

Imprisoned, the character's base speed becomes 0 — no movement — though they don't lose Movement Actions.

Though base speed is 0, an imprisoned target in midair does not fall, being pinned to a point of space. If forcibly moved (knocked aside, say), their contest against the forcing ability gains 20 AS, and moving them counts as moving an object of twenty times the weight.

Imprisonment always comes from a supernatural ability and never fades naturally; the ability states the duration.

**Silence**

Silence is a supernatural muting of one target — or of a region.

Silenced, a character can use no ability involving "speech" or "sound"; every "sound"-related check automatically fails — performance, casting spells with verbal components, Investigation (Listening) toward a silenced target's noise, all of it.

Silence doesn't touch already-active abilities with verbal or sound components — but if the ability needs continued speech or sound to maintain, its duration ends immediately.

Silencing a target puts that target in the silence state; silencing a region puts everyone entering it during the duration into silence, lifted the moment they leave.

Silence always comes from a supernatural ability and never fades naturally; the ability states the duration.

**Sealing**

Sealing uses supernatural power to seal — not destroy — an Energy Pool, item, ability, Stat/skill, or creature.

Sealing kills and destroys nothing.

A sealed Energy Pool cannot be used — cannot be spent — which may disable every ability that spends it, though abilities merely requiring it as a prerequisite are untouched.

A sealed item loses all effects: a weapon loses base and special properties alike; armor and shields lose base and special properties alike.

A sealed ability cannot be used, though abilities built on it as a prerequisite are untouched.

A sealed Stat/skill: such seals generally seal only some value; for the duration, the Stat/skill drops by the sealed points, directly affecting abilities that require them.

A sealed creature suffers whatever the seal effects — imprisonment, banishment, petrification.

Unless specially stated, seals don't fade naturally — most end when their duration does, as the granting ability states; some carry their own removal (the Five-Finger Mountain).

**Frozen solid**

Frozen solid is the state of bodily function crushed by extreme cold.

Frozen solid, the character cannot move, base speed drops to 0, Base, Evade, and Guard Defense are lost, all abilities with "posture" or "movement" components automatically fail, attacking is impossible, and Physical checks automatically fail.

Unless stated otherwise, freeze points landing on a frozen-solid character convert to equal cold Lethal damage, unsaveable.

Frozen solid generally comes from natural environments, or directly or cumulatively from a supernatural ability.

Environmental freezing usually accrues through freeze points; a frozen target brought into normal temperature gradually recovers per the "freeze points" rules.

If a supernatural ability caused it, the ability states the duration or removal; if unstated, follow the "freeze points" rules.

**Deafness**

A deaf character loses half their successes on all Investigation-related checks other than Investigation (Lip-reading), and Investigation (Listening) automatically fails. They keep only half their Initiative, cannot use hearing-based abilities, and the multiple-attack penalty they take from repeated attacks doubles.

Creatures with no auditory system to begin with escape deafness's effects: beyond being unable to use hearing-based abilities and failing Investigation (Listening) automatically, they take none of its other effects.

**Blindness**

A blind character loses sight and visual range, can do nothing that requires vision, loses Base Defense and Evade Defense, halves successes on all Physical checks, loses half their successes on non-Listening Investigation checks, and fails Investigation (Lip-reading). Unable to place their surroundings, all units count as invisible to them under the invisibility rules. Without some means of pinpointing their surroundings, their base speed halves.

Specially, blindness cuts both ways: the blind take no further dazzle penalties, and are immune to gaze effects and other sight-targeted effects.

Beings with eyelids or the like may close their eyes and enter blindness deliberately — a Free Action.

A character with blindsense can place their surroundings within its range: they lose no defense, their skill checks (Lip-reading aside) escape blindness, units inside the range are no longer invisible to them, and their speed is unaffected.

A character with blindsight suffers none of blindness's drawbacks within its range.

**Queasiness**

Nausea is the misery of the digestive system.

Queasy, a character gets only one Movement Action per round and loses Base Defense and Evade Defense.

Note: creatures without digestive systems are immune to nausea points.

Queasiness generally comes from disease, toxins, blows to the abdomen, or some supernatural ability.

If from status points, queasiness lifts when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats. If from a supernatural ability, disease, or toxin, it generally doesn't fade naturally; the ability states duration or removal.

**Petrification**

Petrification turns matter to stone.

Generally, petrification is a shapeshift effect.

Petrified, the character loses all actions, plus Base, Evade, Insight, and Guard Defense, and cannot make Reflex Saves. They gain construct traits, weight ×3, an armor value equal to their EDR, and Hardness equal to the sum of all their Physical AS (minimum 3).

The petrified are not dead, nor quite alive: they count as stone objects, not creatures.

A petrified character broken and then restored after complete reassembly suffers no aftereffects; otherwise restoration leaves the body damaged to the same degree.

Petrification always comes from a supernatural ability and never fades naturally; the ability states the duration.

**Held**

Held is the state of a body rigid and unable to move.

Held, the character cannot move, base speed drops to 0, Base, Evade, and Guard Defense are lost, all abilities with "posture" or "movement" components automatically fail, attacking is impossible, and Physical checks automatically fail.

Holding may come through status points or special abilities. From points, it lifts when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, it generally doesn't fade naturally — the ability states duration or removal.

**Unconsciousness**

Unconsciousness is the loss of awareness, all attention to the world extinguished.

Unconsciousness brings helplessness with it.

The unconscious cannot respond to the world: they have no sensory range and cannot detect anyone's approach. They count as mindless, and so nothing mind-affecting can touch them.

Unconsciousness may come through status points or special abilities. From points, it fades naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, it generally doesn't fade naturally — the ability states duration or removal.

**Infatuation**

Infatuation is a character besotted with a particular target, obedient to their commands.

Infatuation is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

The infatuated character has an object of their infatuation

and gladly obeys them. Short of marching to their death, harming themselves, injuring their own interests, or utterly violating their morals, the infatuated accept every command. On receiving a command of those kinds, the character immediately gets one save; if still in severe status afterward, they carry it out. Nor can they bear the beloved's indifference: so slighted, they will show themselves to the beloved at any cost. And in contests against any attempt by the beloved to go unfound or unrecognized, they gain a Reinforcement Bonus of their DTR in DP.

Infatuation may come through status points or special abilities. From points, the character wakes naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, it generally doesn't fade naturally — the ability states duration or removal.

**Hunger and thirst**

Hunger and thirst is the Negative Status of lacking food and water.

Twelve hours without adequate water and food brings it on.

Each cycle it persists — a cycle being 24 hours — the character makes an EDR check at DC equal to their own EDR, taking fatigue points equal to the margin of failure.

If the character can go longer without food or drink, the cycle becomes that span; with several such abilities, take the highest.

E.g.: 1 Legendary EDR lets a character go a week without food or drink — the cycle becomes a week.

On obtaining adequate food and water, the status lifts at once,

and the fatigue points recover per the status-point recovery rules.

**Weariness**

Weariness is the Negative Status of continuous action without sleep.

Each cycle it persists — a cycle being 24 hours — the character makes an EDR check at DC equal to their own EDR, taking fatigue points equal to the margin of failure.

Note that "sleep" here means 8 hours of it. Sleeping less accumulates the shortfall until it passes 8 hours, then forces a weariness EDR check; the character may nap before reaching 8 hours to shrink the shortfall.

If the character can go long without sleep, add that span to the cycle; with several abilities, take the highest.

E.g.: 1 Legendary EDR lets a character skip sleep for a week — the cycle becomes a week.

On adequate sleep, the status lifts at once,

and fatigue points recover per the status-point recovery rules.

**Exhaustion**

Fatigue comes of overworked bodies, lost sleep, hunger, and their kin.

Exhausted, a character loses their Movement Action, base speed halves, and charging and all-out attacks are impossible.

Exhaustion may come through status points or special abilities. From points, it fades naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, the ability states duration or removal.

**World-weariness**

The world-weary character doubts their own existence and the world's meaning, unable to summon any drive.

World-weariness is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

The world-weary will do nothing to better or worsen anything. Short of unambiguous, irresistible orders, they take no active action.

The world-weary no longer take dejection points' mild-status effects; instead they lose DP equal to their dejection points on all checks, and lose Evade Defense and Guard Defense.

World-weariness comes only from dejection points, lifting at once when the dejection points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats.

**Frenzy**

The frenzied character has fallen into uncontrollable restlessness and impulse.

Frenzy is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

The frenzied cannot settle: no action that requires staying put and calming down is possible.

Resting naturally, they gain nothing beyond the HP recovery (no Willpower recovery, seated cultivation, meditation, Energy Pool recovery, and so on). Patience gone, they cannot perform extended actions with periods of a minute or longer.

Frenzy comes only from mania points, lifting at once when the mania points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats.

**Terror**

The terror-stricken character shuns the unit that terrifies them to the utmost.

Terror is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

The terror-stricken flee the object of their fear with everything they have, until they can no longer sense it, using their most effective means of movement.

Terror may come through status points or special abilities. From points, the character recovers naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, the ability states duration or removal.

**Sleep**

Sleep is the state of slumber or meditation, attention to the world all but gone.

Sleep brings helplessness with it.

Natural sleep ends naturally; humans need 8 hours per 24.

Unless stated otherwise, others can shake a sleeper awake with a Standard Action.

A sleeper's sensory range is computed as though PCT were 1 lower.

Anyone approaching always counts as deliberately hiding; the sleeper can only contest with PCT + Investigation (Listening), losing 1 success.

A sleeper wakes automatically upon taking any damage.

Sleep may come naturally, through status points, or through special abilities.

From points, the sleeper wakes naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, it generally doesn't fade naturally — the ability states duration or removal (Sleeping Beauty).

From other special abilities, judge by the ability's notes; if none, default to natural sleep.

**Limb disability**

A limb-disabled individual cannot use the disabled limb at all.

Limb disability touches only the four limbs, nothing else — you cannot limb-disable a target's head into a beheading.

One arm disabled: only the remaining hand can hold items or weapons; spells with "posture"/"movement" components demand Focusing checks; whatever the disabled arm held drops.

Both arms disabled: nothing can be held, posture/movement-component spells automatically fail, and every action needing hands automatically fails (shooting, say).

One leg disabled: land speed halves.

Both legs disabled: land speed is lost, and every action needing legs automatically fails (flying kicks).

Limb disability may come through status points or special abilities. From points, it fades naturally when the points fall below the higher of the key resisting Stats; from a supernatural ability, the ability states duration or removal.

Permanent limb disability, barring a reattachment ability, is cured only through the Lord God Space or certain supernatural abilities.

**Negative Status point types:**

**Freeze points**

**Key resisting Stats: STR, AGI**

Freezing is bodily function impaired by deep cold. Unless stated otherwise, freeze points are saved with Fortitude.

Mild: each freeze point costs the creature 1 DP on Physical-related checks and 1 meter of base speed, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter the frozen-solid state.

Ruinous consequence: none.

At suitable temperatures, freeze points recover at 1 per round.

Freeze and burn counteract each other: taking burn points while frozen grants no burn points — instead subtract freeze points equal to the burn points, up to the freeze total; only the excess arrives as burn points.

**Tinnitus points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, PCT**

Tinnitus impairs hearing.

Mild: the ringing creature takes penalties on listening.

Severe: enter deafness.

Ruinous consequence: hearing is lost forever — permanent deafness.

**Dazzle points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, PCT**

Dazzle blurs and blinds.

Mild: the dazzled take penalties on visual scouting, on gathering information by reading and the like, and on attacks; for precise extended work — crafting, brewing magical draughts — checks take half the dazzle as a penalty.

Severe: enter blindness.

Ruinous consequence: sight is lost forever — permanent blindness. Eyes burst, optic nerves seared, the brain's visual centers wrecked — or the very soul rewritten past all understanding of visual information.

**Nausea points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, DTR**

Nausea is the misery of the digestive system. Unless stated otherwise, nausea points save with Fortitude or DTR + Survival (Self-Preservation).

Mild: each point penalizes the creature's checks to attack and to use skills, Techniques, and spells.

Severe: enter queasiness.

Ruinous consequence: none.

Generally, creatures without digestive systems are immune to nausea points.

**Crystallization points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

Crystallization is the process of matter turning to stone. Unless stated otherwise, it saves with Will.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on Physical-related checks and 1 meter of base speed, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter petrification.

Ruinous consequence: permanent petrification.

**Mental-bind points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

Mental binding hampers movement by pressing on the mind; it is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on Mental-related checks and 1 meter of base speed, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter the held state.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Entanglement points**

**Key resisting Stats: STR, AGI**

Hindrance by outside force is entanglement — ropes coiled tight, glue and slime, invisible fields of force.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on attack checks and Athletics-related checks and 1 meter of base speed, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter the held state.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Paralysis points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, DTR**

Paralysis works on the body itself, robbing it of the power to act.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on attack checks, Athletics-related checks, and base speed, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter the held state.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Agony points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, DTR**

Great pain deranges sound judgment.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on Social-related checks, Mental-related checks, and Will checks, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter unconsciousness.

Ruinous consequence: pain wrecks flesh and mind alike. Agony points beyond twice the key resisting Stats become Stats Damage, spread evenly over EDR, DTR, and CMT in that priority — EDR −1, DTR −1, CMT −1, then EDR −1 again, cycling until the excess is spent.

**Stun points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, DTR**

Stunning sets the head spinning.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on attack checks, Athletics-related checks, and PCT-related checks, and 1 meter of base speed.

Severe: enter unconsciousness.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Charm points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, DMR**

Charm besots a character with a particular target; it is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on Social checks resisting the beloved's initiatives, and 1 DP on Will Saves against the beloved's abilities.

Severe: enter infatuation.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Fatigue points**

**Key resisting Stats: EDR, STR**

Fatigue comes of an overworked body or similar effects.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on STR- and AGI-related checks and 1 meter of base speed.

Severe: enter exhaustion.

Ruinous consequence: excess weariness damages the body. Fatigue points beyond twice the key resisting Stats become Stats Damage, spread over the Physical Stats in the priority STR, AGI, EDR — STR −1, AGI −1, EDR −1, then STR −1 again, cycling until the excess is spent.

**Abnormal emotions**

Emotion effects are what a character's own emotional swings do to them — arising spontaneously, triggered by sights or sounds, induced by hypnosis or self-hypnosis, drugs, even artful conversation.

Abnormal emotions are mind-affecting Negative Statuses.

**Abnormal emotion — dejection points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

The dejected doubt their own existence and the world's meaning, unable to summon drive.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on attack checks, all skill-related checks, and all extended-action checks.

Severe: enter world-weariness.

Mania and dejection counteract each other: taking mania points while dejected grants no mania points — instead subtract dejection points equal to them, up to the dejection total; the excess arrives as mania points.

**Abnormal emotion — mania points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

The manic fall into uncontrollable restlessness and impulse.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on Initiative checks, Social-related checks, and all extended-action checks, and imposes a 1-point penalty in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order.

Severe: enter frenzy.

Ruinous consequence: gain a mental disorder — see the "Mental Disorders" rules.

Mania and dejection counteract each other: taking dejection points while manic grants no dejection points — instead subtract mania points equal to them, up to the mania total; the excess arrives as dejection points.

**Abnormal emotion — fear points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

The fearful shun the unit that frightens them.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on Social-related checks against the feared target and on checks using Mental Stats. While the feared target shares the Scene and can be sensed, each fear point imposes a 1-point penalty — in Base–Evade–Guard Defense order — against the attacks of every target the character does *not* fear.

Severe: enter terror.

Ruinous consequence: gain a mental disorder — see the "Mental Disorders" rules.

**Drowsiness points**

**Key resisting Stats: DTR, CMT**

Drowsiness sets the eyelids drooping; it is a mind-affecting Negative Status.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 DP on attack checks, Athletics-related checks, and PCT-related checks, and 1 meter of base speed.

Severe: the character falls into sleep.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Limb-impairment points**

**Key resisting Stats: STR, AGI, EDR**

Limb impairment is a limb rendered hard to use by some adverse working — a severed tendon, poison, current scrambling the nerves.

Limb impairment has 3 key resisting Stats, so status grades compare against the highest of three rather than two.

Generally, impairment points afflict the matching limb.

Mild: each point costs 1 DP on STR-related, AGI-related, and Crafts-related checks led by that limb. If the impaired individual moves with the limb, land speed drops 1 meter per point.

The ST may rule a creature's limb impairment or disability harmless to movement — a centipede short one leg loses no speed. Disputes over how many limbs a creature moves on (octopi) fall to the ST.

Severe: enter limb disability.

Ruinous consequence: the limb, or all use of it, is lost forever — permanent limb disability.

**Stall points**

**Key resisting Stats: STR, AGI**

Stalling is a creature's base speed penalized by circumstance.

Mild: each point costs the creature 1 meter of base speed — affecting base speed and everything derived from it: drives on base speed, land speed on base speed, flight on base speed, and so on.

Severe: the character cannot move, base speed dropping to 0; if flying, they begin to fall, airborne until they land.

Ruinous consequence: none.

**Burn points**

**Key resisting Stat: AGI**

Burning is the state of combustibles on a character — or the character — set alight.

Mild: at the end of each of their actions, the character takes fire Lethal damage equal to their burn points, until the flames go out.

Severe: none.

Ruinous consequence: none.

A character may spend a Standard Action trying to beat out the flames — a Reflex check, each success removing 1 burn point. Dropping and rolling grants 2 DP; leaping into water or using an extinguisher kills the fire instantly.

Ordinarily, natural-flame burning fades at 1 point per round; malicious burning like napalm does not fade on its own; and magical flame cannot be physically extinguished before its duration ends.

Burn and freeze counteract each other: taking freeze points while burning grants no freeze points — instead subtract burn points equal to them, up to the burn total; the excess arrives as freeze points.

**Bleeding points**

**Key resisting Stat: EDR**

Bleeding is life draining without pause from a wounded body.

Mild: at the end of each of their actions, the character takes Lethal physical damage equal to their bleeding points, until healed.

Severe: none.

Ruinous consequence: none.

At the start of each round, the character may make an EDR check as a Swift Action, each success removing 1 point of bleeding.

A character may also spend a Standard Action on a touch-attack check against another target; on success, roll AGI + Medicine (First Aid), each success removing 1 of the target's bleeding points.
