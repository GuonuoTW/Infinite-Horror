# Vehicle Rules

**Vehicle rules**

Vehicles come in two kinds: mechanical vehicles and animal vehicles.

Fighting from a mechanical vehicle is collectively called 【driving combat】; fighting from an animal vehicle is collectively called 【mounted combat】.

Vehicle Categories: vehicles fall into Natural, Tech, Fantasy, and Eldritch Categories.

Natural generally means warhorses, or manufactured creatures that exist in the real world.

Tech can be manufactured but is vulnerable to a film's tech-nullification — generally mechanical vehicles built by technological means. The moment a vehicle uses anything supernatural, it stops being a Tech vehicle: a Gundam is Tech, but a Z Gundam driving funnels through a psychic frame is not.

Fantasy covers vehicles from Fantasy-Category worlds — mechanical (Ryujinmaru) or biological (a nightmare steed).

Eldritch means every vehicle that is neither Fantasy nor Tech — like that psychic-framed Z Gundam.

===

**Basic traits of mechanical vehicles:**

【Object】: unless stated otherwise, mechanical vehicles count as objects rather than creatures, so damage resolves as for objects — immune to Mental damage and Poison damage, for instance.

Because a vehicle runs on motive power and is not alive, it is immune to mind-affecting effects, disease and toxins, mania points and the manic state, dejection points and the world-weary state, charm points and the infatuated state, fear points and the terror-stricken state, sleep, tinnitus points and deafness, dazzle points and blindness, nausea points and queasiness, mental-bind points, agony points, fatigue points, stun points, and bleeding points. Other special states or Negative Status points are the ST's call.

【Driver】: any mind-affecting effect, any ability resisted by Will Saves, and all of — mania, dejection, charm, fear, sleep, tinnitus, dazzle, nausea, mental-bind, agony, fatigue, stun, and bleeding points and their states, plus Mental damage and Poison damage — may bypass the 【Sealed】 property of the "sealed vehicle" trait and strike the driver directly, unless the driver has a matching counter (eyes shut against dazzle points, say).

Instant death, causality effects, time effects, space effects, banishment, imprisonment and all similar effects strike vehicle and driver together; for such joint effects, if the driver is immune, the vehicle is immune with them.

E.g. 1: an instant-death effect may target the driver directly, even sealed inside a fighter plane.

E.g. 2: Mental damage aimed at the vehicle lands on the driver. Note: if it is an area effect, the sealed vehicle's 【Sealed】 property applies as normal.

E.g. 3: Poison damage aimed at the vehicle lands on the driver — same area-effect caveat.

E.g. 4: teleporting the vehicle carries the driver along; teleporting the driver leaves the vehicle behind, unless stated otherwise.

E.g. 5: time effects hit vehicle and driver together; a driver's immunity covers the vehicle.

E.g. 6: banishment, imprisonment, and the like hit both together; a driver's immunity covers the vehicle.

E.g. 7: fear points cast at the vehicle land on the driver. Note: if the points ride on damage — "inflict fear points equal to this damage" — and the damage lands on the vehicle, the follow-on points cannot reach the driver.

E.g. 8: gas attacks affect the driver as normal; area-damage versions face the sealed vehicle as usual, non-area versions don't. 【Tri-Protection】 or the like grants immunity to inhaled gases.

E.g. 9: effects that deal damage and then, keyed to that damage, inflict instant death, causality, time, space, banishment, imprisonment, or the like — since the damage lands on the vehicle, the follow-on effects cannot reach the driver.

E.g. 10: a driver with out-of-turn movement may spend it driving the vehicle instead; but none of the driver's movement-related abilities — swim speed, climb speed, added base speed, flight, travel pace — transfer to the vehicle, nor the reverse.

【Sealed vehicle】: unless stated otherwise, every vehicle except light vehicles is sealed, carrying the 【Sealed】 property at a value equal to its armor value. Creatures outside have neither line of sight nor line of effect to those inside, and vice versa — so the great majority of effects, helpful or not, cannot reach the driver through the hull, and the driver likewise cannot directly affect outside targets.

【Independent】: unless stated otherwise, the driver's abilities and the vehicle's are essentially unrelated. Unless the ability or vehicle says so, a driver who can double-slash with two blades neither makes the vehicle able to, nor means it can.

Physical-skill checks made through a vehicle always roll the Drives skill instead, whatever skill they normally use — though Professions on the original skill apply as normal.

Active abilities the driver uses through Mental and Social skills (mind checks excluded), unless stated otherwise, cannot reach the outside world — only units in the cockpit, and the vehicle itself.

Attacks with the vehicle's weapons generally don't count as the character's own attack checks (so effects boosting the character's attack checks do nothing — unless they explicitly work on vehicles), nor as firearm shooting (so the "Gunslinger" Title, "Gun Kata", and the like do nothing), even though the check may use the Firearms skill as its key skill for DP and AS.

Nevertheless, abilities that simply "grant bonuses or penalties to the relevant skill or Stat checks" still apply.

When the driven vehicle is affected, the driver may trigger "when you are affected"-type abilities. The driver's immunities do not extend to the vehicle unless stated otherwise — but when the effect reaches the driver, they apply as normal.

【Shared】: if you hold special abilities premised on refraining from some act — not attacking, casting, or moving, say — then while driving a vehicle, attacks, casts, and moves you perform through it immediately count against those abilities. As a mere passenger, the driver's acts don't concern you — but the moment you yourself move, attack, or cast inside or on the vehicle, they count as usual.

【Unit】: a vehicle counts as a unit as normal — friendly or enemy — and enjoys effects that work on units and targets, but a mechanical one gains nothing reserved for creatures: HP recovery, Willpower recovery, gaining Born Defense, and the like.

While driverless, attackers may strike the vehicle as though delivering a coup de grâce.

【Weapons】: unless stated otherwise, a combat robot's hands and legs default to 0L natural weapons, and a vehicle's dedicated weapons ignore the vehicle's own Size restrictions.

【Bonuses】: a vehicle can gain any bonus as normal except Born Defense — every other bonus type may come through its own cultivation, buff magic, or other effects.

E.g.: an effect reading "Target: one target. Effect: its firearm attack checks gain a 6 DP Machinery Bonus" can sit on the driver or on the vehicle. On the driver, it survives changing vehicles; on the vehicle, it survives changing drivers — and a driver leaving the vehicle loses it. If both carry it, take the higher.

【Equipment】: unless stated otherwise, combat robots have two hand equipment slots; beyond that, unless stated otherwise, no vehicle has any equipment slot.

【Power supply】: unless stated otherwise, a D-Tier vehicle runs one day on a standard energy cell or fuel of equal points; C-Tier needs a large energy cell (or equivalent fuel) per day; B-Tier runs two days on one fusion cell; A-Tier one day per fusion cell; S-Tier counts as having a limitless power source, at no cost.

【Fixed speed】: unless the ability says otherwise, moving by vehicle doesn't count as the character's own movement and so escapes the matching abilities.

【Damage cap】: vehicle-weapon damage caps change thus:

Fighting styles capped by STR now use the vehicle's Output instead.

Fighting styles capped by AGI now use the vehicle's Handling instead.

Special: an ability substituting AGI or STR for the other as the damage cap in an attack works as normal; abilities raising your own damage cap do nothing for the vehicle.

Fighting styles not capped by a Stat are unchanged.

【Fuel and ammunition】: if a vehicle involves fuel or weapon ammunition bought with points and/or Tiers, purchase includes a full tank and a full ammunition load; anything beyond that is bought separately.

===

**Basic statistics of mechanical vehicles:**

【Size】: vehicle Size generally follows the source material. Vehicles, built large by design, get certain allowances in Size effects: for every 10 points of Size above 10 (at 20, 30, 40, and so on), −1 Base Defense and +1 armor value. If a vehicle's Size exceeds 10, attacks from its weapons at targets below Size 10 take −1 DP per point below 10 — this Size-gap penalty applies only once, so even a Size-50 Zaku firing an anti-MS bazooka at a Size-5 human takes only −5.

Special: the vehicle's defense adjustment may reduce its Evade Defense to 0, the remainder then eating Base Defense; if Base Defense also hits 0, no further defenses are affected.

【Structure (HP)】: Structure marks the greatest damage a vehicle can bear before destruction.

Unless stated otherwise, vehicles cannot gain abilities that affect HP — raising or lowering the cap — nor benefit from Temporary HP.

A vehicle also has just one HP track: it is immune to Blunt damage, takes Lethal in place of Aggravated at equal value, and takes unavoidable Lethal in place of unavoidable Blunt at equal value.

【Armor value】: the armor value is the vehicle's Born Defense equivalent and yields to Breach. Vehicles cannot gain Born Defense, and abilities affecting Born Defense cannot touch vehicles.

【Hardness】: Hardness is the vehicle's power to shrug off damage — each point negates 1 physical damage; for energy damage see the "damage types" chapter. Breach that pierces the armor value carries any remainder through to Hardness.

【Speed】: the vehicle's maximum movement speed.

【Acceleration】: the speed a vehicle can add per turn — generally always 1/5 of maximum speed, scaling in proportion when maximum speed rises.

【Threshold】: a vehicle's sheer bulk generally lets it ignore trifling wounds. A vehicle with a damage threshold is immune to all damage below it; damage at or above the threshold lands as normal. Damage below the threshold never reduces Structure.

【Mobility】: even ground vehicles can't wheel about like people, so ground vehicles carry Mobility like fliers. Mobility shapes how the vehicle moves and alters its Base Defense. Standard vehicle Mobility is average.

【Output】: the vehicle's Output is the greatest STR it can supply, gaining Legendary STR benefits as normal.

【Handling】: the vehicle's Handling is the greatest AGI it can supply, gaining Legendary AGI benefits as normal.

【Base Defense】: a vehicle's Base Defense is the lower of the driver's AGI and PCT, capped by the vehicle's Handling, gaining the defense AS from Legendary AGI and Legendary PCT as normal — plus other Base Defense boosts, which may exceed the Handling cap.

【The three Saves】:

Fortitude: vehicles ignore all checks requiring Fortitude Saves; where the effect works on non-creatures too (the ability must say so — or Negative Statuses that do affect vehicles, like freeze points or burn points), use Hardness directly as the Fortitude result.

Reflex: a vehicle's Reflex is (the lower of the vehicle's Handling and the driver's INT) + Drives (matching vehicle type) skill level, plus a Perfect Bonus of (the matching Legendary Stat × 3) DP depending on whether Handling or INT was used — replacing the Perfect Bonus AGI normally gives Reflex Saves.

Will: vehicles ignore all checks requiring Will Saves. Aimed at the driver, the driver saves with their own Will.

Special: buying a mechanical vehicle normally in the Lord God Space includes a matching transformation capsule auto-bound to the vehicle. Vehicles self-built or otherwise obtained get no capsule.

For vehicles and combat robots without listed Output or Handling, use:

D-Tier: Output 6, Handling 6

C-Tier: Output 11, Handling 11

B-Tier: Output 16, Handling 16

A-Tier: Output 21, Handling 21

S-Tier: Output 31, Handling 31

===

Mechanical vehicles divide into light vehicles, heavy vehicles, and combat robots:

Light vehicles

Drivable by one person, carrying at most 1–2 beings of the driver's species or smaller besides.

Generally, light vehicles lack the 【Sealed vehicle】 trait: driver and passengers can be attacked directly, using their own defenses.

You may move by other means with the vehicle in tow — it then counts as carried goods, requiring a suitable carrying method and enough STR.

Structurally, light vehicles generally cannot grapple, overrun, trip, slam, feint, assist, guard, take full defense, or make all-out attacks — but lacking the sealed trait, driver and passengers can do all of these as normal.

Specially, a vehicle cannot slam like a character — only the ram of the vehicle-attack rules — while called shots and coups de grâce work as usual.

Light vehicles are immune to the prone state.

Heavy vehicles

Often need several crew to use in full, and can carry many passengers or heavy cargo.

They protect the crew completely.

You cannot move the vehicle along by your own movement while inside it.

Structurally, heavy vehicles generally cannot grapple, overrun, trip, slam, feint, assist, guard, take full defense, or make all-out attacks.

Specially, the vehicle cannot slam like a character — only the vehicle-attack ram — while called shots and coups de grâce work as usual.

Heavy vehicles are immune to the prone state.

Combat robots

Generally single-pilot, with no room for more.

They protect the pilot completely.

The combat robot is the compromise between the two: it carries fully standard statistics, and only combat robots may use the humanoid combat options — all-out attacks, charge attacks, grapples, trips, feints, assists, throwing a target, and so on.

Combat robots may slam as normal (mind the changed Stat checks) and also ram under the vehicle-attack rules, with called shots and coups de grâce as usual.

===

Common actions when driving mechanical vehicles:

===

Driver's acts:

Boarding: unless stated otherwise, boarding a vehicle of Size 10 or below is a Movement Action; above Size 10, each 20 points over adds another Movement Action.

Bailing out: the driver may leap out with a Movement Action, leaving the vehicle to run on. You take falling damage as though dropped from a height equal to the vehicle's current speed, reducible by Reflex as normal — see the falling rules.

Consoles:

Every vehicle has one core console; the driver must occupy it to drive.

Once a console is used, it cannot be used again until that driver's next turn begins. Each console holds only one driver at a time.

Unless stated otherwise, the console controls both the vehicle and its weapons.

Beings off the console may sit in passenger seats or cling to the hull, acting as normal.

===

**Vehicle movement:**

Moving: once per turn, as a Movement Action, you steer the vehicle a distance equal to its current speed.

Before each Movement-Action move, the driver declares "accelerate", "brake", or "hold speed"; the vehicle then behaves accordingly.

Accelerate: current speed rises by one Acceleration, up to the vehicle's 【Speed】.

Brake: current speed falls by one Acceleration, to as low as 0.

Hold speed: the vehicle keeps its previous speed unchanged.

If you spend no Movement Action on the vehicle this turn, it automatically travels its current speed along your last heading, unable to turn — which may end in a crash.

Emergency braking: as a Reflex or Swift Action, roll (lower of AGI/INT) + Drives (matching vehicle Profession); every 2 successes drop the vehicle's speed one Acceleration step.

===

**Vehicle attack actions:**

Attacking with the vehicle's weapons: operating each battery of weapons is a Standard Action. The check's Stat becomes (the lower of the vehicle's Output or Handling and the driver's INT); the skill becomes the driver's Drives.

Damage caps follow the vehicle's Output and Handling.

All else follows the ordinary battle rules.

Running over / ramming: when a moving vehicle meets an obstacle, an obstacle above half the vehicle's Size triggers a ram; otherwise a run-over. Either way, each obstacle is affected once per turn at most.

When this happens, if the driver drove normally this round (spent the Movement Action driving), they may roll INT + Drives (matching vehicle Profession) and record the successes.

Vehicle and obstacle each take physical Lethal damage of (current speed / 10) — capped as falling/falling-object damage — ignoring the vehicle's armor value, though its Hardness applies as normal; the crew take physical Lethal damage of half what the vehicle takes.

A living obstacle may reduce the damage with a Reflex Save, each success saving 1 damage. Damage the target avoids through its save or the driver's evasive successes is likewise subtracted from the vehicle's — and correspondingly the driver's — damage.

The driver may spend their recorded successes: aimed at ramming the target, the target's Reflex Save loses DP equal to those successes; aimed at avoiding harm, each success reduces the target's damage by 1.

On a ram: the target is knocked back 1 meter per point of damage taken.

On a run-over: if the vehicle ends atop the crushed obstacle, the target counts as grappled by the vehicle, escape difficulty set by the vehicle's bulk.

If the ram/run-over succeeds but the target takes no damage, the vehicle's speed immediately drops to 0.

Head-on collisions: if the rammed target is a moving vehicle, sum both speeds to compute the impact damage (same heading: subtract, faster minus slower); then contest (driver's INT or vehicle's Output) + Drives (matching Profession) both ways — the loser is knocked back meters equal to the margin.

After a head-on, both vehicles' current speeds drop to 0.

===

**Vehicle defensive actions:**

Emergency evasion: as a Movement Action, the vehicle gains an Evade bonus on defense and Reflex Saves equal to your Drives skill level. Combat robots may guard, but cannot guard and evade at once.

Full defense: as in the battle rules' "full defense".

===

Rollovers:

【Rollover】: moving vehicles risk rolling — tipping onto a side or flipping outright from causes within or without. While a vehicle is 【rolled over】, leaving it takes a Full-turn Action, as does boarding it.

【Righting】: a rolled-over vehicle cannot drive — no movement — until 【righted】, which also takes a Full-turn Action.

Under the encumbrance rules, several units may heave together, summing everyone's STR; if the total could push or drag the vehicle, it can be righted.

In combat, when A rights the vehicle, others may join: A spends their Full-turn Action, and each helper loses a Full-turn Action on their own next turn.

Note: righting a rolled-over vehicle normally requires units adjacent outside it; but combat robots and other humanoid-shaped vehicles, when rolled over, are simply prone.

Vehicles risk rolling when:

1. Moving in combat above 3/4 of their Speed: the driver rolls INT + Drives at DC 6 or rolls over. The DC drops 1 per 5 points of vehicle Size above 10, to a minimum of 1 (so every vehicle keeps some chance of rolling).

Note: on special terrain (sheet ice, downhill grades…), the ST may raise the DC (ice +2; downhill +1 or +2), set by the story's road conditions.

2. Ramming or being rammed: if the driver's Reflex Save against the impact scores less than half the impact damage, the vehicle rolls; and if a vehicle collides (attacking or attacked) with a unit or vehicle over 3 times its own Size and actually takes damage from it, it rolls.

4. Others may actively flip a vehicle within reach as a Standard Action: their STR must suffice to push or drag it, then they roll a STR check at DC equal to the vehicle's Size — success flips it into rollover. The driver may make a Reflex Save; every 3 successes raise the DC by 1.

5. Flying vehicles never roll directly — but meeting the conditions above, they plummet per the falling rules, taking falling damage as normal. If they fail to resume flight with a move before hitting ground, they land in rollover like any other vehicle.

===

Vehicle explosions and paralysis

When a vehicle's Structure hits 0, the driver casts an Opportunity Roll: success leaves the vehicle paralyzed; failure sets it to explode in 1d10 rounds.

A paralyzed vehicle loses all actions and effects, and its Base, Evade, Insight, and Guard Defense — but it can be recovered, and won't explode. Each time it takes damage while paralyzed, cast another Opportunity Roll; a failure again means explosion in 1d10 rounds.

When a vehicle explodes, everything around it — occupants included — takes area physical damage equal to the vehicle's Structure, in a radius of the vehicle's Size in meters; targets in the area may Reflex-Save, each success reducing the damage by 1. Units still inside cannot save.

An exploded vehicle cannot be recovered — total loss, beyond repair.

Paralyzed or exploded, the vehicle is effectively dead for rulings: barring a return to the Lord God Space or special notes, no Structure recovery or repair-like measure restores a paralyzed vehicle to action.

===

**Fighting vehicles:**

Attacking a mechanical vehicle's specific spots:

Striking the fuel tank (reactor, etc.) from outside: first make a periodic INT + Science check on Whole-round-Action periods; per the vehicle blueprint's Tier, your Stats, Science skill, and Profession count must meet the prerequisites for building the vehicle.

DC is the vehicle's (Tier × points price) / 500, rounded up; No-Tier items succeed automatically. Success reveals the enemy's weak point.

You may then attack normally at a 6-point called-shot penalty; the attack's damage doubles after the damage cap, and if it zeroes the vehicle's HP, the vehicle explodes at once.

If you have already read the vehicle's blueprint, you know the weak point with no check.

Striking the driver from outside:

Against a 【Sealed vehicle】: first make a periodic INT + Science check on Whole-round-Action periods; per the blueprint's Tier, your Stats, Science skill, and Profession count must meet the build prerequisites.

DC is (Tier × points price) / 500, rounded up; No-Tier items succeed automatically. Success reveals the driver's position.

To attack the driver: a ranged weapon must carry 【Penetrating】; melee requires the vehicle be no more than 2 size classes above you (a medium creature can reach into at most a gargantuan vehicle). The attack takes a 6-point called-shot penalty, and the driver adds the vehicle's armor value as extra armor bonus on this defense (stacking with their own armor) plus the vehicle's Hardness as extra damage reduction.

With the blueprint read — or X-ray-style sight — no check is needed to know the spot.

Fighting inside a vehicle:

Some large crewed vehicles can be breached from outside and boarded for interior fighting.

Only weapons dealing 【slashing】 or 【bludgeoning】 damage types can breach the outer armor — or area-damage attacks.

Breaching needs the vehicle within reach and a normal attack that deals no damage, accumulating instead: each time the total passes the vehicle's Hardness, it opens a Size-1 gap, up to your weapon's Size (or your own Size, if brawling).

Each Size-1 gap lowers the vehicle's Structure cap by 5 — reduced by 1 (minimum 1) per 10 points of vehicle Size above 10.

When a gapped vehicle sits at full Structure and keeps recovering, every 2 extra Structure recovered mends 1 Size of gap; recovering the Structure cap alone recovers no Structure.

When the gap grows big enough to admit a character, they may enter.

Inside, wreck the vehicle by smashing its innards — as outside, but ignoring the vehicle's Base Defense, and the vehicle itself can no longer attack that unit in any meaningful way.

Hacking vehicles: vehicles with computer functions can usually be hacked — see device intrusion under the other rules. You need a suitable means of intrusion (many enhancements provide auto-adapting wireless interfaces at suitable ranges, or the vehicle may be network-linked); a successfully hacked vehicle is paralyzed.

===

**Mounted combat rules:**

These rules apply only to animal vehicles.

===

Your mount acts on your Initiative. Mount and rider each have their own Swift, Movement, and Standard Actions.

Unless stated otherwise, you must spend a Swift Action each turn steering your mount, or it cannot act this round.

On your turn you may use the mount's actions and your own together; since you ride it, its movement carries you along, costing only the mount's own matching action.

Note: a creature must carry the "mount" designation to be ridden as one; otherwise resolve as clinging or grappling, not riding.

Being mounted constrains some of your actions: unless stated otherwise, you cannot use movement arts and the like.

===

Common mounted-combat actions:

Actions without special mounted rules follow the basic combat actions.

===

Mounting and dismounting:

Each is a Movement Action.

With an AGI + Animal Ken (Riding) check at DC 5, you may mount or dismount as a Swift Action instead.

Mounted charge:

A mounted charge costs you and your mount a Whole-round Action each.

If your mount charges, its defense takes the charge penalty, and its melee attacks afterward gain the Reinforcement Bonus, as in "charging".

If you make a melee attack after the mount's charge, your attack gains the same DP Reinforcement Bonus — without reducing your own defense.

Picking up items:

A Movement Action.

You may snatch items mid-gallop —

but grabbing things off the ground from high in the saddle is hard: an AGI + Athletics (Balance) check at DC equal to the mount's Size. Success picks it up; failure with successes below half the DC unhorses the rider.

Unhorsed:

An unhorsed rider takes falling damage from (mount's height — generally computed from its build and Size, or flying altitude) + 3 meters, Reflex-savable per the falling rules.

Either way, taking even 1 damage from it leaves you prone.

If you lose consciousness in the saddle, you have a 50% chance of staying on; otherwise you fall as normal.

Riderless, your mount immediately quits the battle unless it has a fighting will of its own.

Mount downed:

If your mount goes down, "unhorsed" follows.

Mounted casting:

Casting while mounted requires Focusing checks.

Taking special attacks:

While mounted, opponents must pick you or your mount as the target of grapples, trips, and slams, contesting against whichever was chosen.

While mounted, when you or your mount is grappled, tripped, or slammed (where possible), Size counts as rider's Size + mount's Size.

If your mount is successfully tripped, slammed, or grappled, you are "unhorsed".

If you are grappled and forcibly moved, you leave the mounted state.

If you are successfully tripped or slammed, you are "unhorsed".
