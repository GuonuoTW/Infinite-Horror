# Detection and Locating

**Core rules:**

1. Every means of detection and anti-detection carries its own "detection Tier" and "anti-detection Tier", set by its Tier.

2. Detection and anti-detection means come in four Categories: Natural, Tech, Fantasy, Eldritch. When the two sides' Categories differ, the detecting side counts as one Tier higher.

3. A detection means is one that inherently provides a perceptive capability allowing direct observation. If the means cannot itself produce a rough fix or a pinpoint, this rule does not make it able to locate.

**Core concepts:**

Locating a target: a player knowing a target's position is called locating the target. Locating splits into pinpointing and rough fixes.

Pinpoint: knowing the target's position exactly. Generally, for humans, seeing the target clearly, definitely, and truly means you can locate it. A character can attack a pinpointed target unimpeded, or use "designate X targets / designate X targets in an area" spells and abilities on it. A character can always pinpoint a *position* ("five meters ahead of me, three meters up").

Rough fix: knowing a target's position approximately. The character knows the general bearing but not the exact spot. For a human, hearing sustained speech and noise up close without seeing the speaker can yield a rough fix. A character may attack a roughly fixed target, but successes, AS, and any effect points all count at half, rounded down. The character may also choose a roughly fixed target for a designated-target spell, but the spell has only a half chance of working (though if the spell's area reads "area" or "every target in the area", it works as usual).

Unable to locate: the character has no idea where the target is — for a human, neither seeing nor hearing the enemy, with no data to analyze. The character may assume the target occupies some position and attack it; if the target happens to be there, resolve as a rough fix. But the character cannot designate it as a spell's target (though spells whose area reads "area" or "every target in the area" work as usual).

Unless stated otherwise, human-based characters can only pinpoint by sight.

A blind character cannot locate by sight. A deaf character cannot locate by hearing.

**Detection versus anti-detection:**

The contest runs in three stages:

Stage one — establish the anti-detecting side's anti-detection capability:

In the usual sense, anti-detection means the detecting side cannot detect the target — colloquially, the anti-detecting side has gone "invisible".

Whatever renders them "invisible" is their anti-detection means.

Stage one first establishes whether the anti-detecting side has any anti-detection means at all.

That is, if every target in the environment lacks anti-detection, everyone stands exposed in everyone's sight, and the detection-contest rules are unnecessary.

If a character does have anti-detection, establish its Category and Tier, then proceed to stage two.

Stage two — establish the detecting side's detection capability:

Here we enumerate all the detecting side's detection means,

then compare each against the target's anti-detection. The result decides whether stage three follows:

If a detection means outranks the anti-detection in Tier, the anti-detecting side is pinpointed outright.

If detection and anti-detection Tiers are equal, the anti-detecting side is roughly fixed outright.

If the detection means underranks the anti-detection, the anti-detection holds and the detecting side cannot locate them.

If, at this stage, the detecting side has not pinpointed the anti-detecting side, proceed to stage three.

Stage three — the skill contest:

Once per round, initiated by the detecting side:

the detecting side rolls PCT + Investigation (Scouting) against the anti-detecting side's AGI + Stealth (Hiding).

If the detecting side wins: having roughly fixed the target in stage two, they may now pinpoint them; having failed to locate in stage two, they may now take a rough fix.

Note that the contest is shaped by the following, all stacking:

While the anti-detecting side cannot be located, their "invisible" state grants all their Stealth-related checks a 10 DP Environmental Bonus.

Noisy surroundings: the detecting side takes a 2 DP Environmental Penalty. (No hearing, no penalty.)

The anti-detecting side attacked, cast, or did something similar last round: the detecting side gains a 2 DP Environmental Bonus. (No hearing, no bonus.)

The anti-detecting side made sound last round (speaking, say): 2 DP Environmental Bonus for the detector. (No hearing, no bonus.)

The anti-detecting side gives off an unusual smell: 2 DP Environmental Bonus for the detector. (No smell, no bonus.)

The anti-detecting side moved more than half its speed last round: 2 DP Environmental Bonus for the detector.

The anti-detecting side is inside the detector's rough range: 1 DP Environmental Bonus.

The anti-detecting side is inside the detector's sensory range: 1 DP Environmental Bonus.

Special: the ordinary person's naked eye counts as a Natural-Category, No-Tier detection means.
