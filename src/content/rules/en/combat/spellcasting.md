# Spellcasting

**Spell overview**

"Spell" is the umbrella name for a class of abilities, marked by the following:

A spell is an ability that produces a fixed magical effect on a one-shot basis; a spell's user is called a caster.

A caster can only cast spells they have learned. A spell is a single ability, used through "casting", producing effects from instantaneous to permanent.

A caster need not personally share the traits of a spell's effect — which has rules consequences. A fire mage, for instance, needn't be a fire-type creature, and doesn't necessarily hold fire abilities when not casting. He receives neither the buffs a fire-caster enhancement grants against fire powers or fire creatures, nor the matching drawbacks — unless that enhancement says otherwise.

A spell is, in a sense, like a specific tool or piece of software built to achieve one specific effect. Generally, a caster can hardly add or strip parts of it on the fly.

"Spell" is shorthand for everything casters use; it breaks down into many skills and abilities in detail.

==================================

**Caster roles — general outline**

Casters divide at the top level into four wholly independent caster-role systems: arcane casters, ninjutsu casters, daoist casters, and divine casters.

If a resource granting a caster role doesn't specify, it defaults to the arcane caster role.

The four systems stand independent of one another, and their arcana, ninjutsu, daoist arts, and divine arts do not interchange.

Every resource that grants casting ability is its own independent caster role — even when two grant the same spell, they are different caster roles, and spells do not pass between them. When you gain an exclusive spell, it belongs by default to the role that granted the casting ability; when you gain a generic spell, you must assign it to a suitable caster role you currently hold.

==================================

**Effects and limits of caster roles**

A caster-role enhancement bundles everything needed to cast a system's spells — the capability itself and the knowledge to research spells. It is generally a set of Fantasy-category cultivation. Buying a caster role at some rank grants:

1. The role's self-enhancement benefits (each role's page describes them), along with the right to buy the spells under that role.

2. The ability to research spell constructions yourself — you may self-teach spells within your role; see the "self-taught skill rules".

3. More effective energy combination and more efficient use of Energy Pools: when casting a spell you may be able to Amplify it, and you may pursue expansion research or uptiering research on spells.

You also accept these limits:

1. You may only buy, learn, and use exclusive and generic spells of your own caster system at or below your caster rank; you cannot buy or self-teach other systems' exclusive spells.

2. If the role carries abilities enhancing spellcasting — added spell power, reduced costs, and so on — they boost only spells under that same role.

==================================

**Caster-role ranks**

A caster role's rank equals the highest rank among the abilities that grant you the casting capability itself —

mage Titles, magician Titles, Tomes, certain casting-granting Bloodlines and Alternations, and the like.

Caster-role ranks run D 1, C 2, B 3, A 4, S 5.

==================================

**Role classifications**

Arcane casters

The most traditional mages — casters who wield mysterious power gained through spell research or other means.

The arcane caster's default casting check is INT + Occult + power value ± relevant adjustments.

The arcane casting cap is INT + Occult skill level + power value — even if your final roll exceeds this cap, only successes up to the cap count.

Daoist casters

The daoshi as commonly understood — the ritualists and magicians of the Chinese tradition — casters who release mysterious power through daoist arts.

The daoist caster's default casting check is DMR + Occult + power value ± relevant adjustments.

The daoist casting cap is DMR + Occult skill level + power value — successes beyond the cap do not count.

Ninjutsu casters

The peculiar casting of the Japanese ninja, releasing strange mysterious power through hand seals and the like — most familiar from manga and anime.

The ninjutsu caster's default casting check is PCT + Occult + power value ± relevant adjustments.

The ninjutsu casting cap is PCT + Occult skill level + power value — successes beyond the cap do not count.

Divine casters

Casting that draws power from prayer and faith — its practitioners commonly called priests. Casters with this role generally have a faith orientation.

The divine caster's default casting check is PCT + Occult + power value ± relevant adjustments.

The divine casting cap is PCT + Occult skill level + power value — successes beyond the cap do not count.

==================================

**Spells in detail**

A spell's power value is generally 2 at D-Tier, 4 at C, 6 at B, 10 at A, 16 at S.

A spell's energy cost is generally D 1, C 3, B 5, A 7, S 9.

Zero-cost spells: a cost of 0 doesn't mean the spell needs no energy — only that its draw is trivial next to the caster's recovery, or that ambient free energy suffices.

Some spells let the user invest extra XP for extra effects, generally called "research developments".

Some spells' effects depend on spell rank; such spells usually carry their own rank notes — if not, judge rank as D 1, C 2, B 3, A 4, S 5.

Double-Tier spells count at the single-Tier rank: a BB-priced spell counts as B-rank.

==================================

**Amplifying spells**

Many spells allow the character to Amplify them.

Amplification covers the Amplify cost, the Amplify effect, and the Amplify cap, each generally described in the spell's Amplify entry.

Amplification strengthens the spell's cast effect.

Some abilities strengthen amplification, e.g.:

"If the ability can Amplify, it counts as already Amplified X points (times)": treat it as pre-Amplified; if the cap isn't reached you may keep Amplifying, but the cap doesn't change, and further Amplification builds on the already-Amplified effect. (If the 1st Amplify costs 1 mana and the 2nd costs 2, being pre-Amplified once means your next Amplify costs 2.)

"If the ability can Amplify, the Amplify cost drops X points": Amplify as normal, with X of the extra cost waived.

"If the ability can Amplify, the Amplify cap rises X points (times)": the cap rises by X points — or possibly X uses.

Some abilities cost more than 1 energy per Amplify step. E.g.: a character counts as pre-Amplified 3 points, and each of their Amplify steps costs 2 mana — the first step is then free, and the second costs 1.

==================================

**Uptiering spells**

Uptier a spell to raise its rank (D–C–B–A–S), one rank per uptiering; afterward the spell's cost and power follow the new rank. Unless stated otherwise, the uptiered spell counts at its raised rank when judging spell rank. You may only uptier a spell up to the maximum rank of the caster role it belongs to.

Uptiering costs XP equal to (the spell's current rank: D 1, C 3, B 9, A 27) × 8 XP.

Unless stated otherwise, every spell can be uptiered.

==================================

**Casting components**

Spells demand particular procedures — chanted incantations, set gestures, casting materials: the necessary conditions of the spell formula, rituals to rouse one's inner will, or means of drawing some great being's attention.

Verbal: unable to speak clearly, you cannot cast spells with verbal components.

Book: spells with a book component are generally Tome spells; casting them requires holding the Tome that records the spell, or the cast is impossible.

Material: some spells consume materials. Unless stated otherwise, a D-rank cast takes 10 points of materials, C 20, B 50, A 100, S 200. Casting materials generally take no encumbrance; producing them costs no action, being part of the casting action, and needs no free hand — even for several materials. But without the materials, the spell cannot be cast. Generally only Lord God-provided materials qualify, unless the character can plausibly obtain them inside a horror film and the Lord God recognizes it.

Somatic: unable to move precisely, you cannot cast spells with somatic components. This covers:

1. No casting hand (at least 1 free hand).
E.g.: weapon in one hand, shield in the other — no casting. A two-handed weapon is exempt: you may cast holding it in one hand.
E.g.: losing hand form — transformed into tentacles, hooves, claws, and so on.
Special: holding a book does not occupy a hand — you may cast with a book in one hand and a weapon in the other.

2. The caster's casting check takes a Machinery Penalty of (worn armor's Armor Penalty × 2) DP.

==================================

**Suppression effects**

When a caster falls under a suppression force forbidding some effect, then — provided the suppression doesn't state that it works on spells, and isn't power at the level of world laws, the Three Thousand Great Daos, or a deity's will — the caster may still attempt to cast a spell producing that effect. Their casting check (required now even for spells that normally need none) takes a penalty equal to (the number of D Tiers contained in the suppression's rank) × 2. If that drops their casting DP to 0 or below, the spell cannot be cast. And even if it is cast, its effect holds only for an instant.

\*Suppression forces include restrictions like "no fire effects may be produced near an Elder Ice Elemental". Purely natural constraints — trying a fire spell underwater, say — count as Tier-0 suppression (unable to stop the casting, but able to stop the spell from persisting). Where physical conditions impose the limit, the spell halts at the limit: a sealed room cannot hold Create Water's outpouring, so the water stops when the space fills (or when air pressure balances water pressure, if the ST cares to honor physics).

==================================

**Interrupting casting**

Casting in unstable circumstances can be interrupted, calling for checks to maintain the cast:

1. Interrupted by special abilities: another character's "counterspell" or similar may break your casting; the ability's entry gives details.

2. Taking heavy damage: if someone with an out-of-turn damage ability (a Prepare Action, say) damages the caster mid-cast, the caster makes a Fortitude check at DC equal to the damage taken; failing the save breaks the cast.

3. Losing bodily control: if someone with an out-of-turn body-control ability (a prepared grapple, say) puts you airborne, prone, grappled, or ridden mid-cast, make an EDR check at DC equal to (the cast spell's rank + 1) squared; success lets the cast proceed as normal.

If a cast is broken, the action, energy, and materials spent are not refunded — all lost as normal.

Note: every ability with a long casting time falls under this rule.

==================================

**Spell Professions**

Every spell has its Occult Profession; without the matching Profession, it cannot be cast.

Divination: these spells generally reveal intelligence or information.

Conjuration: these spells are various, spanning many characteristics.

Evocation: these spells generally involve the elements — plain, blunt damage as a rule.

Necromancy: these spells generally involve the undead, or the harming of other creatures.

Illusion: these spells create false objects or capabilities; every spell with this Profession counts as an "illusion".

Warding: these spells raise wards for protection, isolation, or other ends; every magical barrier they create is called a "ward".

Abjuration: these spells affect a target and strengthen its protections — generally a status.

Transmutation: these spells affect a target and change it — generally a status.

Creation: these spells generally create a new object, a new capability, or something conceptually "new" in some other way.

Summoning: these spells generally call up an existing object or creature, or borrow part of their power.

Curse: these spells affect a target and weaken its capabilities — generally a status; every spell with this Profession is a "curse".

Mind: these spells affect the target's mind.

Enchantment: these spells affect the state of creatures or items — akin to Transmutation — and may create certain objects — akin to Creation; it sits between the two.
