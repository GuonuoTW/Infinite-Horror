# Tiers and Resisting

**Ability Tiers:**

Many effects in the Resources section compare ability Tiers, so ability Tiers are defined explicitly here:

Abilities have 6 Tiers: No Tier, D, C, B, A, S. Double Tiers equal the single Tier. Abilities above S all count as S — no double-S or higher exists.

Whatever an ability calls itself internally, whenever Tiers are compared between abilities or effects, its Tier governs. Unless stated otherwise, that is the Tier of its purchase price.

Abilities from Bloodlines, Alternations, and Titles have their Tier fixed at purchase; unless stated otherwise they never auto-upgrade or downgrade.

Example: if a Bloodline grants toxin immunity at its C rank, and its higher ranks grant no stronger version, that is a C-Tier toxin immunity.

Spells and similar effects use their own Tier; if two spells tie in Tier, the higher caster rank wins.

A character without the matching immunity is still affected by an ability even when its Tier is low.

Example: a winning feint still strips defense from an A-Tier character who lacks the relevant immunity.

Unless stated otherwise, an effect's Tier is the higher of the ability's Tier when cast and when acquired.

E.g.: a spell bought at C can inflict C-Tier Negative Status points; once uptiered, the points it inflicts rise automatically to B-Tier.

**Immunities granted by ability Tiers:**

Immunity: an immunity can only block abilities whose source Tier is at or below its own.

E.g. 1: C-Tier mind immunity —

it blocks No-Tier, D, and C mind effects, but not B or higher.

E.g. 2: D-Tier mind immunity —

mind immunity also covers abnormal-emotion points, so against an A-Tier fear-point ability, the fear points still land: the source outranks the immunity.

**Gaining an immunity twice**

When a character gains flat-footed / touch-attack / mind-effect immunity from any source while already holding a matching immunity of equal or lower Tier, they receive, in compensation, a Perfect Bonus on the matching Save — growth of nerve, body, or will.

The sources must be entirely different — a Bloodline, a Title, a skill. When a character replaces a prior immunity with an equal- or higher-Tier flat-footed / touch-attack / mind-effect immunity, they gain a Perfect Bonus on Reflex / Fortitude / Will Saves of (the replaced ability's Tier × 2) DP (D 1, C 2, B 3, A 4, S 5).

**On contradictory abilities:**

Abilities in direct and total contradiction resolve by Tier. At equal Tier the shield (the defender) prevails; a higher-Tier spear (the attacker) breaks a lower-Tier shield.

Some examples of the shield side:

flat-footed vs. flat-footed immunity — the immune side;

detection vs. anti-detection — the detecting side;

poisoning vs. poison immunity — the poison-resisting side;

and so on.

Contradictory abilities must be exactly opposed (inflicting flat-footedness / flat-footed immunity, invisibility / anti-invisibility, ignoring armor defense / immunity to weapon Breach, and the like).

Abilities not fully opposed — a weapon's Breach against armor's anti-Breach, say — are excluded, since a weapon's Breach targets not just the anti-Breach but the armor's own defense and Born Defense too (not fully opposed).

Whatever Talents, Defects, and Quirks grant counts as No-Tier.

Effects arising from the basic battle rules, unless stated otherwise, count as No-Tier — flat-footedness from a surprise attack, say.

Abilities costing only XP with no Tier convert their XP cost through the self-created-skill scale to derive a Tier (Feats, samsara-team Feats, and certain special enhancements).

E.g.: player Lu Ren enhanced the Title "Mage Killer" at C-Tier, gaining C-Tier flat-footed immunity. Facing a C-Tier Zerg Alternation "Barbed Tongue", he is never flat-footed; facing a B-Tier "Nanaya Body Technique", he still is.

**Immunities that never contest Tiers — list and notes:**

**The following abilities have no exactly opposed counterpart:**

Damage immunity: usually immunity to one damage type — bludgeoning, slashing, piercing, solid-round, pure energy, fire, cold, lightning, acid, sound, radiant, Mental, Field, and so on.

In particular cases an ability may break through an immunity — always specially noted; if several such abilities target the same damage, their effects don't stack: take the higher.

Grapple immunity: you are never grappled; grapple checks against you automatically fail.

Trip immunity: you are never tripped; trip checks automatically fail.

Slam immunity: you are never slammed; slam checks automatically fail.

Called-shot immunity: you have no vulnerable spots; the attacker still takes the called-shot penalty, but you take no called-shot effect.

Disarm immunity: your held weapons cannot be struck loose.

Breach immunity: Breach does nothing to you.

Dispelling immunity: Dispelling does nothing to you.

Falling-damage immunity: you take no falling damage.

Difficult-terrain immunity: difficult terrain doesn't hinder you.

Environmental-effect immunity: you are immune only to the environment's environmental effects — its bonuses, penalties, damage and all.

Immunity to XX environment: you are immune to everything that environment does (e.g. space combat: immune to the effects of space).

8+ Dice immunity: enemy "8+ Dice" doesn't work against you; if the attacker has 8+ Dice and you lack 9+ Dice immunity, you count as having 9+ Dice immunity in that moment.

9+ Dice immunity: enemy "9+ Dice" doesn't work against you.

Revival ban: cannot be revived.

Emotion-point immunity: usually immunity to one emotion point type — dejection, mania, fear.

Negative-point immunity: usually immunity to one Negative Status point type — burn, freeze, agony, nausea, tinnitus, dazzle, crystallization, mental-bind, entanglement, paralysis, stun, charm, bleeding, fatigue, stall, drowsiness, limb impairment. Unless stated otherwise, the immunity means you never gain those points at all.

Multiple-attack-penalty immunity: repeated attacks impose no multiple-attack penalty on you.

Negative-status immunity: usually immunity to one Negative Status — frozen solid, queasiness, petrification, infatuation, exhaustion, blindness, deafness, sleep. Holding these does not make you immune to the matching status points; the points simply cannot tip you into the state.

Aging immunity (limitless lifespan): owing to legacy issues, the book currently splits limitless lifespan into D-Tier and A-Tier traits. The D-Tier reads: immune to natural aging, with limitless lifespan. The A-Tier reads: immune to all aging and age-affecting effects — though abilities powered by deliberately spending years or aging yourself bypass this immunity, aging you as normal. Report nonconforming traits to the rulebook author for errata.

High Speed immunity: High Speed does nothing to you.

**Immunities that do contest Tiers — list and notes:**

**When the following meet an exactly opposed ability of higher Tier, resolve by comparing both sides' Tiers.**

Invisibility: the character holds anti-detection.

Shapeshift immunity: the character is untouched by anything altering form or creature type.

Possession immunity: the character cannot be possessed.

Immunity to effects from a given Energy Pool: effects powered by qualifying pools (a named pool, or Mental-category pools, say) have no effect on you whatsoever.

Instant-death immunity: instant-death effects don't affect you.

Suffocation immunity: suffocation doesn't affect you; creatures without respiratory systems are automatically immune.

Silence immunity: silence doesn't affect you.

Sealing immunity: you cannot be sealed — Energy Pools included, held items excluded.

Imprisonment immunity: you cannot be imprisoned.

Banishment immunity: you cannot be banished.

Disease immunity: you contract no diseases.

Toxin immunity: you are never poisoned and take no Poison damage.

Mind-effect immunity: generally covers emotion points, Morale Bonuses and Penalties, Mental damage, and charm, control, infatuation, coercion, and other mind effects.

Illusion immunity: illusions don't affect you.

Flat-footed immunity: you are never flat-footed.

Touch-attack immunity: you ignore touch attacks against you.

Curse immunity: curses don't affect you — including spells of the "Curse" casting Profession.

Time immunity: time-related abilities don't affect you.

Space immunity: space- and teleport-related abilities don't affect you.

Causality immunity: causality-related abilities don't affect you.

Forced attack: you must attack.

Unattackable: you cannot be attacked.
